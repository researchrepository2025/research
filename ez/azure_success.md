# Microsoft Azure AI Platform - Success Metrics
## Date Range: August 1, 2025 - January 13, 2026

### Executive Summary

Microsoft Azure AI building platform demonstrated exceptional growth from August 1, 2025 to January 13, 2026, with total AI business reaching a $13 billion annual revenue run rate, up 175% year-over-year. Azure and other cloud services revenue grew 40% in Q1 FY26 (ended September 30, 2025), driven by strong demand for AI workloads. Microsoft Cloud revenue surpassed $49 billion for the quarter, up 26% year-over-year. Azure OpenAI Service now serves 230,000 organizations with 95% Fortune 500 adoption, processing over 100 trillion tokens quarterly. Microsoft 365 Copilot reached over 90% Fortune 500 adoption, while the broader Copilot family surpassed 150 million monthly active users. Infrastructure investments totaled $80 billion through 2028, with AI capacity increasing over 80% in FY26.

## Financial Performance

### Overall AI Business Revenue

Microsoft's AI business reached an annual run rate of $13 billion as of January 2025, representing 175% year-over-year growth. CEO Satya Nadella stated during the Q2 FY25 earnings call: "the massive opportunity ahead" for customers using AI and highlighted that "the company's AI business has an annual run rate of $13 billion, up 175 percent year-over-year."

Source: [GeekWire, January 2025](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/microsoft-earnings-2/)

Microsoft's AI business exceeded the $10 billion run rate previously forecasted, with the company "generating revenue from its artificial intelligence products and services at a rate of $13 billion annually."

Source: [Fortune, January 30, 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/01/30/microsofts-ai-business-has-topped-an-annual-revenue-run-rate-of-13-billion/)

### Azure Cloud Services Performance

Azure and other cloud services revenue increased 40% (39% in constant currency) in Q1 FY26 (quarter ended September 30, 2025). CFO Amy Hood noted: "In Azure and other cloud services...revenue grew 40% and 39% in constant currency. Results were ahead of expectations."

Source: [Microsoft Investor Relations FY26 Q1](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/earnings/fy-2026-q1/press-release-webcast)

Microsoft Cloud revenue surpassed $49 billion in Q1 FY26, up 26% year-over-year (25% in constant currency). CEO Satya Nadella stated: "Microsoft Cloud revenue surpassed $49 billion, up 26% year over year."

Source: [Microsoft FY26 Q1 Earnings Call](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q1)

Azure reached $75 billion in annual revenue with 34% growth for fiscal year 2025. "Azure revenue exceeded $75 billion with a 34% increase, demonstrating increased customer trust in Microsoft's ecosystem."

Source: [GeekWire](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/microsoft-posts-strong-quarter-cites-broad-ai-and-cloud-growth-as-azure-revenue-tops-75b-annually/)

### Azure AI-Specific Revenue

Azure AI services revenue run rate reached approximately $26 billion as of December 2025. "Microsoft's Azure AI revenue reached an annual run rate of approximately $26 billion."

Source: [FinancialContent, December 25, 2025](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2025-12-25-the-silicon-fortress-microsofts-azure-ai-expansion-solidifies-its-dominance-in-the-enterprise-cloud-war)

Azure AI services alone grew 42% in 2025, "driven by demand for large language models and cognitive APIs."

Source: [How Is Microsoft's AI Strategy Driving Growth?, AI Magazine](https://aimagazine.com/news/microsofts-new-growth-era-inside-satya-nadellas-ai-vision)

### GitHub Copilot Revenue

GitHub Copilot reached $2 billion in annual recurring revenue. "GitHub Copilot reached $2 billion in annual recurring revenue, marking a significant milestone."

Source: [Mind the Product](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/what-git-hub-copilots-2-b-run-taught-us-about-how-ai-is-rewriting-the-product-led-growth-playbook/)

Satya Nadella noted that "GitHub Copilot has become a larger business than all of GitHub was when Microsoft acquired the platform for $7.5 billion in 2018."

Source: [Business of Apps](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/microsoft-copilot-statistics/)

### Commercial Performance

Commercial bookings increased 112% and 111% in constant currency in Q1 FY26. CFO Amy Hood stated: "Commercial bookings increased 112% and 111% in constant currency and were significantly ahead of expectations."

Source: [Microsoft FY26 Q1 Earnings Call](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q1)

Commercial remaining performance obligation (RPO) increased 51% to $392 billion, indicating strong future revenue commitments. This increase was "due in part to new Azure commitments from OpenAI."

Source: [Microsoft Investor Relations FY26 Q1](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/earnings/fy-2026-q1/press-release-webcast)

### Quarterly Revenue and Profit

Microsoft reported revenue of $77.7 billion for Q1 FY26, up 18% (17% in constant currency). Operating income reached $38.0 billion, up 24%.

Source: [Microsoft Investor Relations FY26 Q1](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/earnings/fy-2026-q1/press-release-webcast)

For Q2 FY25 (ended December 31, 2024), Microsoft reported $69.6 billion in revenue, up 12%, "with earnings of $3.23/share, topping Wall Street's expectations by both measures."

Source: [GeekWire, January 2025](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/microsoft-earnings-2/)

### AI Capacity Expansion

Microsoft expects to increase its AI capacity by more than 80% throughout fiscal year 2026. CEO Satya Nadella stated: "We will increase our total AI capacity by over 80% this year, and roughly double our total datacenter footprint over the next two years."

Source: [Microsoft FY26 Q1 Earnings Call](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q1)

### Data Center Investments

Source: [DataCenters.com](https://www.datacenters.com/news/microsoft-s-80b-investment-in-ai-data-centers-the-digital-backbone-for-a-multimodal-world)

### Capital Expenditures

Source: [GeekWire, January 2025](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/microsoft-earnings-2/)
