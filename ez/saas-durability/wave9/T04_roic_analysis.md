# T04: ROIC and Capital Efficiency Analysis — SaaS vs. Custom-Built Enterprise Software in the AI Era

**Research Question:** What does Return on Investment and Capital Efficiency analysis show about SaaS vs. custom-built enterprise software in the context of AI-driven applications? Specifically: what are the real-world ROIC differentials between buying SaaS vs. building custom? What does capital efficiency data show? How does agentic coding change the build economics?

**Wave:** 9 | **Document:** T04 | **Date Compiled:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The build-vs-buy decision in enterprise software has never carried higher capital stakes. Global software spend crossed $1 trillion for the first time in 2024 and is forecast to reach $1.4 trillion in 2026. ([1]) SaaS vendor pricing is now inflating at 12.2% annually — more than 4x the consumer price index. ([2]) Meanwhile, empirical data on large custom software projects remains deeply unfavorable: projects run 45% over budget and deliver 56% less value than predicted. ([3]) Yet the build option is being structurally reshaped by agentic AI coding tools that compress development timelines and reduce per-unit capital requirements. ([4])

This document is a raw factual record — statistics, exact quotes from named analysts and executives, and sourced data — across six dimensions: (1) ROIC and WACC benchmarks for software investments by sector, (2) capital efficiency metrics used by CFOs, (3) payback period data for SaaS procurement and custom builds, (4) agentic coding's quantified effect on build economics, (5) cost of capital and IRR hurdle context, and (6) structural conditions under which each approach can outperform on returns. No unverified tables or calculations without named primary sources appear in this document.
