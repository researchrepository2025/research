## Overall Assessment

### Strengths
1. **Citation discipline is consistently strong across all files.** Every significant claim has an inline URL and date. This is rare in research of this scope.
2. **Expert attribution is precise.** Names, titles, companies, and event contexts are correctly provided throughout. No fabricated attributions were detected.
3. **Date range compliance is good.** Nearly all sources fall within 2025-2026. The handful of 2024 sources provide legitimate context for products launched in the target period.
4. **Analytical balance is present in the strongest files (S01, S03, S06).** These files present vendor claims alongside market skepticism, stock declines, and counterarguments.
5. **S06 is the standout file** for analytical rigor, scope discipline, and source quality. It should be the model for any future rewrites.

### Gaps
1. **Oracle and SAP are absent from S01.** The file covers "major vendor responses" but limits scope to Salesforce, ServiceNow, and Workday. SAP (Joule) and Oracle (AI Agent Studio) are mentioned in passing in S04 but receive no dedicated vendor-perspective analysis.
2. **No independent customer validation.** All vendor adoption metrics (Agentforce deals, Now Assist ACV, Illuminate adoption) are vendor-reported. No file includes independent survey data validating these claims (e.g., Gartner Peer Insights satisfaction scores, TrustRadius reviews, independent NPS data).
3. **No failure case studies.** The files document vendors claiming success but do not include examples of failed AI feature deployments, abandoned agent rollouts, or customer churn driven by AI pricing changes. The Gartner "40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled" stat (S05) is the closest to a failure data point but is a prediction, not a case study.
4. **Open-source and self-hosted alternatives are unaddressed.** The "build" threat is framed exclusively as enterprises building custom software. Open-source SaaS alternatives (Odoo, ERPNext, Mattermost, etc.) enhanced by AI are not discussed in any file.

### Verdict
Five files PASS; two have MINOR_ISSUES (S04 for scope creep and Tier 3 sources, S05 for source tier concentration and pro-moat bias). No file requires full rework. The primary remediation priorities are: (1) deduplicating verbatim Bain/Gartner quotes across files, (2) reconciling Salesforce metric inconsistencies with reporting-period annotations, and (3) strengthening S05's source profile by replacing blog citations with Tier 1-2 equivalents where available.
