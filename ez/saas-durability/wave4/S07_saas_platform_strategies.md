# S07: SaaS Platform Strategies — Co-opting the Build Impulse

**Research Wave:** 4
**Topic:** How SaaS Vendors Use Platform and Extensibility Strategies to Neutralize the Agentic Coding Threat
**Audience:** C-Suite
**Date:** March 2026
**Word Count:** ~2,400

---

## Executive Summary

The agentic coding era has forced a strategic reckoning inside every major SaaS boardroom. Rather than cede ground to the "build your own" impulse now enabled by AI coding tools, the largest enterprise SaaS vendors — Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday, and Microsoft — have executed a coordinated pivot: transform their products from closed applications into extensible platforms, then invite customers and partners to build on top. The bet is that if enterprises can satisfy their customization needs *within* the SaaS ecosystem, they will not exit it.

The data suggest this strategy is gaining traction but not yet decisive. Salesforce's Agentforce and Data 360 combined ARR reached nearly $1.4 billion and grew 114% year-over-year. ServiceNow's Creator Workflows and App Engine now account for 22% of $13.28 billion in 2025 annual revenue. Microsoft Power Platform crossed 56 million monthly active users. Simultaneously, Retool's February 2026 survey of 817 builders found that 35% of enterprises have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom build — a signal that the platform strategy is buying time, not a permanent solution.

## Key Takeaways (Facts Only — No Analysis)

1. All four major SaaS platform vendors (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday, Microsoft) launched or materially expanded developer extensibility platforms in 2025 — each explicitly targeting the "build your own" impulse as a retention strategy.

2. Salesforce Agentforce ARR: >$500M (330% YoY growth, Q3 FY26). ServiceNow Creator Workflows: 22% of $13.28B 2025 revenue. Microsoft Power Platform: 56M monthly active users (27% YoY growth). All three metrics show accelerating platform adoption.

3. Retool's 817-builder survey (late 2025) found 35% have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with custom builds. 78% expect to build more in 2026. 60% built outside IT oversight in the past year.

4. Gartner (August 2025): 40% of enterprise apps will feature task-specific AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% in 2025.

5. Gartner (per Deloitte, November 2025): By 2030, 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed into larger agent ecosystems.

6. Enterprise barriers to pure custom builds remain: 42% lack engineering bandwidth; 41% face security/compliance obstacles; 26% cite ongoing maintenance burden.

7. AgentExchange (Salesforce) launched March 2025 with 200+ partners; grew to 114 listed solutions by September 2025. AppExchange: 90%+ of Salesforce customers use at least one partner-built app.

8. Workday's platform governance shift (September 2025) reversed its historically restrictive "exhaustive certification and approvals program" — the company's most significant architectural opening since its founding.
