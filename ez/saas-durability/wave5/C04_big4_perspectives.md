# C04: Big 4 Advisory Firms on Enterprise SaaS Durability

**Research Wave:** 5
**Scope:** Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, EY — 2025–2026 sources only
**Audience:** C-suite
**Prepared:** 2026-03-06

---

## Executive Summary

Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, and EY each publish annual technology outlook reports that reach tens of thousands of enterprise executives. Across all four firms in 2025–2026, a consistent structural thesis has emerged: agentic AI will erode — and in some scenarios replace — traditional SaaS as the dominant enterprise software delivery model. Deloitte projects AI agent-powered solutions will represent 60% of the total addressable software market by 2030. Accenture reports 77% of executives agree AI agents will "reinvent how their organization builds digital systems." EY explicitly calls for SaaS companies to reorganize as "AI-first" enterprises. PwC documents CIO concern that expanding SaaS portfolios "inflate the tech stack" rather than drive efficiency.

A critical structural caveat applies to all four: each firm derives substantial revenue from implementing and integrating enterprise SaaS. Their advisory positions on SaaS durability must be read against this commercial interest. None of the four firms publishes a bearish position on SaaS as a category; all frame AI disruption as transformation requiring advisory engagement, not abandonment requiring divestiture.

## Common Themes Across All Four Firms

[FACT]
All four firms (Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, EY) published 2025 or 2026 reports treating agentic AI as the primary structural force reshaping enterprise software, with traditional SaaS positioned as transitional rather than terminal.
— Deloitte: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/software-industry-outlook.html | Accenture: https://www.accenture.com/us-en/insights/technology/technology-trends-2025 | PwC: https://www.pwc.com/us/en/industries/tmt/library/tech-cio-priorities.html | EY: https://www.ey.com/en_us/insights/tech-sector/ai-is-transforming-saas-landscape
Date: 2025–2026

[FACT]
Deloitte projects agentic AI market at $8.5 billion in 2026 rising to $35 billion by 2030 with 75% of enterprises investing in agentic AI by end of 2026; Accenture reports 77% of executives say AI agents will reinvent how digital systems are built; PwC documents 56% of CIOs prioritizing architecture future-proofing; EY calls for full SaaS R&D reorganization to AI-first structures.
— Multiple sources as cited above
Date: 2025–2026

[FACT]
None of the four firms publishes a recommendation to reduce or exit enterprise SaaS investments. All frame disruption as requiring managed transformation engagements.
— Observation across all four firms' 2025–2026 advisory publications
Date: 2025–2026

## Key Takeaways

[FACT]
Deloitte is the most explicit of the four firms in naming SaaS displacement risk, stating: "Agentic AI could even begin to replace today's SaaS tools over time" and projecting AI agent solutions at 60% of the TAM by 2030.
— Deloitte 2026 Software Industry Outlook
URL: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/software-industry-outlook.html
Date: 2026

[FACT]
Accenture is the most survey-reliant of the four, anchoring all major claims to executive sentiment data (4,000+ respondents) rather than market financial data — making its conclusions dependent on respondent self-reporting accuracy.
— Accenture Technology Vision 2025
URL: https://www.accenture.com/us-en/insights/technology/technology-trends-2025
Date: 2025

[FACT]
PwC is the only firm among the four to explicitly name SaaS portfolio expansion as a cost risk ("expanding SaaS portfolios and AI platforms can inflate the tech stack"), framing SaaS sprawl as a governance and FinOps problem rather than a technology-durability problem.
— PwC Tech CIO Priorities 2025
URL: https://www.pwc.com/us/en/industries/tmt/library/tech-cio-priorities.html
Date: July 22, 2025

[FACT]
EY is the only firm among the four to publish named-author analysis (Michael Stricklen, Managing Director, EY-Parthenon) on SaaS transformation, giving its claims higher attribution specificity than the other three firms' anonymously authored reports.
— EY-Parthenon, "AI is transforming SaaS landscape"
