# C03: BCG Perspectives on SaaS Durability and Enterprise Software Strategy in the Agentic Era

**Research Wave:** 5 — C03
**Audience:** C-Suite
**Sources:** BCG.com publications, BCG Henderson Institute, BCG/MIT Sloan co-publications, 2025–2026 (one 2024 source marked [PRE-2025])
**Compiled:** March 6, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

BCG's 2025–2026 research corpus does not publish a "SaaS is dying" thesis. Instead, the firm documents structural pressure on seat-based SaaS licensing models — concentrated in the pricing and monetization layer rather than SaaS existence as a category — while simultaneously showing SaaS spending growing from 13% to 21% of enterprise IT budgets over five years. BCG's most direct advisory position is that enterprises should pursue a hybrid technology portfolio, combining standalone SaaS, embedded agentic solutions within existing platforms, low-code agent builders, and custom-built agents for differentiating use cases; only 11% of firms rely primarily on in-house development. BCG's B2B software pricing research (August 2025) projects a shift away from seat-based subscriptions toward agent-based and outcome-based pricing, but characterizes this as a monetization transition rather than a market collapse, explicitly noting that most incumbents cannot abandon seat models without putting revenue at risk. Named BCG senior partners — including Global AI Leader Nicolas de Bellefonds, Technology Sector Leader Akash Bhatia, and BCG X North America Chair Sesh Iyer — collectively frame SaaS vendors as evolving participants in an agentic ecosystem, not casualties of it.

## Section 1: BCG's Foundational Framing of the Agentic Era and Enterprise Software

BCG's December 2025 article "How Agents Are Accelerating the Next Wave of AI Value Creation" (authors: Vinciane Beauchene, Nicolas de Bellefonds, Djon Kleine, Rich Lesser, Amanda Luther, Tom Martin, Daniel Sack) establishes the firm's framing of the technology transition:

[QUOTE]
"We are entering the era of AI agents. While previous waves of AI focused either on logic and optimization or creativity and synthesis — the 'left brain' and 'right brain' of predictive AI and generative AI, respectively — the next wave will involve agentic systems that take on entire workflows, applying judgment shaped by a company's own institutional knowledge."
— Beauchene, de Bellefonds, Kleine, Lesser, Luther, Martin, Sack
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

[FACT]
BCG defines agentic AI as the "frontal cortex" of AI — the executive function that converts outputs into business impact. "Predictive AI opened value in decision-making functions. Generative AI opened value in knowledge and content production functions. Agentic AI is now opening value in process-heavy functions where execution defines performance."
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

[QUOTE]
"Productivity gains are often the initial benefit of AI adoption, but the real prize in the agentic age is differentiation. And the real challenge is sustaining advantage when AI can enable new, disruptive competitors and shrink traditional advantages."
— Beauchene, de Bellefonds et al., BCG
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

BCG's Fortune op-ed (December 2025, authors: Francois Candelon, Amartya Das, Sesh Iyer, Shervin Khodabandeh, Sam Ransbotham) frames the discontinuity from prior technology waves:

[QUOTE]
"For the very first time, technology is now able to do work rather than merely assist humans."
— Candelon, Das, Iyer, Khodabandeh, Ransbotham
URL: https://fortune.com/2025/12/12/agentic-ai-leadership-redesigning-the-enterprise/

## Section 3: BCG's Core Advisory Position on Build vs. Buy in the Agentic Era

BCG's Widening AI Value Gap report (September 2025, survey of 1,250+ firms worldwide, authors: Jessica Apotheker, Vinciane Beauchene, Nicolas de Bellefonds, Patrick Forth, Marc Roman Franke, Michael Grebe, Nina Kataeva, Santeri Kirvela, Djon Kleine, Romain de Laubier, Vladimir Lukic, Amanda Luther, Mary Martin, Jeff Walters, and Christoph Schweizer) contains BCG's most detailed published framework on sourcing agentic capabilities.

### The Four-Option Portfolio Framework

[FACT]
BCG identifies four broad options for enterprises sourcing agentic capabilities:
1. **Standalone Agentic Solutions** — turnkey applications for narrow tasks (e.g., coding or research assistants)
2. **Agent Builder Platforms** — low-code or self-serve platforms that empower teams to build custom agents using modular components
3. **Custom-Built Agent Solutions** — tailor-made agents developed from scratch for differentiating use cases requiring bespoke logic, heavy orchestration, or strict performance controls
4. **Embedded Agentic Solutions** — capabilities integrated directly within major enterprise platforms like CRM or ERP, leveraging native data and governance
URL: https://media-publications.bcg.com/The-Widening-AI-Value-Gap-Sept-2025.pdf
Date: September 2025

[STATISTIC]
"Our research shows that only 11% of firms rely primarily on in-house development, and just 4% depend on a single, end-to-end vendor for their full AI stack. However, buying solutions everywhere without prioritization risks fragmentation and poor adoption."
— BCG Widening AI Value Gap
URL: https://media-publications.bcg.com/The-Widening-AI-Value-Gap-Sept-2025.pdf
Date: September 2025

[FACT]
BCG explicitly characterizes the hybrid portfolio as the dominant enterprise strategy: "Most companies find that a hybrid approach is the most effective strategy for balancing market solutions with internal customization."
URL: https://media-publications.bcg.com/The-Widening-AI-Value-Gap-Sept-2025.pdf
Date: September 2025

### The Shared AI Platform Imperative

[QUOTE]
"To scale, you cannot have every function building its own infrastructure. You need a shared AI platform that gives P&L owners 'freedom within a frame.' Think of it as an enterprise system that everyone builds on. The platform should include data connectors, orchestration frameworks, model management, monitoring, and tiered autonomy with responsible AI controls. These shared components let teams innovate faster without rebuilding the basics or compromising security."
— de Bellefonds, Beauchene, Kleine, Lesser, Luther, Martin, Sack, BCG
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"In our work with clients, we've observed that shared tools and data can cut costs by up to 30% and lift productivity by 25%. As reuse increases, time to market can improve by 50%."
— BCG
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"Future-built firms are three times as likely to operate a central, integrated AI platform as the backbone for their AI deployment."
— BCG Widening AI Value Gap
URL: https://media-publications.bcg.com/The-Widening-AI-Value-Gap-Sept-2025.pdf
Date: September 2025

[FACT]
BCG frames IT and business function partnership — not wholesale SaaS replacement — as the operating model: "IT maintains the chassis: They manage the data, infrastructure, and safeguards that ensure systems and AI agents are reliable, compliant, consistent, and up to date. The business drives the design: Functional leaders and teams use the platform to design agents that deliver outcomes."
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation
Date: December 15, 2025

## Section 4: BCG's Published Position on B2B SaaS Pricing Disruption

BCG's August 13, 2025 report "Rethinking B2B Software Pricing in the Agentic AI Era" is the firm's most direct analysis of SaaS business model durability. Authors: Naila Dharani, John Pineda, Angel Andeyro, Nate Hamming, Yerim Kim, Nipun Misra, and Akash Bhatia (Global Sector Leader, Technology, Silicon Valley).

### Seat-Based Model Under Pressure — Quantified

[STATISTIC]
"According to BCG's IT buyers survey, 40% of buyers cite seat reduction as their primary lever to decrease software spending — a shift that the adoption of agentic AI facilitates."
— Dharani, Pineda, Andeyro, Hamming, Kim, Misra, Bhatia, BCG
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"In BCG's IT buyers survey, 48% of respondents said they planned to increase their spending on AI and GenAI during the next 12 months."
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"A 2025 ICONIQ Capital survey highlights that 68% of vendors charge separately for AI enhancements or include these features exclusively within premium tiers."
— BCG, citing ICONIQ Capital survey
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"A recent Andreessen Horowitz survey highlighted significant barriers: 47% of buyers struggle to define clear, measurable outcomes; 36% worry about cost predictability; 25% face difficulty aligning on value attribution with vendors; and 24% acknowledge that outcomes often depend on factors outside of vendors' control."
— BCG, citing Andreessen Horowitz survey
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"One vendor of customer engagement software has experienced margin variance exceeding 70 percentage points across different customer accounts."
— BCG pricing report
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

### The Five Emerging Agentic Pricing Models

[FACT]
BCG identifies five emerging pricing models replacing seat-based SaaS, with tradeoffs:
1. **Usage-Based: Resources** — pay per resource/compute consumed; maximum flexibility but least predictable
2. **Agent-Based** — purchase individual AI agents via one-time fee or subscription; e.g., OpenAI rumored to price its PhD-level research agent at $20,000/month
3. **Usage-Based: Interactions** — charges per defined interaction, API call, or activity
4. **Outcome-Based: Jobs Completed** — payment after agent successfully executes a predefined job combining multiple tasks
5. **Outcome-Based: Financial Pricing** — payment only for specific financial results (e.g., revenue growth or cost reduction); BCG characterizes this as "the hardest to operationalize"
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[STATISTIC]
"In our survey of IT buyers, 91% expressed interest only in partially autonomous agents, suggesting that customers do not yet view agents as complete replacements for employees."
— BCG pricing report
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

### BCG's Forecast for SaaS Pricing Transition

[FACT]
BCG projects a hybrid transition rather than a clean break from SaaS: "Most incumbent software providers will not be able to transition entirely to these [agent-based and outcome-based] models without putting revenue at risk. In the near term, we anticipate broad adoption of hybrid approaches that blend established subscription structures with emerging agentic pricing models."
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[FACT]
BCG advises SaaS vendors to monitor leading indicators of seat compression: "Common indicators include greater agentic AI usage, purchases of fewer seat licenses, and workforce reductions within customer organizations or targeted industries."
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

[FACT]
BCG documents a three-step vendor transition playbook: (1) introduce agentic pricing in parallel with existing seat-based model; (2) develop tracking and telemetry capabilities to monetize agentic features transparently; (3) monitor leading indicators and progressively reduce seat-based revenue reliance as agentic adoption grows.
URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai
Date: August 13, 2025

## Section 10: BCG on the AI-Native Tech Stack — Architectural Implications for SaaS

## Key Takeaways

- **BCG does not publish a "SaaS is dying" thesis.** Its published position characterizes agentic AI as disrupting SaaS *pricing models* — specifically seat-based licensing — rather than eliminating SaaS as a category. BCG explicitly forecasts most incumbents will pursue hybrid pricing transitions, not clean breaks, because abandoning seat models risks revenue.

- **BCG's empirical surveys show SaaS spending growing in absolute terms** — from 13% to 21% of enterprise IT budgets over five years — even as specific legacy categories (standalone ERP, standalone analytics) face deliberate budget reallocation toward AI, cloud, and security. ERP and CRM are explicitly described as regaining momentum as AI foundations.

- **BCG's evidence strongly favors hybrid sourcing over full build:** only 11% of firms rely primarily on in-house AI development; only 4% use a single end-to-end vendor. The firm's advisory recommendation is a curated four-option portfolio spanning commercial SaaS platforms, embedded agentic modules, low-code builders, and custom agents for differentiating use cases.

- **BCG identifies two structural risks to the SaaS market, both quantified:** seat compression (40% of IT buyers cite seat reduction as their primary cost lever) and vendor satisfaction stratification (multipurpose service providers score only 48% satisfaction vs. ~80% for digital platform vendors), suggesting consolidation toward fewer, higher-quality SaaS relationships rather than wholesale replacement.

- **BCG's named senior partners** — Akash Bhatia (Technology sector leader), Nicolas de Bellefonds (global AI leader), Sesh Iyer (BCG X North America chair), and Shervin Khodabandeh (AI business leader) — collectively frame SaaS as a transitioning rather than terminal category. Competitive advantage accrues to enterprises that master integration of commercial platforms with custom agent layers, not to those that rebuild everything from scratch.
