# C05: Gartner & Forrester on SaaS Durability in the AI Era

**Research Category:** Analyst Firm Formal Positions
**Scope:** Gartner and Forrester only
**Date Range:** 2025–2026
**Audience:** C-suite
**Word Count:** ~2,100

---

## Executive Summary

Gartner and Forrester have each issued formal frameworks and quantified forecasts that, taken together, describe a SaaS market that is not dying but is structurally transforming. Gartner's Hype Cycle and Magic Quadrant data show generative AI descending into the Trough of Disillusionment while agentic AI rises to the Peak of Inflated Expectations—a shift with direct implications for which SaaS categories retain enterprise pricing power. Forrester's February 2026 "SaaS-pocalypse" report quantifies the market shock ($1 trillion in software market cap erased in seven days) while arguing the enterprise core remains intact. Both firms converge on a five-to-seven-year transition window rather than an imminent replacement event. The divergence from VC narratives is sharp: where venture capital frames AI as an extinction-level event for SaaS, both analyst firms position it as a restructuring of delivery model and pricing architecture—not a wholesale elimination of enterprise software spend.

Gartner defines a composable enterprise as "an organization that achieves business outcomes through the assembly and combination of packaged business capabilities (PBCs)."

## Key Takeaways

**Gartner's formal position:**
- Agentic AI represents 2% of enterprise application software revenue in 2025; best-case 30% by 2035
- GenAI is entering the Trough of Disillusionment; agentic AI is at Peak of Inflated Expectations
- 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027
- Composable enterprise (PBC architecture) is the structural framework for surviving the transition
- Enterprise software spend grows 15.2% in 2026 regardless of AI disruption narrative

**Forrester's formal position:**
- $1 trillion in software market cap erased in one week (February 2026) based on AI displacement fear
- Global SaaS spend grows to $576 billion by 2029 despite disruption
- Vertical/domain-specific SaaS vendors are most durable; horizontal point-solutions are most vulnerable
- 50% of enterprise ERP vendors will launch autonomous governance modules by end of 2026
- 30% of enterprise app vendors will launch MCP servers by end of 2026
- Named analyst Charles Betz cites regulatory complexity (20,000+ legal jurisdictions) as a structural SaaS durability factor

**Analyst-vs-VC divergence:**
- Both firms model a 5–10 year structural transition, not an extinction event
- Both firms show aggregate SaaS spend continuing to grow through 2029
- Both firms identify pricing model disruption (per-seat to outcome-based) as the primary near-term threat, not product displacement
