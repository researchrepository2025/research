# Enterprise Leader Views on SaaS Lock-In and Switching Costs

**Research Question:** How do enterprise leaders evaluate the lock-in and switching cost implications of SaaS vs. build decisions?

**Wave:** 3 | **Document:** E03 | **Date Compiled:** 2026-03-06

---

## Executive Summary

Enterprise leaders in 2025-2026 operate in a paradox: SaaS lock-in has never been more expensive, yet switching costs have never been more visible. Price hikes of 10-900% from incumbent vendors are forcing CIOs to re-examine contracts they once treated as fixed infrastructure. Simultaneously, agentic coding tools are making custom builds economically viable for the first time at scale — Retool's February 2026 survey of 817 builders found 35% of enterprises have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom build. Regulatory pressure, specifically the EU Data Act effective September 12, 2025, is dismantling contractual lock-in mechanisms in Europe. Yet even as switching friction declines on paper, agentic AI is creating a new form of lock-in through workflow orchestration dependencies — one that lives in the AI layer rather than the SaaS contract. The net result: enterprise leaders are not abandoning SaaS, but they are aggressively renegotiating the terms on which they remain.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Lock-in costs are no longer latent** — they are active and measurable. Enterprise leaders cite 10-900% price increases from mission-critical SaaS vendors and 78% report unexpected charges from AI and consumption pricing.

2. **Regulatory dismantling of contractual lock-in is underway in Europe.** The EU Data Act (effective September 2025) prohibits exit fees by January 2027, mandates two-month termination rights, and requires machine-readable data transfers — reducing the legal durability of contract moats for vendors operating in EU markets.

3. **Agentic coding is making custom builds economically viable at scale.** 35% of enterprises have already replaced at least one SaaS tool (Retool, Feb 2026, n=817). The primary driver is not ideology but cost: enterprises can now build targeted replacements in days rather than months.

4. **Agentic AI simultaneously creates a new lock-in layer.** As enterprises build complex agentic workflows tuned to specific LLMs, prompt engineering and orchestration dependencies create switching costs that are engineering-based rather than contractual — and currently unregulated.

5. **Vendor consolidation is the dominant near-term response to lock-in anxiety.** 68% of CIOs plan vendor consolidation; a majority target 20% vendor count reduction. Gartner projects 70% of organizations will reduce cloud-native vendors to three or fewer by 2027.

6. **The build-vs-buy binary has collapsed into orchestration.** The emerging CIO posture — characterized by Vishal Sharma as "stop choosing, start orchestrating" and by ThoughtLinks as "Buy-to-Build" — treats SaaS as infrastructure for commodity functions while building internally for proprietary business logic.

7. **Data, not contracts, is the new lock-in.** Enterprise leaders and analysts (a16z, Bain, Constellation Research) consistently identify proprietary data ownership as the only durable switching cost in an era of commoditizing AI models and weakening contractual barriers.
