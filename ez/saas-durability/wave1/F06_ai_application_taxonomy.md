# F06: AI-Driven Enterprise Application Taxonomy — SaaS vs. Build Implications

**Research File:** F06
**Wave:** 1
**Topic:** Taxonomy of AI-driven enterprise applications and the SaaS-vs-build question
**Date Compiled:** 2026-03-06
**Audience:** C-suite

---

## Executive Summary

Enterprise AI applications are fragmenting into four structurally distinct categories — AI-enhanced SaaS, AI-native SaaS, AI-generated custom applications, and hybrid platform extensions — each with different competitive dynamics, cost structures, and strategic implications for the build-vs-buy decision. The traditional SaaS model is under pressure from two directions simultaneously: AI-native entrants growing at 393% year-over-year among large enterprises, and agentic coding tools that reduce the time and cost of building custom alternatives. However, global SaaS spending is simultaneously projected to grow from $318 billion in 2025 to $576 billion by 2029, indicating that disruption and growth are co-occurring rather than the former eliminating the latter. Analyst consensus from Gartner, Forrester, Bain, and IDC converges on a single structural shift: the unit of software consumption is migrating from seats to outcomes, and the taxonomy of what counts as "an application" is expanding to include AI agents, custom-built tools, and composable modules — categories that did not exist at scale three years ago.

## Key Takeaways

- **The taxonomy has four structurally distinct categories** — AI-enhanced SaaS (incumbents retrofitting), AI-native SaaS (ground-up AI products), AI-generated custom apps (agentic build), and hybrid platform extensions — each with different growth trajectories, competitive moats, and implications for the build-vs-buy decision. No single category dominates; enterprises are deploying all four simultaneously.

- **AI-native SaaS is the fastest-growing category in enterprise portfolio terms**, with large-enterprise spend up 393% year-over-year (Zylo 2026) and the application layer capturing $19B of the total $37B enterprise GenAI spend in 2025 (Menlo Ventures). Horizontal copilots grew 5.3x; vertical AI grew ~3x — both from a low base but now measurable at scale.

- **AI-generated custom applications represent the first structurally new threat to traditional SaaS from the build side since the rise of cloud**. Gartner explicitly states these platforms "hold the potential to replace off-the-shelf SaaS software with custom-built alternatives." The build-vs-buy calculus is shifting: the cost and time barrier to custom development is compressing by 30–79% (Anthropic 2026), which moves the rational default toward building for narrowly scoped, well-defined workflows.

- **Traditional SaaS is not shrinking in absolute terms** — global SaaS spend is projected to grow from $318B (2025) to $576B (2029) (Forrester) — but its structural defensibility is narrowing. The categories at highest risk are seat-based, horizontally scoped, UI-heavy point solutions with standardized workflows and limited proprietary data depth. The categories with the strongest durability are systems of record with deep data gravity, regulatory moats, and network effects.

- **The most durable enterprise software position is the hybrid model**: SaaS as the system of record and compliance layer, with custom AI extensions built atop it for differentiation. This pattern is being codified in market infrastructure — 30% of enterprise app vendors will launch MCP servers in 2026 (Forrester), enabling external AI agents to orchestrate across SaaS platforms — suggesting the buy-and-extend model is becoming the default enterprise architecture rather than a special case.
