# F01: Enterprise SaaS Market Landscape — State of the Market 2025–2026

**Research Wave:** 1 — Market Baseline
**Date Compiled:** 2026-03-06
**Scope:** Global enterprise SaaS market size, growth, penetration, category breakdown, adoption by firm size, NRR trends, and structural market trends

---

## Executive Summary

The enterprise SaaS market entered 2025-2026 as the dominant delivery model for business software, with Gartner tracking $218.5 billion in worldwide enterprise application SaaS revenue in 2024 — a 16.7% year-over-year increase — and cloud application services (SaaS) forecast to reach approximately $299 billion in 2025. Independent market research firms project the broader SaaS market (including SMB) at $390–$428 billion in 2025, depending on definitional scope. Cloud subscriptions now account for roughly 60% of enterprise software revenue, and Gartner's 2022 "cloud shift" forecast — that 51% of enterprise IT spending in key application segments would migrate to public cloud by 2025 — appears to have been directionally validated. Structural consolidation is accelerating: large vendors are acquiring niche players, enterprises are reducing application counts after peaking at 130+ apps per company in 2022, and vertical SaaS is growing at 2–3x the rate of horizontal platforms. Net revenue retention across public SaaS companies remains healthy but has moderated from 2021–2022 peaks, with enterprise-segment NRR benchmarked at 118% versus a private-company median of 101%.

## Key Takeaways

- **Market scale is unambiguous.** Gartner's enterprise application SaaS tracking reached $218.5 billion in 2024 at 16.7% YoY growth; cloud application services (SaaS) is forecast at $299 billion in 2025. Independent firms using broader definitions place the total SaaS market at $390–$428 billion in 2025. By any measure, SaaS is the dominant delivery model for enterprise software.

- **Penetration has crossed the majority threshold.** Cloud subscriptions represented 60.1% of enterprise software revenue in 2024. Application software cloud penetration reached an estimated 65.9% in 2025 per Gartner's 2022 forecast model. ERP cloud adoption reached 70% in 2025 from 52% in 2020.

- **CRM and ERP dominate the category mix.** Within Gartner's $218.5 billion enterprise SaaS total, CRM alone represented 51.4% and ERP 20.2% — meaning these two categories account for roughly 72% of the defined enterprise application SaaS market.

- **NRR has moderated from 2021–2022 peaks but remains structurally healthy.** Enterprise-segment NRR benchmarks at 118%, public company median at 108–110%, and private company median at 101%. Companies achieving NRR ≥100% grow at 48% YoY — 2x faster than those below — confirming the strategic importance of retention economics.

- **Consolidation is reshaping the competitive structure on both supply and demand sides.** On the supply side: 3,183 private software M&A transactions in 2024 (second-best year on record); vertical SaaS now ~30% of deal flow. On the demand side: enterprises have cut peak app counts from 130 to 106 (18% decline), multi-year contract signings are down, and cloud marketplace spend is growing +50% as procurement consolidates through fewer large-vendor relationships.
