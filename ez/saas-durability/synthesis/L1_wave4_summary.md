# L1 Wave 4 Summary: SaaS Vendor Responses — Synthesis for Scenario Construction

**Synthesis Layer:** 1
**Source Wave:** 4 (S01–S07)
**Research Question:** Will SaaS remain the dominant enterprise procurement method (vs. build) for AI-driven applications in 2-5 years, even with agentic coding tools enabling custom software replacement?
**Produced:** March 2026

---

## 1. Wave Theme

Wave 4's seven research segments converge on a single structural finding: the largest SaaS vendors are not defending the SaaS model — they are abandoning the label while preserving the underlying platform moat. Salesforce, ServiceNow, and Workday each publicly repudiated the "SaaS" category designation in 2025–2026, rebranding as "Agentic Enterprise," "AI Operating System," and "System of Record for People, Money, and Agents" respectively. Mid-market vendors (HubSpot, Monday.com, Notion, Intercom) executed parallel pivots to agentic platforms and consumption-based pricing. Vertical SaaS incumbents (Epic, Veeva, Procore, Guidewire) argued they own not merely software but regulatory infrastructure, longitudinal proprietary data, and "systems of consequence" where software failure creates liability — barriers that generative coding cannot replicate. The simultaneous evidence that 35% of enterprise teams have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom build (Retool, February 2026) and that 76% of enterprise AI use cases were still purchased rather than built (Menlo Ventures, 2025) reveals a market in bifurcation: the agentic coding threat is real and already operational at the margin, but structural moats — data flywheels, compliance certifications, ecosystem gravity, and workflow embedding — are absorbing the initial impact and buying the incumbents a transition window whose length is the central contested variable.

## 2. Key Evidence for Each Scenario

### Bull Case: SaaS Remains Dominant (60%+ market share in 5 years)

- [Salesforce Agentforce and Data 360 combined ARR reached $2.9 billion, up 200% year-over-year, as of Q4 FY2026, with over 22,000 deals closed and 60%+ of bookings from existing customer expansion — demonstrating AI deepening, not replacing, incumbent SaaS relationships.](https://investor.salesforce.com/news/news-details/2026/Salesforce-Delivers-Record-Fourth-Quarter-Fiscal-2026-Results/default.aspx)

- [ServiceNow 98% customer renewal rate as of Q4 FY2025, with Current Remaining Performance Obligations of $12.85 billion (+25% YoY) and RPO of $28.2 billion (+26.5% YoY) — indicating enterprises are extending, not exiting, major SaaS commitments.](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/servicenow-q4-fy-2025-earnings-highlight-ai-platform-momentum/)

- [76% of enterprise AI use cases were purchased rather than built in 2025, up from 53% in 2024 (Menlo Ventures, ~500 U.S. enterprise decision-makers) — the build share is growing but purchasing still overwhelmingly dominates.](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)

- [Deloitte TMT Predictions 2026 states "it will likely take at least five years or more before AI agents replace entire enterprise applications," citing security controls, audit trails, and large-scale deployment patterns still in development.](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html)

- [Gartner: over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by the end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, or inadequate risk controls — materially extending the timeline before custom-built AI can threaten SaaS at scale.](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027)

- [Epic's Cosmos dataset — 16.3 billion encounters across 300 million unique patient records from 310 health systems — and Veeva's ~80% global life sciences CRM market share represent proprietary data advantages that agentic coding alone cannot replicate in a 2–5 year horizon.](https://www.healthcareitnews.com/news/epic-unveils-ai-agents-showcases-new-foundational-models)

- [Compliance certifications (HIPAA, FDA 21 CFR Part 11, FedRAMP, SOC 2 Type II, PCI DSS, FINRA) take 2–5 years to establish; fewer than 5% of AI voice vendors have completed HIPAA-compliant agreements meeting enterprise legal standards, structurally excluding most new AI entrants from regulated markets.](https://beancount.io/blog/2026/02/02/vertical-saas-survival-guide-competing-against-ai-giants)

- [Salesforce's internal Agentforce deployment achieves 83% resolution rate on 32,000 customer conversations per week with only 1% escalation to humans — a public benchmark demonstrating that purpose-built, data-rich SaaS agents outperform hypothetical custom-build equivalents.](https://www.salesforce.com/agentforce/use-cases/customer-zero/)

- [Salesforce Agentforce achieves full deployment in 4.8 months versus 75.5 months for DIY builds — a vendor-supplied 16x speed advantage that, even discounted significantly, reflects the near-term time-to-value superiority of incumbent platforms.](https://cyntexa.com/blog/agentforce-statistics-and-trends/)

- [ServiceNow's Creator Workflows segment — including App Engine — accounted for 22% of $13.28 billion in 2025 revenue, featured in all 20 of ServiceNow's top Q2 2025 deals, and App Engine has led Gartner's Magic Quadrant for Enterprise Low-Code Application Platforms for six consecutive years.](https://cyntexa.com/blog/servicenow-statistics/)

### Base Case: SaaS Stays Majority but Declines (45–60% market share)

- [35% of enterprise teams have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom-built solution; 78% plan to build more in 2026 (Retool, survey of 817 builders, February 17, 2026) — the build shift has crossed from hypothesis to documented enterprise reality.](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260217548274/en/Retools-2026-Build-vs.-Buy-Report-Reveals-35-of-Enterprises-Have-Already-Replaced-SaaS-With-Custom-Software)

- [Gartner projects that by 2030, 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed within larger agent ecosystems — a material contraction in market participants that favors consolidation toward platform incumbents, not SaaS preservation broadly.](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html)

- [Seat-based SaaS pricing declined from 21% to 15% market share in a single year while hybrid pricing surged to 41% (Metronome, 2025) — pricing model disruption is compressing per-seat revenue predictability without yet replacing the SaaS delivery model itself.](https://metronome.com/state-of-usage-based-pricing-2025)

- [Workday's market capitalization fell from ~$80 billion to ~$40 billion by February 2026 and the founder returned as CEO with a $138.8 million compensation package — the market's most visible signal that the incumbent SaaS defense is believed but not yet priced at prior confidence levels.](https://fortune.com/2026/02/13/workday-founder-aneel-bhusri-billionaire-pay-package/)

- [Enterprise AI spending grew from $1.7 billion to $37 billion between 2023 and 2025 (3.2x YoY in 2025 alone), capturing 6% of the global SaaS market — a new category is expanding inside and alongside SaaS, not yet replacing it but redirecting procurement dollars.](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)

- [Bain & Company identifies "AI Cannibalizes SaaS" and "Spending Compresses" as active battleground scenarios — explicitly naming HubSpot's list building and Monday.com's task boards as workflows where AI substitution is already feasible.](https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/)

- [Vibe coding tools cut custom software development costs by 70–95% (Steven Forth, Ibbaka, 2026), materially lowering the economic threshold at which custom builds become cost-competitive with SaaS subscriptions.](https://www.ibbaka.com/ibbaka-market-blog/b2b-saas-and-agentic-ai-pricing-predictions-for-2026)

- [Only 39% of McKinsey survey respondents (n=1,993, November 2025) attribute any EBIT impact to AI use, and among those, most report less than 5% of EBIT attributable — confirming that enterprise AI adoption is real but ROI proof is still concentrated in early adopters, slowing wholesale SaaS displacement.](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai)

### Bear Case: SaaS Significantly Eroded (<45% market share)

- [Goldman Sachs projects AI agents will account for more than 60% of software economics by 2030 — if realized, this would structurally bypass the current SaaS layer.](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/ai-agents-to-boost-productivity-and-size-of-software-market)

- [Gartner projects that by 2030, 40% of enterprise application portfolios will include custom applications built using AI-native development platforms, up from 2% in 2025 — a 20x increase that, if it materializes, signals a fundamental procurement shift away from pure SaaS purchasing.](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/7046398)

- [Bill Vass, CTO of Booz Allen Hamilton: "Agentic systems can now read your business policies and generate the software. This is going to disintermediate these SaaS vendors." — a credible enterprise CTO, not a startup founder, asserting near-term structural displacement.](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas)

- [Traditional B2B SaaS funding dropped 60% year-over-year in Q4 2025 while AI-native enterprise companies raised record amounts — capital allocation is already bifurcating away from traditional SaaS toward AI-native alternatives.](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/the-saaspocalypse-ai-agents-are-eating-enterprise-software)

- [AI inference costs fell 78% through 2025; custom AI solutions deliver 50–70% infrastructure cost reduction at scale relative to API-dependent SaaS once enterprises reach $50,000–$100,000 monthly inference spend — the unit economics of custom builds are improving faster than most SaaS incumbents' pricing can accommodate.](https://aristeksystems.com/blog/custom-ai-vs-saas-ai-when-to-build-when-to-buy-and-why-only-5-of-companies-get-it-right/)

- [60% of builders created tools outside IT oversight in the past year; 25% do so frequently (Retool, 2026) — shadow IT custom builds are compounding into unapproved SaaS replacement that never appears in procurement data until it is already entrenched.](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026)

- [Satya Nadella (Microsoft CEO): "Business applications could collapse in the agent era" — the CEO of the company that owns the largest enterprise SaaS portfolio acknowledging the structural risk from his own platform against the market.](https://www.attainmentlabs.com/blog/ai-eating-software)

- [The S&P 500 Software and Services Index dropped 8.7% to a nine-month low following SaaS earnings misses; SaaS stocks declined more than 20% since late 2025 — the fastest drawdown outside the 2022 tech unwind and 2008 financial crisis.](https://investorplace.com/hypergrowthinvesting/2026/02/saasmageddon-is-here-and-not-all-software-stocks-will-survive/)

## 5. Critical Caveats for Synthesis

### Vendor Self-Interest Bias (All S01–S03, S07)

Every quantitative metric cited by Salesforce (Agentforce deal count, ARR, resolution rates), ServiceNow (Now Assist ACV, renewal rates), and Workday (Illuminate adoption, AI actions) is produced by the vendor itself. Agentforce "deal" definitions are not third-party audited — the count of 22,000 deals conflates paid and unpaid, and includes early-stage agreements that may never reach production. Salesforce's 4.8-month vs. 75.5-month deployment comparison is a vendor-supplied benchmark with no disclosed methodology. Salesforce's 83% Agentforce resolution rate on its own help site is a self-reported Customer Zero metric — a controlled internal deployment, not a representative external customer sample.

### Conflicting ARR Figures (S01 vs. S04)

S01 reports Salesforce Agentforce ARR of $800 million (Q4 FY2026) and combined Agentforce + Data 360 ARR of $2.9 billion (+200% YoY). S04, using Q3 FY26 data, reports Agentforce ARR of $500 million (330% YoY) and combined ARR of $1.4 billion (+114% YoY). These are not contradictory — they reflect different reporting periods (Q3 FY26 vs. Q4 FY2026) — but they will appear inconsistent in the position paper without explicit temporal labeling. The S04 figures should be treated as intermediate, S01 as current.

### S04 Scope Creep — Noted in Content Review

S04 ("AI Features in SaaS") drifted toward general enterprise AI adoption statistics (McKinsey, Gartner) that are also covered in other waves (Wave 3 market data, Wave 5 enterprise buyer data). Several statistics in S04 (Gartner 40% agent penetration by 2026, Deloitte outcome-based pricing forecasts) are verbatim duplicates of content in S01, S02, and S06, creating a false sense of corroboration. These statistics originate from single primary sources (Gartner, Deloitte) cited through multiple secondary sources — they should be attributed to the primary source only in the position paper.

### S05 Pro-Moat Framing Bias — Noted in Content Review

S05 ("Structural Moats") sources are structurally selected for moat-affirming perspectives: Bain, Gartner, IDC, Greylock (Jerry Chen), SignalFire, McKinsey, and Andreessen Horowitz are all institutional investors or management consultants with commercial incentives aligned toward SaaS incumbent survival (they advise or invest in those companies). The only meaningful counterforce cited in S05 is the Pavilion Blog's observation that AI-powered migration tooling is compressing switching costs, and the V7 Labs caveat that proprietary data advantages are narrowing as external data becomes widely available. The Meta research finding that synthetic data performs within 2% of real data on classification tasks (cited in S04) is the most direct empirical challenge to the data moat thesis and should be weighted accordingly.

### Retool Survey Methodology Caveat

Retool's 2026 Build vs. Buy Report (n=817) is the wave's primary source for enterprise custom-build activity. Retool is a low-code platform vendor with a direct commercial interest in enterprise build activity. Survey respondents are Retool users and adjacent communities, not a representative cross-section of enterprise procurement decision-makers. The 35% replacement figure should be contextualized as an upper-bound estimate from a build-biased sample.

### Workday Stock as a SaaS Health Proxy — Scope Issue

Multiple segments treat Workday's stock decline as a proxy for broad SaaS market health. Workday's issues — 12–13% FY27 guidance versus analyst expectations of higher growth, a CEO transition, and ERP-specific exposure to AI disruption narratives — are partly company-specific. ServiceNow and Salesforce posted revenue beats in the same period. The stock market reaction to SaaS as a sector (S&P 500 Software and Services Index -8.7%) is a sentiment indicator, not an enterprise procurement share shift.

### Outcome-Based Pricing Adoption Gap (S06)

S06 contains an important internal contradiction: Gartner projects 40% of enterprise SaaS will include outcome-based pricing by 2026, yet market survey data shows only 9% of SaaS companies have fully implemented outcome-based models. The Gartner forecast appears to define "include outcome-based elements" broadly enough to encompass hybrid models with any metered component, while the 9% figure reflects pure outcome implementation. These are not measuring the same thing and should not be cited in proximity without the clarifying distinction.

## 6. Wave Verdict

Wave 4 vendor evidence strongly supports the Base Case as the most probable 2–5 year outcome, with the Bull Case carrying meaningful probability at the platform layer and the Bear Case most applicable to the point-product mid-market. The structural picture that emerges from all seven segments is not a simple SaaS-lives-or-dies binary but a stratified outcome: Tier 1 horizontal platforms (Salesforce, ServiceNow, SAP, Microsoft) are successfully co-opting the build impulse by becoming extensible platform layers — they are likely to grow, not shrink, as agents proliferate, because agents need governed infrastructure to run. Vertical SaaS incumbents in regulated markets (Epic, Veeva, Guidewire, Procore) are the most defensible category, with compliance certification cycles, proprietary longitudinal data, and "system of consequence" workflow embedding providing 2–5 year structural protection that agentic coding tools cannot compress. The bear case is most credible in the mid-market point-solution layer — workflow automation, BI tools, CRM form builders, and project management tools — where Bain, Gartner, and Retool data converge on documented replacement activity already underway. The critical unknown is the pace at which enterprise buyers develop the governance infrastructure, security tooling, and internal engineering capacity to operate custom AI applications at scale — the 42% citing lack of engineering bandwidth and Gartner's 40%+ agentic project cancellation rate are the most significant variables moderating the bear case timeline. The vendor perspective captured in Wave 4 is, by definition, the defense brief. The absence of credible build-side cost data, build-side maintenance burden analytics, and independent enterprise procurement shift measurement means the bear case remains underweighted relative to what a balanced synthesis would require.
