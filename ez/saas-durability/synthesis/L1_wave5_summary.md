# L1 Wave 5 Summary: Consulting & Analyst Perspectives on SaaS Durability
## Synthesis Layer 1 — Scenario Construction Input

**Research question:** Will SaaS remain the dominant enterprise procurement method (vs. build) for AI-driven applications in 2-5 years, even with agentic coding tools enabling custom software replacement?

**Sources synthesized:** C01 (McKinsey), C02 (Bain), C03 (BCG), C04 (Big 4), C05 (Gartner/Forrester), C06 (IDC/Other Analysts), C07 (Consulting Advisory Patterns)

**Compiled:** 2026-03-06

---

## 1. Wave Theme

The major consulting firms and analyst houses of Wave 5 converge on a single overarching thesis: SaaS is not dying, but the model that made it dominant — per-seat subscription pricing, feature-breadth competition, and low switching cost — is under its most serious structural pressure since SaaS itself displaced on-premise software. McKinsey frames this as [a "foundational shift redefining what software is, who builds it, who uses it, and how companies are organized and operate"](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-ai-centric-imperative-navigating-the-next-software-frontier); Bain characterizes it as [transitions that "lead not to extinction but to transformation, adaptation, and coexistence"](https://www.bain.com/insights/the-great-debate-will-agentic-ai-kill-saas/); BCG documents SaaS spending continuing to grow from [13% to 21% of enterprise IT budgets over five years](https://www.ciodive.com/news/saas-software-services-spend-surges-bcg/801732/) even as specific seat-based models erode; Forrester projects global SaaS spend growing from [$318 billion in 2025 to $576 billion in 2029](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) while simultaneously titling their February 2026 report "SaaS As We Know It Is Dead." The firms split on the severity of disruption but not on its existence. The primary threat identified is not enterprise-wide custom rebuilds replacing SaaS — rather it is seat-compression (AI agents replacing human users), pricing-model obsolescence (per-seat to outcome-based), and point-solution displacement by AI-native competitors. The build-vs-buy debate is answered consistently by all firms: build only where genuine competitive differentiation exists; buy or partner everywhere else. The firms' own commercial incentives (implementation revenue, OpenAI Frontier Alliance partnerships) create a structural bias against recommending wholesale SaaS abandonment that readers should weigh alongside the research.

## 2. Key Evidence for Each Scenario

### Bull Case (SaaS remains dominant, 60%+ market share in 5 years)

- **Aggregate SaaS spending is growing, not contracting.** Forrester projects global SaaS spending to reach [$576 billion by 2029, up from $318 billion in 2025](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) — an 81% nominal increase over four years. IDC projects public cloud software at a [15.7% CAGR through 2027](https://www.cio.com/article/648632/idc-pace-of-enterprise-applications-sales-will-increase-next-year.html) and enterprise application software growing to [$483 billion in 2027](https://www.cio.com/article/648632/idc-pace-of-enterprise-applications-sales-will-increase-next-year.html). Gartner projects enterprise software spend growing [15.2% in 2026](https://saastr.com/gartner-enterprise-software-spend-will-grow-a-stunning-15-2-next-year-but-most-of-that-will-go-to-price-increases-and-ai-apps/).

- **Agentic AI revenue is currently negligible as a share of enterprise software.** Gartner quantifies agentic AI at [2% of enterprise application software revenue in 2025, with a best-case ceiling of 30% by 2035](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025) — a 10-year, not 2-5-year, transition arc. McKinsey documents global enterprise spending on AI applications at [nearly $5 billion in 2025, less than 1% of total software application spend](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/upgrading-software-business-models-to-thrive-in-the-ai-era), despite an eightfold year-over-year increase.

- **Historical precedent strongly favors coexistence, not replacement.** Bain's Chuck Whitten cites three analogues: [client/server computing did not kill mainframes; cloud computing did not eliminate on-premises systems; mobile devices did not replace PCs](https://www.bain.com/insights/the-great-debate-will-agentic-ai-kill-saas/). BCG's SaaS spending data — [growing from 13% to 21% of enterprise IT budgets over five years concurrent with cloud and AI adoption](https://www.ciodive.com/news/saas-software-services-spend-surges-bcg/801732/) — empirically supports coexistence.

- **Enterprise SaaS gross retention remains strong.** Bain documents [average gross retention "still around 90% or better" for enterprise SaaS vendors](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/) in 2026, despite the stock market selloff. Forrester notes that ["enterprises spend tens to hundreds of millions of dollars on SaaS software"](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) and characterizes the "death of SaaS" narrative as "overstated."

- **More than 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027.** Gartner's formal prediction that [over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 due to escalating costs and unclear business value](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025) sets a high bar for custom builds to succeed as SaaS alternatives. McKinsey documents enterprises ["retrenching — rehiring people where agents have failed"](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/one-year-of-agentic-ai-six-lessons-from-the-people-doing-the-work) after failed agentic deployments.

- **Regulatory complexity creates durable SaaS moats.** Forrester's Charles Betz: ["There are about 20,000 legal jurisdictions worldwide and compliance with applicable regulation is a major reason why people trust vendors like SAP."](https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/04/ai_replace_saas/) Vertical/domain-specific SaaS vendors are explicitly identified as having the ["greatest chance of survival"](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) through specialization and proprietary data control.

- **Only 11% of enterprises rely primarily on in-house AI development.** BCG's Widening AI Value Gap (n=1,250+) finds [only 11% of firms rely primarily on in-house development, and just 4% depend on a single end-to-end vendor for their full AI stack](https://media-publications.bcg.com/The-Widening-AI-Value-Gap-Sept-2025.pdf) — the overwhelming majority pursue hybrid approaches anchored in commercial platforms.

- **ERP and CRM are regaining momentum, not losing it.** BCG's November 2025 IT Spending Pulse documents [ERP and CRM regaining momentum as "essential foundations for advanced analytics and GenAI adoption"](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/it-spending-pulse-optimism-investment-and-priorities) — the exact opposite of a replacement narrative for the two largest SaaS categories.

### Base Case (SaaS stays majority but declines, 45-60% market share)

- **Seat-based pricing is being structurally challenged by AI headcount reduction.** BCG finds [40% of IT buyers cite seat reduction as their primary lever to decrease software spending](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai); Bain finds [AI could automate "between 30% and 50% of activity across functions"](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/), directly pressuring per-seat SaaS revenue even where vendors retain customers.

- **Bain projects up to 50% of technology spending will shift toward agents long-term.** Bain's "Building the Foundation for Agentic AI" projects ["up to half of technology spending could be on agents running across the enterprise"](https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/) at scale. Near-term: 5-10% directed toward agentic foundation-building. This implies a gradual but substantial reallocation of software wallet from SaaS licenses to agent infrastructure.

- **Net revenue retention is stalling.** Bain reports that [net revenue retention has "stalled as penetration curves flatten"](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/) for enterprise SaaS vendors, and that ["most incumbent software vendors have yet to deliver compelling AI-enabled offerings or establish clear monetization strategies."](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/)

- **SaaS public equity multiples have already repriced.** Market data cited in C06 shows [the median revenue multiple for software firms dropped below 5x in early 2026, from above 7x at the start of 2025](https://ai2.work/technology/the-2026-saas-apocalypse-why-wall-street-is-dumping-software-stocks/), with [the iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF down over 23% year-to-date](https://ai2.work/technology/the-2026-saas-apocalypse-why-wall-street-is-dumping-software-stocks/). Bain attributes [broad software indices down approximately 25% from 12-month highs](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/) partly to the introduction of Claude Cowork by Anthropic.

- **Deloitte projects AI agent-powered solutions at 60% of the total addressable software market by 2030.** Deloitte's 2026 Software Industry Outlook projects [AI agent-powered solutions could represent 60% of the total addressable software market by 2030](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/software-industry-outlook.html) — a scenario compatible with SaaS persistence at reduced share, as AI-powered SaaS would itself be counted in that 60%.

- **78% of IT leaders expect agentic AI to replace or augment ERP functionality within three years.** Bain's Technology Maturity Assessment Benchmarking Survey (n=480) finds [78% of IT leaders expect at least some ERP functionality to be replaced or augmented by agentic AI over the next three years](https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/). This is intent, not outcome — but at scale it represents a structural reallocation of budget.

- **Standalone analytics already declining sharply.** BCG documents standalone analytics as a proportion of IT budget [down 18 percentage points compared with April 2025](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/it-spending-pulse-optimism-investment-and-priorities) as its capabilities are absorbed into broader AI initiatives — an early data point for point-solution SaaS categories being subsumed.

- **35% of surveyed enterprise builders have already replaced at least one SaaS tool.** Retool's 2026 Build vs. Buy Report (n=817) finds [35% of teams have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom build](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026), with [78% expecting to build more custom internal tools in 2026](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026). **Caveat: Retool is a vendor with direct commercial interest in the "build" outcome; see Section 5.**

- **Forrester's SaaS-pocalypse identified four investor fears — all partially valid.** Forrester's analysis finds the four investor fears driving the 2026 selloff — [SaaS losing AI agent primacy, per-seat pricing obsolescence, vibe-coding enabling startup replication, and SaaS sprawl driving abandonment](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) — each have empirical support even if the aggregate narrative is "overstated."

### Bear Case (SaaS significantly eroded, <45% market share)

- **McKinsey's "post-SaaS model" vision.** McKinsey explicitly describes a post-SaaS architecture where ["a human employee primarily interacts with one agent interface while accomplishing their core workflows. That agent, in turn, interacts with several back-end agents and uses back-end APIs to act directly on data repositories — which should accelerate the commoditization of these elements, with more of the value moving to the agent layer."](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/upgrading-software-business-models-to-thrive-in-the-ai-era) This is an architectural vision in which SaaS front-ends are disintermediated.

- **McKinsey forecasts 5-10 percentage-point acceleration in vendor switching.** McKinsey's pre-2025 foundational paper quantifies ["the most lasting and disruptive impact of gen AI will be a wide-scale acceleration of vendor switching of five to ten percentage points, as new upstarts take advantage of the technology to erode established players' advantages."](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/navigating-the-generative-ai-disruption-in-software) At scale, this directly erodes incumbent SaaS market share.

- **AI startups scale 5x faster than SaaS companies did.** Deloitte documents that ["AI startups scale from US$1 million to US$30 million in revenue five times faster than SaaS companies did"](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/software-industry-outlook.html), and Redpoint Ventures confirms ["AI companies are reaching $5M in annualized revenue 13 months earlier than the prior SaaS cohort."](https://www.redpoint.com/content-hub/written/ai-update-2025-infrared-report/) Faster AI-native startup scaling compresses the window in which incumbents can adapt.

- **Forrester names specific incumbents at risk of disintermediation.** Forrester explicitly identifies Oracle, Salesforce, and ServiceNow as companies [facing competition from "agentic control planes above their application suites"](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/), and ["horizontal point-solution vendors" as facing "low switching costs and weak enterprise workflows."](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/)

- **90% seat-revenue reduction scenario from AI-agent substitution.** An analytical model cited in C06 describes [a "90% reduction in seat revenue" if 10 AI agents replace 100 sales reps and enterprises buy 10 Salesforce seats instead of 100](https://ai2.work/technology/the-2026-saas-apocalypse-why-wall-street-is-dumping-software-stocks/). This is the bear case mechanism: not tool replacement, but headcount reduction that directly destroys seat-license revenue even with zero product displacement.

- **Private capital has already voted against SaaS.** PitchBook data shows [AI capturing 65% of total VC deal value in 2025](https://pitchbook.com/news/reports/2026-us-venture-capital-outlook) — the largest sectoral concentration in venture history by that metric — while [venture-growth pre-money valuations for AI companies jumped 95.7% year-over-year](https://pitchbook.com/news/reports/q4-2025-enterprise-saas-vc-trends). The capital reallocation from SaaS to AI has already occurred in private markets.

- **$1 trillion in software market cap erased in one week.** Forrester documents that [over $1 trillion in market capitalization was erased from software stocks in the first week of February 2026](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/), representing the largest single-week repricing of SaaS value in history.

- **Hyperscaler capex ($660-690B in 2026) is crowding out SaaS application spend.** Multiple analyst sources identify [the Big Five hyperscalers planning $660–690 billion in infrastructure spending in 2026, nearly doubling 2025 levels](https://ai2.work/technology/the-2026-saas-apocalypse-why-wall-street-is-dumping-software-stocks/), with [funds being diverted from application software toward massive compute costs](https://ai2.work/technology/the-2026-saas-apocalypse-why-wall-street-is-dumping-software-stocks/) as the primary mechanism of SaaS budget compression.

- **Deloitte: "Agentic AI could even begin to replace today's SaaS tools over time."** The most direct displacement statement in the Wave 5 corpus: Deloitte's 2026 Software Industry Outlook states ["In 2026, SaaS apps will likely get smarter, more personalized, adaptive, and autonomous. Agentic AI could even begin to replace today's SaaS tools over time."](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/software-industry-outlook.html)

## 4. Key Data Points for the Position Paper

These are the 10 most quotable, specific statistics or findings from Wave 5, selected for their precision, source authority, and scenario relevance.

1. **Forrester projects global SaaS spending growing 81% in four years despite disruption narrative.** ["Global SaaS spending: $318 billion (2025), $512 billion (2028), $576 billion (2029)."](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) — Forrester, February 11, 2026. Strongest single data point for the bull case.

2. **Gartner: agentic AI is 2% of enterprise software revenue now, 30% ceiling by 2035 in best case.** ["Agentic AI could drive approximately 30 percent of enterprise application software revenue by 2035, surpassing $450 billion, up from 2 percent in 2025."](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025) — Gartner, August 26, 2025. Establishes the 10-year, not 2-5-year, displacement timeline.

3. **BCG: 40% of IT buyers cite seat reduction as their primary cost lever.** ["According to BCG's IT buyers survey, 40% of buyers cite seat reduction as their primary lever to decrease software spending."](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/rethinking-b2b-software-pricing-in-the-era-of-ai) — BCG, August 13, 2025. Core base-case mechanism: revenue pressure without product replacement.

4. **McKinsey: Only 16% of SaaS incumbents have commercialized AI as a stand-alone product.** ["Although only 16 percent of SaaS incumbents have commercialized AI applications as stand-alone products, those that have report two to three times higher customer traction and revenue."](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/upgrading-software-business-models-to-thrive-in-the-ai-era) — McKinsey, September 22, 2025. Quantifies the execution gap between disruption narrative and SaaS vendor reality.

5. **Gartner: 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027.** ["Over 40 percent of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 due to escalating costs and unclear business value."](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025) — Gartner, August 26, 2025. Most important single data point for the bull case: the friction tax on build alternatives.

6. **Bain: 78% of IT leaders expect agentic AI to replace or augment ERP functionality within three years.** ["78% of IT leaders expect at least some ERP functionality to be replaced or augmented by agentic AI over the next three years"](https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/) — Bain Technology Maturity Assessment Benchmarking Survey, n=480, 2025. Intent signal at scale.

7. **BCG: Only 5% of companies are achieving AI value at scale; 60% are generating no material value.** ["Only 5% of companies in our 2025 study of more than 1,250 firms worldwide are achieving AI value at scale. ## 5. Critical Caveats for Synthesis

**C01 (McKinsey):** McKinsey's February 2026 Frontier Alliance with OpenAI creates a direct commercial interest in enterprise AI agent adoption. McKinsey is simultaneously advising clients on AI strategy and earning from the deployment of the very technology that would displace some SaaS spend. The firm's build-vs-buy framework ("build where differentiation exists, buy everywhere else") is sound but should be read against this deployment revenue interest.

**C02 (Bain):** Bain's SaaS stock market brief attributes index declines partly to the introduction of Claude Cowork by Anthropic, but this is presented as a market-sentiment observation, not a product-displacement claim. The "frozen" market characterization applies to incumbent software vendors' growth outlook, not to SaaS as a category. Bain's 78% ERP-intent statistic (n=480) reflects IT leader expectations, not executed replacements — intent and outcome frequently diverge in enterprise software.

**C03 (BCG):** The "25-35x revenue per employee for AI-native firms" statistic appearing in BCG's comparative data table (Section 13) is an extreme claim sourced to BCG's December 2025 CEO roadmap and should be flagged if used in the position paper. BCG's own body of research shows 60% of firms generating no material AI value at all — the 25-35x figure likely reflects a narrow cohort of AI-native companies, not a generalizable benchmark. Do not use this statistic as a representative AI-native performance claim without the accompanying caveat that 60% of firms achieve no material AI value.

**C04 (Big 4):** The Executive Summary of C04 overstates the Big 4 erosion thesis. The statement that "a consistent structural thesis has emerged: agentic AI will erode — and in some scenarios replace — traditional SaaS as the dominant enterprise software delivery model" is not uniformly supported by the body of the document. The Key Takeaways contradict it: none of the four firms publishes a recommendation to reduce or exit enterprise SaaS investments; all frame disruption as requiring managed transformation. Deloitte's statement that "Agentic AI could even begin to replace today's SaaS tools over time" is hedged with "could" and "over time" — it is not a forecast, it is a risk identification. Weight the Key Takeaways section of C04 over its Executive Summary in the position paper.

**C05 (Gartner/Forrester):** The Forrester "SaaS-pocalypse" blog post (February 11, 2026) uses intentionally dramatic language ("SaaS As We Know It Is Dead") that its own body text contradicts — the same document projects SaaS spending growing to $576 billion by 2029. This is a headline written for attention, not a forecast. The body-text finding ("the enterprise core isn't vanishing") is the substantive Forrester position for scenario construction purposes.

**C06 (IDC/Others):** The CB Insights section uses secondary sources (AI2.work analysis, market data aggregators), not CB Insights primary data. Do not attribute the "median revenue multiple below 5x" or "90% seat revenue reduction" analytical models to CB Insights — they are sourced to AI2.work's synthesis of market data. The Retool 2026 Build vs. Buy Report (n=817) is the most-cited survey in C07 and is also referenced in C06: Retool is a vendor with a direct commercial interest in enterprises choosing to build rather than buy. Their survey methodology skews toward builders (64% of respondents were senior managers and above; self-selected by willingness to complete a Retool survey). The 35% "already replaced SaaS" finding should be treated as a directional signal, not a generalizable enterprise-wide statistic.

**C07 (Consulting Advisory Patterns):** The Retool survey dominates the case study evidence in C07. All named build-replacing-SaaS case studies (ClickUp, Harmonic) come from the same Retool report. The consulting conflict-of-interest analysis (Frank S. Scavo, Enterprise Spectator) is sourced to an independent newsletter, not a research institution — it is analytically sound but should be framed as expert commentary rather than empirical research. The "10-week project executed in six, 30-40% cost reduction, client pays the 10-week price" consulting margin arbitrage finding (ConsultingQuest) is anecdotal, not surveyed.

## 6. Wave Verdict

The consulting and analyst wave tells a consistent story: the base case for 2-5 years is not SaaS collapse but SaaS restructuring, with material market share at risk from pricing model obsolescence and point-solution displacement rather than from enterprise-wide custom rebuilds. The **bull case** (60%+ market share) is supported by aggregate spending forecasts from Forrester ($576B by 2029), IDC (16.5% SaaS CAGR through 2027), and Gartner (15.2% enterprise software growth in 2026), combined with Gartner's finding that 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 and McKinsey's documentation of enterprises retrenching after failed agent deployments — the friction tax on build alternatives is real and substantial. The **base case** (45-60%) is supported by BCG's finding that 40% of IT buyers are already targeting seat reduction, Bain's stalled net revenue retention data, the public market repricing (multiple compression from 7x to below 5x), and the directional evidence from Retool that 35% of builders have replaced at least one SaaS tool. The **bear case** (<45%) requires simultaneous execution of three conditions that all major consulting firms treat as unlikely in a 2-5 year window: (1) agentic coding tools democratizing complex enterprise software development at negligible cost; (2) enterprise risk tolerance for custom-built solutions in regulated, mission-critical workflows expanding dramatically; and (3) SaaS incumbents failing to adapt pricing and AI embedding fast enough to retain wallet share. Gartner's 2035 ceiling of 30% agentic AI share suggests the bear case, if it materializes, takes longer than 5 years to fully develop. The modal consulting/analyst position, controlling for commercial bias, assigns the bear case low near-term probability — the most aggressive credentialed forecast (Deloitte's 60% AI-agent TAM by 2030) is compatible with SaaS dominance if AI-powered SaaS is counted as SaaS, not as its replacement.
