### Experts Whose Positions Have Been Disputed or Misattributed

1. **Sebastian Siemiatkowski / Klarna**: The Klarna narrative ("SaaS is dead" anchor case) is misattributed across most coverage as proof of SaaS displacement by AI. CX Today reported Klarna replaced Salesforce/Workday with alternative SaaS apps. Siemiatkowski personally corrected the narrative. Use only with the full context of the correction.

2. **Alap Shah / Citrini Research**: The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis report is widely cited in financial media but originates from a "little known firm" (Bloomberg). Shah admitted the market reaction "was definitely larger than we expected." The report's scenarios (S&P 500 -38%, unemployment 10.2%) are speculative macro scenarios, not enterprise software analysis. Use with explicit credibility caveat.

3. **John Collison (Stripe) / Brian Armstrong (Coinbase)**: The quote "It's not clear how you run an AI-coded codebase" is attributed to both Collison and Armstrong in different source files. At least one attribution is incorrect. The quote was identified as ambiguously attributed in Wave 8's critical caveats. Do not use with either named attribution without verifying the primary source.

4. **Dario Amodei**: His external prediction that AI would write 90% of code in "6–12 months" was documented as not having materialized at the 6-month check (IT Pro). His internal claim is now "effectively 100%." The internal/external gap should be noted whenever citing his capability predictions.

5. **Gartner (institutional)**: Multiple Gartner forecasts cited across all 9 waves are mutually tension-producing and originate from different analysts (Verma vs. Nag vs. Lovelock vs. Torii). They should not be cited as a coherent "Gartner forecast" — always attribute to the specific named analyst and date.

6. **Satya Nadella's "SaaS will collapse" quote**: This quote appears in Wave 2, Wave 3, Wave 4, and Wave 6 with slightly different wording. The most precise version is from Windows Central: "Business applications will probably all collapse in the Agent Era because they are essentially CRUD databases with a bunch of business logic." All variants are from the same BG2 podcast interview. The Windows Central version is the most specific and should be used as the primary citation.
