# L1 Wave 7 Summary: Equity Research & Financial Data
**Synthesis Layer:** 1 | **Source Wave:** 7
**Date:** March 6, 2026
**Prepared by:** Synthesis Layer 1 Agent
**Research Question:** Will SaaS remain the dominant enterprise procurement method (vs. build) for AI-driven applications in 2–5 years, even with agentic coding tools enabling custom software replacement?

---

## 1. Wave Theme

Wave 7 — spanning equity research, valuation trends, IT budget data, public company financials, M&A patterns, and venture funding — reveals a SaaS sector caught between genuine spending growth and a sharp repricing of its future business model viability. The dominant signal is bifurcation, not collapse: public markets erased approximately [$1 trillion in software market capitalization between mid-January and mid-February 2026](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-11-saaspocalypse-2026-why-wall-street-is-slashing-software-valuations-and-turning-cautious-on-debt) in what Jefferies named the "SaaSpocalypse," yet [Gartner simultaneously forecasted 14.7% worldwide software spending growth in 2026 to over $1.4 trillion](https://www.saastr.com/gartner-business-software-spend-will-grow-a-stunning-14-7-in-2026-to-1-4-trillion-up-from-11-5-in-2025-are-you-grabbing-it/). Wall Street has fractured into irreconcilable camps — Goldman Sachs drawing a newspaper-industry analogy, JPMorgan calling the selloff "broken logic" — while actual enterprise financials show no measurable AI-driven displacement in reported churn or NRR as of March 2026. M&A activity hit an [all-time record of 2,698 SaaS transactions in 2025](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/software-equity-groups-2026-saas-report-highlights-record-deal-volume-and), private equity is committing the largest technology-focused fund in history to software buyouts, and incumbent SaaS vendors are absorbing rather than ceding ground to AI-native competitors. The financial data wave is best read as a bifurcation thesis: the seat-based licensing model faces its most serious structural threat in two decades, but SaaS as a procurement modality — the practice of buying rather than building enterprise software — is being reinforced, not abandoned, by both acquirers and enterprise CIOs.

## 2. Key Evidence for Each Scenario

### Bull Case (SaaS remains dominant, 60%+ market share in 5 years)

- [Gartner forecasts worldwide software spending growing 14.7% in 2026 to $1.4 trillion](https://www.saastr.com/gartner-business-software-spend-will-grow-a-stunning-14-7-in-2026-to-1-4-trillion-up-from-11-5-in-2025-are-you-grabbing-it/), and [Forrester projects global SaaS spending to rise from $318 billion in 2025 to $576 billion by 2029](https://www.forrester.com/press-newsroom/forrester-forecasts-the-future-of-software-as-a-service-technologies/) — absolute dollar growth is not in question.

- [SaaS M&A hit an all-time record volume of 2,698 transactions in 2025, up 28% year-over-year, with total software deal value rising 66% to $420 billion](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/software-equity-groups-2026-saas-report-highlights-record-deal-volume-and) — acquirers are buying SaaS assets at scale, not exiting the category.

- [Private equity firms are at a historical apex of commitment to SaaS: Thoma Bravo alone closed $34.4 billion in buyout funds (including a $24.3 billion fund described as the largest technology-focused fund in history) and completed a $12.3 billion Dayforce take-private — its largest ever deal](https://www.corpdev.org/2025/06/05/thoma-bravo-secures-34-4-billion-buyout-fund-haul-decoding-the-software-sectors-new-power-play/), signaling long-horizon confidence in recurring-revenue software.

- [No actual customer cancellation data, churn rates, or NRR changes attributable directly to AI agent displacement were found in primary sources as of March 2026](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas); enterprise gross retention holds at ~90% and the median public SaaS NRR remains at 108% (Blossom Street Ventures, [Q1 2025](https://blossomstreetventures.medium.com/the-state-of-saas-revenue-growth-and-efficiency-in-q1-2025-9413d086bc92)).

- [76% of organizations are purchasing AI solutions rather than building them in-house](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/the-great-ai-flip-why-76-of-enterprises-stopped-building-ai-in-house), and [51% of 2025 enterprise generative AI spend ($19 billion of $37 billion) went to user-facing applications — i.e., bought SaaS-like products — not custom infrastructure](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/).

- [JPMorgan research (February 17, 2026) identified an internal contradiction in the bear thesis: "If AI companies are going to disrupt, destroy, and replace all software companies, those AI stocks should be more valuable. Or if AI is overvalued...software stock investors should be less worried."](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/02/17/jp-morgan-research-says-broken-logic-is-driving-th/) The firm called the selloff an "overshoot."

- [Gartner predicts that because AI is in the "Trough of Disillusionment" in 2026, it will most often be sold to enterprises by their incumbent software provider](https://www.christianandtimbers.com/insights/why-does-gartner-describe-2026-as-a-trough-of-disillusionment-year-for-ai) — reinforcing incumbent SaaS platform consolidation.

- [SaaS spending grew from 13% to 21% of total tech budgets over five years (2019–2024) per BCG](https://www.ciodive.com/news/saas-software-services-spend-surges-bcg/801732/), and [average organization SaaS spend increased to $55.7M in 2026, up 8% year-over-year per Zylo](https://zylo.com/reports/2026-saas-management-index/).

### Base Case (SaaS stays majority but declines, 45–60% market share)

- [Public SaaS median EV/NTM revenue compressed from 18–19x in 2021 to 5.1x in December 2025](https://aventis-advisors.com/saas-valuation-multiples/), with the 10-year Sapphire Ventures average at 5.9x — suggesting the current level is close to a long-run equilibrium reflecting a structurally lower growth trajectory.

- [Median SaaS revenue growth fell from 39% (Q2 2015 peak) to 12.2% by Q4 2025](https://aventis-advisors.com/saas-valuation-multiples/), and [aggregate quarterly net new ARR across the cloud software universe dropped 29% year-over-year in Q1 2025 — $1.65 billion vs. $2.33 billion](https://www.saastr.com/the-great-saas-slowdown-what-q1-2025-numbers-reveal-about-the-cloud-software-market/).

- [Gartner projects 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed within agent ecosystems by 2030, and 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will shift to usage-, agent-, or outcome-based pricing by 2030](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html) — a structural change in revenue architecture, not category elimination.

- [NRR has declined even for the top 10% of SaaS companies since 2022 (ChartMogul, 2,500+ companies)](https://chartmogul.com/reports/saas-retention-the-new-normal/): HubSpot fell from a 115% peak to ~102%; Okta declined from 115% to 106% sequentially; the sector median private SaaS NRR fell from 105% (2021) to 101% (2024).

- [AI spending is responsible for 30% of all new budget dollars in 2025, with enterprise AI spending rising 5.7% while total IT budgets grow just 1.8%](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241205135351/en/Enterprise-AI-Spending-to-Rise-5.7-Percent-in-2025-Despite-Overall-IT-Budget-Increase-of-Less-than-2-Percent-ISG-Study) — a mathematically crowding-out dynamic for non-AI SaaS.

- [The average company has reduced their SaaS applications by 18% from 2022 to 2024, with companies now using fewer SaaS apps than in 2021 (Zylo)](https://zylo.com/reports/2025-saas-management-index/) — consolidation is actively contracting the long tail of point-solution SaaS.

- [AI-native platforms command public market median multiples above 10x, while traditional SaaS clusters below 5x (Eqvista)](https://eqvista.com/ai-vs-saas-valuation-multiples/), and Sales & Marketing Automation SaaS has already hit a floor of 1.9x EV/NTM (Multiples.vc, [October 2025](https://multiples.vc/reports/software-saas-valuation-multiples)).

- [Morgan Stanley analyst Keith Weiss found that AI excitement is not translating into improving IT budget growth, with all IT industries in their CIO survey showing deterioration to 2026 budget growth expectations](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/morgan-stanley-cio-survey-why-ai-hype-isnt-boosting-2026-it-budgets-4446984).

### Bear Case (SaaS significantly eroded, <45% market share)

- [Goldman Sachs strategist Ben Snider drew an explicit analogy to the newspaper industry's 95% stock decline between 2002 and 2009, warning the software selloff may be "the end of the beginning" of a structural decline, and that near-term earnings beats are "in many cases insufficient to disprove the long-term downside risk"](https://www.thestreet.com/investing/stocks/goldman-sachs-signals-grim-shift-as-software-stocks-bounce).

- [Goldman Sachs identified 41 software companies as AI "losers," naming Salesforce, Workday, DocuSign, Atlassian, UiPath, and marketing automation platforms on its sell list, constructing explicit pairs trades based on seat-based licensing exposure](https://dynamicbusiness.com/topics/news/goldman-sachs-ai-software-stocks-2026-which-companies-made-the-buy-list-and-which-made-the-sell-list.html).

- [Jefferies (Brent Thill, February 23, 2026) cut Workday from Buy to Hold with a price target from $325 to $150, and DocuSign from Buy to Hold with a target from $105 to $45, characterizing the sector as requiring a "full reset" rather than a cyclical correction](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-23-the-saaspocalypse-deepens-jefferies-downgrades-workday-and-docusign-as-ai-disrupts-the-app-software-sector).

- [Bain & Company found that AI could automate 30–50% of activity across business functions, and that OpenAI's o3 frontier reasoning model dropped 80% in cost in just two months](https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/) — accelerating the cost curve toward agentic replacement of workflow SaaS.

- [Bill Vass, CTO of Booz Allen Hamilton: "This is going to disintermediate these SaaS vendors and these ERP vendors. The market just realized that."](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas) — a named enterprise buyer articulating displacement intent.

- [Multiple SaaS companies reported in Q4 2025 earnings that AI succeeded too well — customers are reducing software seats rather than adding them, as AI-enhanced workers accomplish more with fewer licenses](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/saaspocalypse-ai-agents-software-industry-analysis); the AI coding market jumped from $550M to $4B in 2025 (Menlo Ventures, [December 2025](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)), expanding the internal-build capability baseline.

- [Anthropic's 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report documents that TELUS teams created over 13,000 custom AI solutions while shipping engineering code 30% faster](https://resources.anthropic.com/2026-agentic-coding-trends-report) — concrete evidence of enterprise custom-build capability at scale. (Caveat: commercially interested source; see Section 5.)

- [Software revenue growth fell from a 40-year average of 27% to 11% for 2025 per CorpDev.Org](https://www.corpdev.org/2025/06/05/thoma-bravo-secures-34-4-billion-buyout-fund-haul-decoding-the-software-sectors-new-power-play/), and [AI's share of seed funding hit 42% in 2025 with the "great rotation" away from non-AI-native SaaS at early stage (AngelList data via SaaStr)](https://www.saastr.com/the-great-rotation-how-ai-ml-crushed-traditional-saas-in-seed-investing-during-1h-2025-per-angellist/) — the replacement pipeline is being funded.

## 4. Key Data Points for the Position Paper

The 10 most quotable, specific statistics with inline citations:

1. **The "SaaSpocalypse" scale**: ["Between mid-January and mid-February 2026 alone, approximately $1 trillion was wiped from the collective value of software stocks, with the S&P North American Software Index posting its worst monthly decline since the 2008 financial crisis"](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-11-saaspocalypse-2026-why-wall-street-is-slashing-software-valuations-and-turning-cautious-on-debt) — while Gartner simultaneously forecasted 14.7% software spending growth in 2026.

2. **SaaS M&A at all-time record**: [2,698 SaaS transactions closed in 2025 — the highest annual volume on record, up 28% year-over-year — with AI-referenced targets accounting for 72% of all SaaS M&A transactions](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/software-equity-groups-2026-saas-report-highlights-record-deal-volume-and). Total software deal value rose 66% to $420 billion.

3. **Gartner's 35% point-tool displacement and 40% pricing shift**: [Gartner projects that by 2030, 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed within larger agent ecosystems, and 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will shift toward usage-, agent-, or outcome-based pricing](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html). These are the most credible named-source displacement projections in the corpus.

4. **Net new ARR collapse**: [Aggregate quarterly net new ARR across the cloud software universe dropped 29% year-over-year in Q1 2025 — $1.65 billion versus $2.33 billion in Q1 2024](https://www.saastr.com/the-great-saas-slowdown-what-q1-2025-numbers-reveal-about-the-cloud-software-market/). This is the strongest single bearish financial signal in the dataset, predating widespread agentic deployment.

5. **Valuation bifurcation by sector**: [Sales & Marketing Automation SaaS traded at 1.9x EV/NTM revenue as of October 2025, while Data Infrastructure SaaS traded at 6.2x and DevOps at 5.7x (Multiples.vc)](https://multiples.vc/reports/software-saas-valuation-multiples) — a 3x valuation spread within the same SaaS category, driven by AI disruption risk pricing.

6. **Buy vs. build remains buy**: [76% of organizations are purchasing AI solutions rather than building them in-house](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/the-great-ai-flip-why-76-of-enterprises-stopped-building-ai-in-house), and [enterprise generative AI spend reached $37 billion in 2025 — tripling from $11.5 billion in 2024 — with most going to applications and platforms, not custom development](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/).

7. **The seat compression signal**: [Multiple SaaS companies reported in Q4 2025 earnings that customers are reducing software seats rather than adding them, as AI-enhanced workers accomplish more with fewer licenses](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/saaspocalypse-ai-agents-software-industry-analysis). No company has reported this in hard NRR figures yet, but the anecdotal pattern is corroborated by Morgan Stanley's Sanjit Singh, who found [only 4 of 150 CIOs raised their hands when asked about agentic AI deployment in enterprise settings](https://fintool.com/app/research/analyst/sanjit-singh).

8. **PE capital commitment as confidence signal**: [Thoma Bravo closed $34.4 billion in buyout funds (including the largest tech-focused fund in history at $24.3 billion) and completed its largest-ever deal — a $12.3 billion take-private of Dayforce — in February 2026](https://www.corpdev.org/2025/06/05/thoma-bravo-secures-34-4-billion-buyout-fund-haul-decoding-the-software-sectors-new-power-play/). Global PE dry powder targeting software stood at approximately $1.2 trillion.

9. **AI coding market 7x growth in one year**: [The AI coding market grew from $550 million to $4 billion in 2025 (Menlo Ventures)](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-2025-the-enterprise/), with [50% of developers using AI coding tools daily as of Anthropic's 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report](https://resources.anthropic.com/2026-agentic-coding-trends-report). This represents the fastest-growing custom-build enablement category in the dataset.

10. **No displacement in hard financials — yet**: [No actual customer cancellation data, churn rates, or NRR changes attributable directly to AI agent displacement were found in primary sources as of March 2026](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas). Enterprise SaaS churn remains below 1.5% monthly; public SaaS median EBITDA margins reached 9.3% in Q3 2025 — [the industry's decade-long tolerance for unprofitable growth is officially over](https://www.ngpcap.com/insights/unveiling-the-future-can-saas-successfully-bounce-back-in-2024), but not due to displacement.

## 5. Critical Caveats for Synthesis

**R06 — SaaStr "great rotation" characterization:**
The claim that ["Traditional SaaS — software that moves data around without intelligent processing — is dying at seed stage"](https://www.saastr.com/the-great-rotation-how-ai-ml-crushed-traditional-saas-in-seed-investing-during-1h-2025-per-angellist/) is attributed to SaaStr citing AngelList data. No named analyst at AngelList is quoted directly; SaaStr is an editorially opinionated publication, not a primary research source. The underlying AngelList data may support the directional claim but the "dying" characterization is editorial framing. Treat as Tier 3 corroborating signal, not a primary assertion.

**R01 — JPMorgan bull position institutionally attributed, not by named analyst:**
JPMorgan's "broken logic" thesis and "overshoot" characterization are attributed to "JPMorgan research" in Motley Fool and buildy.ai coverage without a named analyst. The reports cite the institution but no individual analyst is on record. Weight as institutional position, not a named-analyst call; do not place it in the same epistemic category as Ben Snider (Goldman), Jackson Ader (KeyBanc), or Brent Thill (Jefferies), all of whom are named with specific public statements.

**R02 — Eqvista "25–30x AI multiple" is Tier 3:**
Eqvista's claim that ["AI-native platforms command 25–30x EV/Revenue, a staggering premium over traditional SaaS"](https://eqvista.com/ai-vs-saas-valuation-multiples/) is from a financial services marketing site without named methodology or dataset disclosure. The directional claim (AI-native commands a premium) is confirmed by Tier 1 sources (BVP Nasdaq Cloud Index shows Palantir at 63.7x; Sapphire Ventures documents high-growth SaaS at 10.7x). Do not use the Eqvista "25–30x" figure as a specific cited statistic; use BVP and Multiples.vc data instead.

**R03 — Anthropic 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report is commercially interested:**
Anthropic's report (cited for the TELUS 13,000 custom AI solutions example and the 50% daily developer AI tool usage figure) is produced by Anthropic, which directly profits from agentic coding adoption. The TELUS statistic is plausible and consistent with other enterprise AI adoption surveys, but should be disclosed as a commercially interested source in any position paper. Do not treat it as equivalent to Menlo Ventures, Gartner, or Morgan Stanley data.

**R04 — "84% gross margin erosion from AI infrastructure" is unverified:**
The statistic that ["84% of companies see 6%+ gross margin erosion from AI infrastructure costs"](https://www.softwareseni.com/outcomes-based-pricing-and-ai-first-saas-gross-margin-economics-explained/) — cited in R04 — could not be confirmed against a primary survey source during research. Do not use this figure.

**R05 — Valsoft "most active acquirer" claim:**
The claim that Valsoft completed 16 acquisitions in 2025 as the "most active strategic buyer in SaaS" is sourced from SaaSStock (Tier 3). It is plausible but not confirmed by Software Equity Group or PitchBook primary data in the corpus. Use with caution or omit.

**General note on "SaaSpocalypse" market data:**
Multiple statistics about the February 2026 selloff are sourced from FinancialContent/MarketMinute, which appears to be a syndicated financial content service. The statistics (IGV -30%, $1 trillion market cap erased, $830 billion first-week loss) are internally consistent and corroborated by buildy.ai and Goldman Sachs coverage, but should not be treated as primary exchange data. Gartner, BVP, and named-analyst sources remain the gold standard for this wave.

## 6. Wave Verdict

The equity and financial data wave most strongly supports the **base case** — SaaS retaining majority status but experiencing structural compression, estimated at 45–60% of enterprise software procurement over a 5-year horizon — with meaningful tail risk toward the bear case if seat compression moves from anecdote to reported metric in 2026–2027 earnings cycles. The bull case requires accepting that the "SaaSpocalypse" selloff represents mass market irrationality (JPMorgan's position), that spending growth data (Gartner's 14.7% software growth in 2026) reflects underlying demand health rather than AI-inflated pricing at renewal, and that incumbent SaaS platforms will successfully monetize agentic AI features before upstarts or internal builds displace them — all of which are live hypotheses, not settled facts. The bear case requires accepting Ben Snider's newspaper-industry analogy as the correct structural frame, seat compression reports as early-stage evidence of an S-curve transition rather than noise, and the 29% aggregate net new ARR decline in Q1 2025 as a leading indicator rather than a macro-budget artifact — none of which has yet been confirmed in reported enterprise churn data as of March 2026. The most financially grounded conclusion is that the *seat-based subscription model* is at high risk of structural disruption, while *SaaS as a procurement modality* (buy vs. build) is being reinforced by the same forces: record M&A volume, PE capital concentration, and the finding that 76% of enterprises continue to buy rather than build AI solutions. The position paper should distinguish these two layers — model risk versus modality risk — rather than treating "SaaS durability" as a single binary question.
