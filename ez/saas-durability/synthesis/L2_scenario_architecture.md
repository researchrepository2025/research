## 1. Baseline Definition

### What Is "SaaS Market Share" as Measured Here?

**The metric:** Share of enterprise software *procurement spend* allocated to externally purchased commercial SaaS applications (delivered via subscription or consumption model), versus custom-built software (internally developed using any tooling, including agentic coding assistants) or open-source self-hosted deployments.

**Share of what:**
Enterprise software application spend — the subset of total IT budget directed at business application software (excluding infrastructure, hardware, and IT services). The primary denominator is Gartner's enterprise application software category. This is distinct from total IT spend (which includes cloud infrastructure, networks, and hardware) and narrower than the full software market (which includes developer tools, security, and middleware). Gartner estimates enterprise application software at approximately $218 billion as of 2024 ([https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/6647734](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/6647734)), with the broader cloud SaaS end-user spending at $299 billion in 2025 ([https://www.channel-impact.com/gartner-worldwide-public-cloud-end-user-spending-to-total-723-billion-in-2025/](https://www.channel-impact.com/gartner-worldwide-public-cloud-end-user-spending-to-total-723-billion-in-2025/)).

**Compared to what:**
- Custom build: Internally developed software using in-house engineering resources, including AI-assisted and agentic coding tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Devin, Replit Agent, etc.)
- Open-source self-hosted: Deployed open-source software with internal operations burden (excluded from SaaS count even if cloud-hosted)
- Hybrid: Configurations in which enterprises buy a platform but build the application logic on top (e.g., ServiceNow App Engine, Salesforce AgentExchange) — these count as SaaS in this framework because the procurement relationship is with a commercial vendor

**What is NOT counted as "SaaS displacement":**
- Enterprises switching from one SaaS vendor to another (vendor churn, not category loss)
- Enterprises consolidating from 10 point-solution SaaS tools to 3 platform SaaS tools (consolidation, not displacement)
- AI-native SaaS products purchased from commercial vendors (these remain SaaS by definition)

**Current baseline (2025–2026):**
- Global SaaS end-user spending: ~$299–$318 billion in 2025, depending on scope (Gartner via Channel Impact: $299B; Forrester: $318B ([https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/)))
- Enterprise AI use cases: 76% purchased rather than built internally in 2025, up from 53% in 2024 ([https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/))
- Enterprise build behavior: 35% of enterprises have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom build ([https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026)), but this survey is build-biased (see caveats throughout)
- No hard NRR or churn data attributable directly to AI displacement found in primary sources as of March 2026 ([https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas))
- Average organization SaaS spend: $55.7M in 2026, up 8% year-over-year ([https://zylo.com/reports/2026-saas-management-index/](https://zylo.com/reports/2026-saas-management-index/))

**The definitional caution for the position paper:** "SaaS pricing model disruption" (per-seat to consumption) and "SaaS category displacement" (buying replaced by building) are separable phenomena. Analyst consensus strongly confirms the former; the evidence on the latter is contested. The position paper must keep these distinct throughout.

## 2. Scenario Definitions

---

### BULL SCENARIO: SaaS Adapts and Dominates

**SaaS Share Range:** 60%–70% of enterprise AI application procurement by 2027; 58%–68% by 2030

**Narrative:** Agentic AI tools accelerate the migration of enterprise software from per-seat models to consumption-based platforms, but SaaS vendors — especially Tier 1 platforms and vertical specialists — successfully absorb this transition, embedding AI agents into their own offerings and capturing the AI application budget rather than losing it to custom builds. Enterprise buyers default to purchasing AI from trusted vendors, not building autonomously, because the non-code costs of enterprise software (security certification, compliance, disaster recovery, change management, legal liability) remain prohibitive for DIY approaches. SaaS absolute spend grows substantially even as its share of a rapidly expanding market holds at or above 60%.

**Required Conditions (ALL must hold):**

1. **Gartner's 40%+ agentic project cancellation rate materializes.** Over 40% of agentic AI projects are canceled by end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, or inadequate risk controls ([https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027)), resetting enterprise confidence in DIY agentic builds and reinforcing buy preference.

2. **Incumbent SaaS platforms successfully convert to agentic and consumption-based revenue.** Salesforce's Agentforce trajectory — $2.9B combined AI and Data ARR, 22,000+ deals, 200% year-over-year growth in Q4 FY2026 ([https://investor.salesforce.com/news/news-details/2026/Salesforce-Delivers-Record-Fourth-Quarter-Fiscal-2026-Results/default.aspx](https://investor.salesforce.com/news/news-details/2026/Salesforce-Delivers-Record-Fourth-Quarter-Fiscal-2026-Results/default.aspx)) — scales to represent the dominant AI agentic delivery channel. ServiceNow's 98% renewal rate holds ([https://futurumgroup.com/insights/servicenow-q4-fy-2025-earnings-highlight-ai-platform-momentum/](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/servicenow-q4-fy-2025-earnings-highlight-ai-platform-momentum/)). SaaS incumbents capture the AI application budget, not just defend their legacy subscriptions.

3. **AI-generated code quality deficits remain unresolved at enterprise grade.** AI-generated code introduces security vulnerabilities in 45% of cases across 100+ LLMs ([https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/](https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/)); no agentic coding tool holds FedRAMP authorization ([https://www.fedramp.gov/ai/](https://www.fedramp.gov/ai/)); the 9.1% commercial-codebase success rate on SWE-bench Pro ([https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16941](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16941)) does not materially improve by 2027, keeping enterprise security teams from approving wholesale custom deployment.

4. **Enterprise buy preference for AI solutions continues strengthening.** The 2024-to-2025 reversal from 47%/53% build/buy to 76% buy ([https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)) persists. CIO incumbent preference — 65% of Global 2000 CIOs prefer incumbent solutions when available ([https://a16z.com/leaders-gainers-and-unexpected-winners-in-the-enterprise-ai-arms-race/](https://a16z.com/leaders-gainers-and-unexpected-winners-in-the-enterprise-ai-arms-race/)) — holds through 2027.

5. **SaaS gross retention holds near 90%.** No actual customer cancellation data or NRR changes attributable to AI displacement emerge in 2026–2027 earnings cycles ([https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/02/is-ai-killing-saas)). Bain confirms average gross retention "still around 90% or better" ([https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/)).

6. **Regulated and vertical SaaS moats hold.** Compliance certification cycles (FedRAMP at $250K–$1.5M and 6–18 months; HIPAA; SOC 2 Type II) ([https://www.workstreet.com/blog/fedramp-certification-cost](https://www.workstreet.com/blog/fedramp-certification-cost)) remain prohibitive for custom-build alternatives. Fewer than 5% of AI vendors hold HIPAA-compliant agreements meeting enterprise legal standards ([https://beancount.io/blog/2026/02/02/vertical-saas-survival-guide-competing-against-ai-giants](https://beancount.io/blog/2026/02/02/vertical-saas-survival-guide-competing-against-ai-giants)), protecting Epic, Veeva, Guidewire, Procore, and vertical analogues.

**Triggering Events to Watch (leading indicators):**
- Salesforce Q4 FY2027 Agentforce ARR exceeds $5 billion with >80% of bookings from existing customers (confirms incumbent capture of agentic budget)
- Two or more high-profile enterprise custom-build failures become public (resets CIO risk tolerance)
- Gartner AI Hype Cycle shows agentic AI entering Trough of Disillusionment through 2027 with widespread project cancellations reported
- METR task-horizon trajectory slows — doubling time extends beyond 250 days (suggesting bear case timeline pushed past 2030)
- No AI coding tool achieves FedRAMP authorization by end of 2027

**Probability Estimate: 25%**

Reasoning: The bull case is strongly supported by current buying behavior (76% buy over build, 65% CIO incumbent preference), the absence of measurable NRR impact in reported financials through Q1 2026, and the genuine structural barriers of compliance certification and enterprise-grade code quality. However, it requires that multiple converging pressures — SaaS inflation at 4.5x CPI ([https://www.vertice.one/insights/saas-inflation-rate](https://www.vertice.one/insights/saas-inflation-rate)), AI inference cost collapse (OpenAI o3 dropped 80% in cost over two months per Bain ([https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/](https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/))), documented build displacement in workflow and BI categories (Retool), and the METR capability doubling trajectory — all fail to compound into category-level share erosion over 5 years. That is too many convergent pressures to dismiss. The bull case is more credible for 2027 than for 2030, and more credible for regulated verticals than for horizontal SaaS.

**Key Named Advocates:**
- **Jensen Huang** (CEO, Nvidia): "The notion that the software industry is in decline and being replaced by AI [is] the most illogical thing in the world"; agentic AI will "use these tools [SAP, ServiceNow] on our behalf" rather than replace them ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/26/nvidia-jensen-huang-gpu-ai-threat-software-companies-saas-earnings-chips.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/26/nvidia-jensen-huang-gpu-ai-threat-software-companies-saas-earnings-chips.html))
- **Maribel Lopez** (Principal, Lopez Research): "The reports of SaaS's death are greatly exaggerated"; the narrative is generating "real confusion for enterprise buyers" ([https://www.lopezresearch.com/saas-isnt-dead-but-the-dead-narrative-is-leading-enterprise-buyers-astray/](https://www.lopezresearch.com/saas-isnt-dead-but-the-dead-narrative-is-leading-enterprise-buyers-astray/))
- **Orlando Bravo** (Founder/Managing Partner, Thoma Bravo): "Software is about domain knowledge, not code"; companies with "30 years of domain expertise built into their product are really, really cheap right now"; closed $34.4B in SaaS buyout funds in 2025 ([https://capwolf.com/orlando-bravo-why-software-stocks-are-oversold-now/](https://capwolf.com/orlando-bravo-why-software-stocks-are-oversold-now/))

**Weakest Assumption:** That SaaS incumbents can successfully monetize agentic AI at the pace required to offset seat compression. Only 16% of SaaS incumbents had commercialized AI as a stand-alone product as of 2025 ([https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/upgrading-software-business-models-to-thrive-in-the-ai-era](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/upgrading-software-business-models-to-thrive-in-the-ai-era)). If SaaS vendors successfully defend market share but cannot convert seat revenue into agent revenue fast enough, the bull case on SaaS dominance is achieved but the bull case on SaaS *financial health* is not — undermining the category's reinvestment capacity and long-run position.

### BASE SCENARIO: Hybrid Dominance with Meaningful SaaS Erosion

**SaaS Share Range:** 47%–58% of enterprise AI application procurement by 2027; 43%–55% by 2030

**Narrative:** SaaS retains majority status as the primary procurement modality through 2030, but loses 10–20 percentage points of market share as agentic coding tools cross economic feasibility thresholds for a growing set of bounded enterprise use cases. The displacement is category-selective — concentrated in horizontal point solutions, workflow automation, internal tooling, and mid-market BI — while regulated verticals, platform infrastructure, and mission-critical systems of record remain structurally protected. SaaS vendors simultaneously face pricing model disruption (seat-based to consumption), point-solution displacement (by AI agents and custom builds), and competitive pressure from AI-native startups growing 5–10x faster than SaaS benchmarks. The net result is a SaaS sector that is smaller as a share of enterprise software spend, but still the dominant procurement modality — a differentiated landscape where the strongest platform incumbents grow while the long tail of point-product SaaS contracts.

**Required Conditions (MOST must hold, 4 of 6):**

1. **Agentic coding tools continue maturing at their documented trajectory.** METR's task-completion time horizon doubles every 88.6–196 days ([https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/](https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/)), producing agents capable of autonomous multi-day software projects by 2028–2029. Build costs compress 40–60% for targeted applications, but enterprise-grade quality gaps (security vulnerabilities in 45% of AI-generated code ([https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/](https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/)), 9.1% commercial-codebase SWE-bench Pro success rate ([https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16941](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16941))) prevent generalized deployment at scale.

2. **SaaS inflation remains elevated, increasing build incentive.** SaaS inflation at 12.2% annually — 4.5x general CPI ([https://www.vertice.one/insights/saas-inflation-rate](https://www.vertice.one/insights/saas-inflation-rate)) — compresses build-vs.-buy break-even timelines to 18–30 months for commodity workflows. Salesforce's pattern of extracting 72% of ARR growth from price increases ([https://www.saastr.com/salesforce-microsoft-google-and-atlassian-all-raise-prices-again-in-2025-hooray/](https://www.saastr.com/salesforce-microsoft-google-and-atlassian-all-raise-prices-again-in-2025-hooray/)) rather than new customers drives CFO-level build decisions in targeted categories.

3. **35% of point-product SaaS tools are replaced by agents or absorbed into platforms by 2030.** Deloitte's formal forecast ([https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html)) and Gartner's parallel projection materialize in the long tail of horizontal, UI-first, CRUD-adjacent tools while core systems of record remain intact.

4. **Enterprise governance maturity improves but remains a limiting factor through 2027.** Only 21% of enterprises currently have mature governance models for autonomous agents ([https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/about/press-room/state-of-ai-report-2026.html](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/about/press-room/state-of-ai-report-2026.html)), and 42% of AI initiatives are abandoned before production ([https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/ai-experiences-rapid-adoption-but-with-mixed-outcomes-highlights-from-vote-ai-machine-learning](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/ai-experiences-rapid-adoption-but-with-mixed-outcomes-highlights-from-vote-ai-machine-learning)). Governance capability improves to ~50%+ mature governance by 2029, enabling larger-scale build deployments in the 2028–2030 window.

5. **Seat compression reduces SaaS NRR to 95–100% range without triggering wholesale vendor replacement.** Bain's "seat compression" dynamic — customers purchasing 450 seats instead of 500 and deploying agents for marginal usage ([https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/)) — materializes as the primary NRR headwind. AI reduces the number of human seats needed rather than eliminating the vendor relationship.

6. **AI-native SaaS startups capture a meaningful share of the new AI application budget.** LLM-native companies grow at 400% year-over-year while reaching 80% of traditional SaaS ACV ([https://www.bvp.com/atlas/the-state-of-ai-2025](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/the-state-of-ai-2025)), but this growth is counted as SaaS (buy) not build, maintaining overall buy dominance even as specific vendor relationships change.

- 2027 earnings cycles show SaaS median NRR dropping below 100% across public market names (first hard financial evidence of seat compression)

**Probability Estimate: 55%**

Reasoning: The base case is supported by the convergence of 7 of 9 research waves. Wave 1 (Foundation) assigns it ~50%; Wave 2 (VC/Investor) explicitly confirms it as the modal institutional position (Bain: "convergence — a heterogeneous landscape where AI and SaaS complement and compete"); Wave 3 (Enterprise CTO/CIO) shows hybrid orchestration as the dominant buyer posture; Wave 4 (SaaS Vendors) shows vendors repositioning as platforms rather than defending point-product SaaS; Wave 5 (Consulting/Analyst) produces unanimous base-case positions from McKinsey, Bain, BCG, Forrester, and Gartner; Wave 6 (Developer Community) shows real but bounded build activity; Wave 7 (Financial) shows equity repricing without financial confirmation in reported churn; Wave 8 (Agentic Coding) shows capability trajectory that will compress build economics by 2028–2030 but not 2026–2027; Wave 9 (TCO) provides the clearest mechanistic support — the break-even math crosses the favorable threshold for custom builds in commodity categories within 18–30 months at base scenario assumptions, but ERP and security remain structurally SaaS territory. The base case is also supported by every historical technology disruption analogy: cloud vs. on-premise (13+ years to majority displacement), open source vs. commercial (10+ years), mobile vs. desktop. The 45–60% estimate reflects real but bounded erosion, most heavily concentrated in the 2028–2030 window when capability and governance mature together.

If agentic tools reduce maintenance costs from the classical 15–20% annual rate toward 1–5% (as RPA-context vendors claim but which is unverified in broad enterprise software contexts ([https://blog.duvo.ai/why-every-rpa-project-breaks-and-how-agentic-ai-fixes-it](https://blog.duvo.ai/why-every-rpa-project-breaks-and-how-agentic-ai-fixes-it))), the base case economics shift toward the bear case, and 43–55% share becomes a floor rather than a midpoint.

### BEAR SCENARIO: Structural SaaS Displacement

**SaaS Share Range:** 30%–45% of enterprise AI application procurement by 2027; 25%–40% by 2030

**Narrative:** Agentic coding tools cross the enterprise-grade quality threshold by 2027–2028, fundamentally collapsing the economic case for purchasing software in the majority of workflow-layer and mid-market categories. Combined with AI inference cost deflation, SaaS pricing-as-extraction reaching its political limit with CFOs, and the emergence of "superhuman coder" AI capability eliminating the talent constraint on building, enterprises across segments accelerate replacement of the majority of their SaaS point-product estates. The AI agent layer (predicted by Satya Nadella to absorb all business logic) disintermediates traditional SaaS front-ends. Platform SaaS (Salesforce, ServiceNow, SAP) survives as infrastructure but is redefined as an API substrate rather than a user-facing application, collapsing ACV. SaaS share drops below 45% by 2028 and continues declining toward 30–35% by 2030 in a market dominated by custom agentic applications and the few platforms that become genuine agent operating systems.

**Required Conditions (ALL must hold):**

1. **METR's task-horizon doubling trajectory (88.6 days) persists through 2027, producing agents capable of autonomous multi-week software projects by late 2027.** At this rate, frontier agents reach 8-hour task horizons (the enterprise workday threshold) by late 2026–early 2027 ([https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/](https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/)). Nikola Jurkovic's median "superhuman coder" arrival of 2027 ([https://ai-2027.com/research/timelines-forecast](https://ai-2027.com/research/timelines-forecast)) materializes. If this condition fails, the bear case timeline shifts to 2030+.

2. **AI-generated code security and quality deficits are substantially resolved by 2027–2028.** The current 45% security vulnerability rate in AI-generated code ([https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/](https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/)) falls to below 10% through model improvement and automated security tooling, allowing enterprise security teams to approve production deployment at scale without prohibitive remediation overhead.

3. **Ongoing maintenance costs for AI-built software are substantially automated below 5% of initial build cost annually.** This is the highest-leverage TCO variable (Wave 9 sensitivity analysis). Maintenance automation reaching 1–5% annually — versus the classical 15–20% rate — compresses 5-year custom software TCO to below SaaS at almost any application scale ([https://xenoss.io/blog/total-cost-of-ownership-for-enterprise-ai](https://xenoss.io/blog/total-cost-of-ownership-for-enterprise-ai)). This condition is currently supported only by RPA-context vendor claims, not validated in broad enterprise settings.

4. **Enterprise governance frameworks for autonomous agents mature to where 50%+ of enterprises have production-ready agentic deployment infrastructure.** Currently only 21% have mature governance ([https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/about/press-room/state-of-ai-report-2026.html](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/about/press-room/state-of-ai-report-2026.html)). The bear case requires this to triple to ~60%+ by 2028, enabling the organizational infrastructure to operate custom-built agentic software at scale.

## 6. The Hypothesis Verdict

**Hypothesis under evaluation:** "SaaS is unlikely to be dead in 2-5 years and will remain the majority method for AI-driven enterprise applications."

**Verdict: Partially confirmed, with important category-level and timing-horizon caveats.**

The evidence from all 9 research waves supports the hypothesis in its core claim — SaaS will remain the majority procurement modality for enterprise software through 2027 and very likely through 2030 — but does not support the hypothesis without substantial qualification. The weight of evidence assigns approximately **55% probability to the base case** (SaaS majority at 43–58% market share by 2030 with meaningful erosion), **25% to the bull case** (SaaS dominant at 58–68% share with successful adaptation), and **20% to the bear case** (SaaS declining below 43% share by 2030). This probability distribution confirms the hypothesis — SaaS remains the majority method — at approximately **80% confidence** (bull + base combined). However, that 80% confidence contains a substantial "barely majority" outcome: the base case's lower bound (43–45% share by 2030) represents a scenario where SaaS barely retains majority status, having lost 15–20 percentage points of enterprise software procurement share in 5 years.

**The three crucial caveats that condition this verdict:**

First, the "majority" is not monolithic. SaaS remains the majority procurement method in aggregate, but ceases to be majority in specific categories — workflow automation, standalone analytics, internal tooling, and mid-market BI — within 3–5 years. The position paper should be precise that it is making a market-level argument, not a category-level argument. Forrester, Deloitte, and Gartner independently project 25–40% displacement in the point-product SaaS long-tail by 2030, a finding this research cannot dismiss. The largest SaaS categories (ERP, platform horizontal SaaS, regulated vertical SaaS) remain structurally protected and drive the overall "majority" finding; they are not representative of the entire market.

Second, "SaaS pricing model disruption" is not the same as "SaaS category displacement," and confusing them introduces systematic error into the hypothesis evaluation. The position paper must be explicit that the per-seat pricing model is almost certainly dead — IDC projects 70% of vendors will refactor pricing by 2028 ([https://www.idc.com/resource-center/blog/is-saas-dead-rethinking-the-future-of-software-in-the-age-of-ai/](https://www.idc.com/resource-center/blog/is-saas-dead-rethinking-the-future-of-software-in-the-age-of-ai/)) — without concluding that the SaaS procurement category itself is imperiled. Enterprises are buying AI agents (from Salesforce Agentforce, ServiceNow, and AI-native startups), which is still buying, not building. The hypothesis is confirmed insofar as buying remains dominant; it would only be refuted if custom building becomes the majority modality, which the evidence does not support.

Third, the most consequential uncertainty is not the current state but the 2028–2030 trajectory hinge point. The 5-year verdict is genuinely more uncertain than the 2-year verdict. If METR's 88.6-day task-horizon doubling continues and agentic maintenance costs are substantially automated, the bear case arrives within the 5-year window. The single most important number to watch is not market share (a lagging indicator) but the METR task-horizon trajectory, which is the leading technical indicator for the displacement economics. If the doubling rate slows to 196+ days, the hypothesis is confirmed at high confidence through 2030. If it holds at 88.6 days, the bear case arrives on time. The evidence as of March 2026 supports the hypothesis — SaaS will remain majority — but holds it loosely for the 2029–2030 window, where the technical trajectory and enterprise governance maturity will jointly determine whether the base case holds or the bear case arrives ahead of schedule.

**Overall probability estimate for the hypothesis (SaaS remains majority procurement method through 2027–2030):**
- By 2027: **87%** (bull 35% + base 52% = 87% probability SaaS holds majority)
- By 2030: **77%** (bull 20% + base 57% = 77% probability SaaS holds majority)

The hypothesis is confirmed, but conditionally, with a meaningful and rising tail risk that the bear case arrives within the 5-year window — a risk that should be presented transparently rather than dismissed.
