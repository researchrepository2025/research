# E06: Enterprise Talent and Organizational Capability Gaps in the Agentic Coding Era

**Wave 3 Research | Enterprise SaaS Durability Project**
**Date:** March 2026
**Audience:** C-Suite
**Research Question:** Do enterprises have the engineering talent and organizational capability to build and maintain custom software at scale, even with agentic coding?

---

## Executive Summary

The premise that agentic coding tools will enable enterprises to close their software talent gap — and thus reduce dependence on commercial SaaS — rests on a shaky empirical foundation. The evidence from 2025–2026 reveals a more complicated picture: enterprises face a deepening structural shortage of senior engineering talent, agentic coding tools deliver measurably inconsistent productivity gains (and in some controlled settings, net negative gains for experienced engineers), and the organizational prerequisites for effective agentic deployment — mature DevOps, governance frameworks, and security practices — are absent in most enterprises. Meanwhile, the new skills demanded by agentic workflows themselves require the kind of senior judgment that is already most scarce. Far from closing the talent gap, agentic coding appears, in the near term, to widen the performance spread between organizations that already have strong engineering capability and those that do not.
