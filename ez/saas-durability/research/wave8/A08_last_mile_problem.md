# A08: The Last Mile Problem — Deployment and Adoption Challenges of Custom-Built Enterprise Software

**Wave 8 Research | Enterprise SaaS Durability Project**
**Date:** March 2026
**Audience:** C-Suite
**Research Question:** Beyond code generation, what are the "last mile" challenges of deploying custom-built enterprise software?

---

## Executive Summary

The collapse in the cost of generating functional software via agentic coding tools has created a seductive illusion: that shipping code is the hard part of enterprise software. It is not. The hard part is everything that follows. Enterprise deployments routinely fail not because the software does not work, but because employees do not use it — a pattern so prevalent it has its own designation in the practitioner literature. According to Gartner research, 67% of software features go unused across enterprise deployments, and digital transformation failure rates range from 70% to 95% globally, costing an estimated $2.3 trillion per year. Custom-built tools accelerated by agentic coding lower the barrier to producing a working prototype, but they do not reduce — and may amplify — the last mile costs of user training, change management, documentation maintenance, and ongoing support. This report catalogs the specific factual evidence for each of those cost centers.

## Key Takeaways

- [STATISTIC] Digital transformation failure rates average 87.5%, costing $2.3 trillion annually globally — the dominant cause is poor user adoption, not poor code quality. ([Source](https://blog.meltingspot.io/why-digital-transformation-projects-fail/))

- [STATISTIC] The Standish Group documents that 64% of enterprise software features are "rarely" or "never" used; MIT research shows custom internal AI builds succeed at roughly half the rate (33%) of purchased vendor solutions (67%). ([Source 1](https://agileculture.com/64-percent-unused-software-features/), [Source 2](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/))

- [STATISTIC] Training for enterprise software averages $1,000–$2,500 per user at initial deployment, with perpetual annual costs for organizations with typical turnover rates; 45% of employees report that new software arrives with no adequate training at all. ([Source 1](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/what-is-the-true-cost-of-enterprise-training-and-onboarding-at-scale), [Source 2](https://blog.meltingspot.io/why-digital-transformation-projects-fail/))

- [STATISTIC] 60% of custom tool builders in a 2025 survey created tools outside IT oversight; only 19% of organizations have advanced AI automation maturity; and 37% cannot measure the productivity impact of what they have shipped. ([Source](https://www.newsweek.com/nw-ai/enterprises-are-replacing-saas-faster-than-you-think-11521483))

- [STATISTIC] When custom enterprise software does reach production, it transfers the full support burden internally: the help desk ticketing market is growing at 12% CAGR to an estimated $40 billion by 2033, driven in part by the proliferation of underdocumented internal tools requiring continuous user-initiated support. ([Source](https://www.datainsightsmarket.com/reports/help-desk-ticketing-system-1440276))
