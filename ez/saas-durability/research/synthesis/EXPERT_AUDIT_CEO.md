# Expert Camp Assignment Audit — Tech CEOs and Corporate Executives
## SaaS Durability Research Project
**Audit Date:** 2026-03-06
**Auditor:** Critical Audit Agent
**Scope:** C-suite and VP-level executives at technology companies only
**Standard Applied:** Direct, explicit statement about enterprise SaaS-vs-build procurement modality required for Bull/Base/Bear assignment. Statements about AI capability, coding productivity, product roadmap, or general software disruption without explicit procurement connection → UNCLEAR.

### Jensen Huang — Current Camp: BULL

**Title/Org:** CEO, Nvidia

**What they actually said:** "This notion that the software industry is in decline and being replaced by AI [is] the most illogical thing in the world." And: "All of these tools that we use today, whether it's Cadence or Synopsys or ServiceNow or SAP, these tools exist for a fundamentally good reason. These agentic AI will be intelligent software that uses these tools on our behalf and help us be more productive."

**Source:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/26/nvidia-jensen-huang-gpu-ai-threat-software-companies-saas-earnings-chips.html (February 26, 2026)

**Is this directly about SaaS procurement vs. build?** YES

**If NO or PARTIALLY — what is it actually about?** N/A. Huang's statement directly addresses the thesis that AI agents will replace existing SaaS tools. He explicitly names ServiceNow and SAP as tools that agents will use rather than displace. The statement directly rebuts the buy-vs-build displacement narrative.

**Conflict of interest:** Significant but genuinely ambiguous. Nvidia's GPU revenue grows whether enterprises buy SaaS (SaaS vendors invest in AI infrastructure) or build custom (enterprises run their own inference workloads). Huang made this statement on the same day Nvidia reported 73% revenue growth. His financial incentive is to sustain enterprise AI investment broadly — SaaS durability serves that interest by keeping large compute buyers active. However, unlike Benioff or Bhusri, Huang does not sell SaaS himself, so his interest in defending SaaS is indirect and not as distorting.

**Recommended camp:** BULL

**Rationale for recommendation:** The statement directly and explicitly addresses whether AI agents will displace SaaS tools, and his answer is no — agents will use them. This meets the strict criterion. The BULL assignment is correct. Conflict of interest is real but does not disqualify the statement from bearing on the procurement question.

**Confidence in recommendation:** HIGH

### Satya Nadella — Current Camp: BEAR

**Title/Org:** CEO, Microsoft

**What they actually said:** "The approach at least we're taking is, I think, the notion that business applications exist, that's probably where they'll all collapse, right?" And: "Business applications will probably all collapse in the Agent Era because they are essentially CRUD databases with a bunch of business logic." And: "You can think of agents as the new apps."

**Source:** https://podcastnotes.org/bg2-with-bill-gurley-and-brad-gerstner/satya-nadella-microsoft-ceo-on-the-future-of-ai-technology-bg2-with-bill-gurley-brad-gerstner/ (December 2024); https://www.windowscentral.com/microsoft/hey-why-do-i-need-excel-microsoft-ceo-satya-nadella-foresees-a-disruptive-agentic-ai-era-that-could-aggressively-collapse-saas-apps (January 2025)

**Is this directly about SaaS procurement vs. build?** PARTIALLY

**If NO or PARTIALLY — what is it actually about?** Nadella's "collapse" statements are primarily about software architecture — business logic moving from hardcoded apps to AI agents. This is a statement about how software will be structured, not definitively about whether enterprises will buy that software as SaaS or build it themselves. His framing of "agents as the new apps" could mean enterprises buy agent-based SaaS (Microsoft Copilot Studio, Agent 365) rather than traditional SaaS — which is still a buy, not a build. The statement is ambiguous on the procurement modality question.

**Conflict of interest:** Severe and directional. Microsoft competes directly with Salesforce (Dynamics vs. CRM), Workday (Viva vs. HCM), and ServiceNow (Copilot Studio vs. IT workflows). Nadella's "SaaS will collapse" narrative, made on a public podcast, directly serves Microsoft's competitive positioning: if enterprises believe SaaS is collapsing, they are more likely to consolidate onto Microsoft's agent platform. He also sells SaaS himself (Microsoft 365, Teams, Azure SaaS services), so his bear thesis is specifically targeted at competitors, not at the model generically. This is the most commercially weaponized statement in the corpus.

**Recommended camp:** NUANCED-CONDITIONAL

**Rationale for recommendation:** Nadella's statements are about software architecture transformation, not directly about procurement modality. His own product response (Copilot Studio, Agent 365, 80% of Fortune 500 building agents on Microsoft) suggests he believes enterprises will still buy — they will just buy from Microsoft's agent platform instead of from category SaaS vendors. The BEAR camp assignment treats his statement as a proclamation that enterprises will build rather than buy, which is not what he said. He said business logic will move to AI agents — those agents could be purchased from Microsoft. NUANCED-CONDITIONAL is more accurate: traditional SaaS application categories will be disrupted, but enterprises will still procure from platforms — specifically, he believes, from Microsoft.

**Confidence in recommendation:** HIGH

## Critical Observations for Research Integrity

**1. The largest misattributions are Amodei and Altman in BEAR camp.** Both are placed there based on AI coding capability statements. Neither directly addressed the enterprise buy-vs-build procurement question. Amodei's "AI will write 90% of code" is the clearest example of the problematic pattern identified in the audit brief: SaaS companies use AI to build better SaaS, so increased AI code generation does not imply reduced SaaS procurement.

**2. Nadella's "SaaS will collapse" quote is the most over-cited statement in the corpus.** It appears in Waves 2, 3, 4, and 6. The quote describes software architecture evolution, not enterprise procurement decisions. More importantly, Nadella's proposed replacement for SaaS is Microsoft's own agentic platform — which enterprises would still buy, just from Microsoft. His statement is better read as competitive positioning than as a buy-vs-build thesis.

**3. McDermott's bear thesis is a category-consolidation argument, not a build argument.** He argues enterprises will buy one platform (ServiceNow) instead of many SaaS tools. This is bearish for category SaaS vendors but has no bearing on whether enterprises will build vs. buy.

**4. Lutke belongs in UNCLEAR.** Every piece of evidence for his BEAR placement is about Shopify's internal operations or Shopify's own product launches. None addresses what Shopify's merchant customers or other enterprises will do about SaaS procurement. His placement in BEAR appears to be based on inference from his AI mandate memo.

**5. The three most directly on-point CEO statements for the actual research question are:** (a) Aneel Bhusri — "no vibe coding will produce ERP" (directly addresses build limitation for enterprise systems); (b) David Hsu — "35% of enterprises have replaced SaaS with custom software" (directly addresses build prevalence, but self-sourced); (c) Jensen Huang — "agents will use these tools on our behalf" (directly addresses AI-as-SaaS-user thesis). All three have severe conflict of interest, but they are the only tech CEO statements that squarely address the research question.
