# Expert Camp Assignment Audit
## SaaS Durability Research — Critical Re-examination of Camp Assignments
**Audit Date:** 2026-03-06
**Auditor:** Critical Audit Agent
**Scope:** AI Researchers, Developer Influencers, Equity/Financial Analysts
**Research Question:** Will SaaS remain the majority enterprise procurement method (vs. custom build) for AI-driven applications in 2–5 years?

**Strict criterion applied throughout:** An expert belongs in Bull/Base/Bear ONLY if they made a direct, explicit statement about whether enterprises will predominantly *buy SaaS vs. build custom software* for AI-driven applications. Capability claims, valuation observations, and personal workflow statements do not qualify on their own.

## AUDIT CONCLUSIONS

**1. The bear camp for AI researchers is substantially over-populated.**
Of the eight AI researchers audited (Karpathy, LeCun, Hinton, METR, Marcus, Sutskever, Amodei, Hassabis), none made a direct enterprise procurement statement. Five are currently assigned to Bear or the Nuanced/Conditional group used to support the bear thesis. Critically, LeCun, Sutskever, METR, and Covello — all currently coded as supporting or being adjacent to the bear case — actually constrain the bear thesis. Their arguments (LLM architectural limitations, generalization failure, empirical productivity slowdown, AI ROI skepticism) make enterprise custom-build programs harder, not easier, to execute.

**2. The directional error on LeCun, Sutskever, METR, and Covello is the most significant finding.**
All four argue or demonstrate that current AI is less capable than the bear case requires. Under the strict criterion, none belongs near the Bear camp. Their arguments support SaaS durability. This is the specific error the audit instructions anticipated: "LeCun criticizing LLMs supports the BULL case, not BEAR."

**3. The equity analyst bear camp is the most validly assigned.**
Ader (KeyBanc) and Thill (Jefferies) made genuine direct procurement-direction statements through formal Underweight ratings citing seat count pressure and structural licensing disruption. Snider's newspaper analogy is partially valid. Covello, Mourad, JPMorgan, Singh, and Weiss are over-attributed — they made valuation, ROI, labor market, or equity return arguments that do not constitute procurement statements.

**4. Developer influencer assignments are mostly over-attributed.**
Pieter Levels (Bear) and Simon Willison (Base) made no enterprise procurement statements. DHH's Bear assignment was valid in 2023 but his January 2026 update materially softens his position. Guillermo Rauch's "kingdoms collapse" is misread as a custom-build thesis when it is actually a SaaS-adaptation thesis.

**5. The net effect of correcting these assignments is a significant rightward shift** (toward Bull/Base) among researchers and equity analysts, primarily because the methodological skeptics — the people most carefully studying AI's actual limitations — were incorrectly coded as supporting the bear case.
