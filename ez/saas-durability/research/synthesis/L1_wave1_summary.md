# L1 Wave 1 Summary — Evidence Brief for Scenario Construction
## SaaS Durability Question: Will SaaS Remain the Dominant Enterprise Procurement Method for AI-Driven Applications in 2–5 Years?

**Synthesis Layer:** 1 (Wave 1 Foundation)
**Date Produced:** 2026-03-06
**Source Files:** F01–F08 (Wave 1)
**Purpose:** Evidence brief for scenario construction by downstream synthesis agents. Not a literature review.

---

## 1. Wave Theme

Wave 1 establishes that the enterprise SaaS market is large, entrenched, and still growing in absolute dollar terms — but is undergoing a structural fracture that separates the question of category survival from the question of category dominance. The overarching story is one of simultaneous expansion and erosion: global SaaS spending is forecast to grow from $318 billion in 2025 to $576 billion by 2029 even as agentic coding tools cross the threshold at which custom-built alternatives become economically rational for a non-trivial subset of enterprise use cases. The February 2026 "SaaSpocalypse" — a $1+ trillion software equity sell-off — revealed how quickly market sentiment can shift, even when the underlying revenue data does not yet confirm a structural decline. Every historical parallel (cloud vs. on-premise, open source vs. commercial, low-code vs. professional dev) shows that disruptions of this type take 10–15 years to reach majority displacement in the segments they ultimately dominate, and that incumbents with deep data gravity and switching costs consistently absorb or outlast challengers. The wave's evidence therefore supports a base case of managed disruption rather than rapid collapse — but leaves credible room for both a bull case (SaaS adapts and captures AI demand) and a bear case (agentic custom-build crosses an inflection that erodes market share faster than historical precedents would predict).

## 2. Key Evidence for Each Scenario

### Bull Case (SaaS Remains Dominant, 60%+ Market Share in 5 Years)

- [Global SaaS spending is projected to grow from $318 billion in 2025 to $576 billion by 2029](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) (Forrester), demonstrating that the overall software spend category is expanding rather than contracting — even under current AI disruption pressure.

- [Cloud application services (SaaS) spending reached $250.8 billion in 2024 and is forecast to reach $299.1 billion in 2025, a 19.2% year-over-year increase](https://www.channel-impact.com/gartner-worldwide-public-cloud-end-user-spending-to-total-723-billion-in-2025/) (Gartner via Channel Impact), with business software spending growing 14.7% in 2026 to more than $1.4 trillion — indicating structural demand acceleration, not contraction.

- [76% of AI use cases in enterprises are now purchased rather than built internally in 2025, a complete reversal from 2024's 47%/53% build/buy split](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/) (Menlo Ventures, survey of 495 U.S. enterprise AI decision-makers), suggesting that as AI becomes strategically critical, enterprises are defaulting back to commercial solutions, not away from them.

- [Every major enterprise software disruption since 2000 — cloud, open source, low-code, mobile — took 10–15 years to reach majority market share](https://cloudwars.com/cloud-wars-minute/microsoft-sap-oracle-cloud-at-least-50-of-revenue/) (Cloud Wars), and categories with deep data integration and switching costs (ERP, HR of record, financial systems) consistently resisted displacement across every disruption wave, providing the strongest historical precedent for SaaS durability.

- [Gartner projects 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, or inadequate risk controls](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027), and McKinsey's historical research found large IT projects run 45% over budget and deliver 56% less value than predicted — both constraining the build alternative's viability as a SaaS replacement at scale.

- [74% of companies above $5 billion in revenue bundle SaaS into multi-year capital expenditure plans, treating licenses like servers](https://www.tropicapp.io/glossary/saas-procurement-predictions-for-2026) (Gartner survey, cited in Tropic 2026 report), and enterprise NRR at 118% confirms that large enterprises are expanding, not contracting, within existing SaaS relationships.

### Base Case (SaaS Stays Majority but Declines, 45–60% Market Share)

- [35% of enterprises surveyed have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom-built alternative, and 78% plan to build more custom tools in 2026](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026) (Retool, n=817 enterprise builders, February 2026), documenting that SaaS displacement by agentic custom builds is already underway at meaningful scale — not just theoretical.

- [Deloitte predicts by 2030, 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed within larger agent ecosystems](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html), and [by 2030, at least 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will shift toward usage-, agent-, or outcome-based pricing](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html) — indicating structural displacement in the long tail of point solutions without eliminating the core SaaS category.

- [Seat-based pricing has declined from 21% to 15% of SaaS market share in just one year](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/) (Forrester), and [IDC predicts by 2028, pure seat-based pricing will be obsolete, with 70% of software vendors refactoring pricing strategies](https://www.idc.com/resource-center/blog/is-saas-dead-rethinking-the-future-of-software-in-the-age-of-ai/), meaning SaaS model transformation is priced-in even by the market's most optimistic forecasters.

- [Gartner identifies AI-native development platforms as a Top Strategic Technology Trend for 2026 and states they "hold the potential to replace off-the-shelf SaaS software with custom-built alternatives,"](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/7046398) while simultaneously projecting that by 2030, AI-native platforms will result in 80% of organizations evolving large engineering teams into smaller AI-augmented ones — a structural compression of the professional development cost that historically protected SaaS economics.

- [Approximately $2 trillion in market capitalization evaporated from the software sector between January 15 and February 14, 2026, triggered by AI agent innovation](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-12-the-saaspocalypse-arrives-why-the-software-sector-is-facing-a-brutal-reckoning), and multiple SaaS companies reported slowing growth in Q4 2025 as customers reduced software seats due to AI-enhanced productivity — providing early but contested empirical evidence of share erosion.

- [The METR task-horizon research documents AI task-completion time doubling every 4–7 months](https://metr.org/blog/2025-03-19-measuring-ai-ability-to-complete-long-tasks/), with frontier models at a 1-hour task horizon as of early 2026 (Claude 3.7 Sonnet); if this trend persists, models capable of completing multi-week software projects autonomously could emerge by 2028–2029, within the 2–5 year window — creating a technical foundation for base-case erosion that no prior disruption wave possessed.

### Bear Case (SaaS Significantly Eroded, <45% Market Share)

- [Agentic coding tools are documented as cutting development costs by 70–95%, with conservative analysis suggesting 50% reduction in human engineering effort](https://belitsoft.com/agentic-ai-coding), and Anthropic's 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report documents 30–79% faster development cycles, [Rakuten achieving 99.9% accuracy on 12.5M-line codebase modifications in 7 autonomous hours, TELUS saving 500,000 hours, and Zapier deploying agents at 97% adoption](https://resources.anthropic.com/2026-agentic-coding-trends-report) — demonstrating real production-scale displacement economics.

- [Dario Amodei (Anthropic) projects AI will write 90% of code within organizations he works with, with significant disruption in entry-level work in 2–5 years](https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/anthropic-ceo-predicts-ai-will-take-over-coding-in-12-months/488533); [FutureSearch aggregate forecasts for superhuman coder milestone range from 2027 (Nikola Jurkovic optimistic) to 2032–2033 (FutureSearch aggregate)](https://ai-2027.com/research/timelines-forecast), spanning the entire 2–5 year window with material probability of the milestone arriving within it.

- [AI-native application spend growth of 393% year-over-year among large enterprises, with AI being the fastest-growing application category in 2025 expanding by 181% in app count within enterprise portfolios](https://zylo.com/reports/2026-saas-management-index/) (Zylo 2026 SaaS Management Index), suggesting that the structural shift toward AI-native procurement is already occurring at pace that could reshape the market's category composition rapidly.

- [Klarna eliminated over 1,200 SaaS services, consolidated to a homegrown knowledge stack, and expects $40 million in annual cost savings](https://www.silicon.eu/ai-strategy-klarna-eliminates-1200-saas-services-17458.html); and Shopify CEO Tobi Lütke mandated that employees justify any task that cannot be completed using AI before requesting additional resources, with AI competency made part of performance reviews — these are leading-indicator corporate behavior changes that signal the build-over-buy mentality spreading at executive mandate level.

- [SWE-bench Verified top agents now resolve 77–79% of real-world GitHub issues (Sonar Foundation Agent at 79.2% as of February 2026)](https://www.sonarsource.com/company/press-releases/sonar-claims-top-spot-on-swe-bench-leaderboard/), representing a dramatic capability advance from single-digit scores in 2023 — if quality improvements track with benchmark improvements, the enterprise-grade reliability threshold required for SaaS displacement could arrive within the 2–5 year window.

- [Forrester declares "SaaS as we know it is dead" and identifies horizontal point-solution SaaS vendors with low switching costs and non-embedded workflows as facing structural challenges that "won't scale beyond their current customer base"](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/), while Bain & Company articulates a specific "AI Cannibalizes SaaS" scenario for standardized, observable workflows where incumbents must proactively replace SaaS activity or face displacement by AI entrants.

## 5. Critical Caveats for Synthesis

The following data quality issues, conflicts, and gaps must be flagged for downstream synthesis agents.

**Caveat 1: The Klarna Case Is Compromised as Primary Evidence**
The Klarna "SaaS is dead" anchor case is materially undermined. [CX Today reported Klarna replaced Salesforce and Workday with alternative SaaS applications, not with AI](https://www.cxtoday.com/crm/klarna-didnt-replace-salesforce-it-replaced-them-with-alternative-saas-apps/). [Siemiatkowski personally corrected the narrative on X](https://www.silicon.eu/ai-strategy-klarna-eliminates-1200-saas-services-17458.html). Any scenario that relies heavily on the Klarna case as proof of SaaS displacement must treat it with extreme caution. The $2 million Salesforce savings figure is documented; the "SaaS replaced by AI" framing is not.

**Caveat 2: Benchmark Performance vs. Production Reality Gap**
SWE-bench Verified scores (77–79%) and real-world production performance diverge sharply. [One independent study found Devin completed only 3 of 20 assigned real-world tasks (15% completion rate)](https://trickle.so/blog/devin-ai-review), versus Devin's benchmark figures. [SWE-bench Pro scores for the same frontier models drop to 14–23%](https://scale.com/leaderboard/swe_bench_pro_public) on previously unseen codebases. Any scenario argument that uses benchmark figures to project SaaS displacement must account for the production-to-benchmark gap.

**Caveat 3: The Retool Survey Has a Sampling Bias**
[The Retool survey (n=817 enterprise builders)](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026) — the primary source for the "35% have already replaced a SaaS tool" statistic — was conducted among Retool's own customer base of "enterprise builders," i.e., people who already use a build platform. This self-selects for pro-build sentiment. This does not make the finding wrong, but it should not be extrapolated to the full enterprise market without discounting.

**Caveat 4: METR Trend Extrapolation Risk**
The METR task-horizon doubling-every-4-7-months trend is the most cited evidence for aggressive bear-case timelines. However, [METR itself notes a 10x measurement error in doubling time shifts arrival-date forecasts by only approximately 2 years](https://metr.org/blog/2025-03-19-measuring-ai-ability-to-complete-long-tasks/), but also that the trend's persistence is not guaranteed. The SWE-bench methodology difference produces a shorter apparent doubling time. Synthesis agents should treat the METR trajectory as directionally reliable but with wide uncertainty bands.

**Caveat 5: Conflicting Gartner Signals**
Gartner is the source of mutually tension-producing predictions within this wave: (a) 40% of enterprise apps will feature AI agents by end of 2026; (b) 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027; (c) agentic AI is at Peak of Inflated Expectations on the 2025 Hype Cycle; (d) in the best case, agentic AI could drive 30% of enterprise application software revenue by 2035. These are all from Gartner press releases within 12 months and should not be treated as a coherent forecast — they represent different analysts and scenarios.

**Caveat 6: Market Cap Sell-Off Is Not Revenue Evidence**
The "SaaSpocalypse" ($1–2 trillion in equity losses, February 2026) is real as a market event but is explicitly characterized by J.P. Morgan as "[broken logic](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/02/17/jp-morgan-research-says-broken-logic-is-driving-th/)" disconnected from underlying revenue trends. [Software spending growth forecasts remain at 14.7% for 2026](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-02-03-gartner-forecasts-worldwide-it-spending-to-grow-10-point-8-percent-in-2026-totaling-6-point-15-trillion-dollars). Synthesis agents should treat the equity sell-off as a sentiment indicator, not a revenue indicator.

**Caveat 7: "SaaS Pricing Dead" vs. "SaaS Category Dead" Conflation**
The "SaaS is dead" narrative conflates two separable claims: per-seat pricing disruption (broad analyst consensus; IDC projects 70% of vendors refactor by 2028) and SaaS category decline (contested by all major revenue forecasts showing absolute growth). Scenario construction must keep these distinct. The bear case for pricing disruption is defensible; the bear case for category extinction within 5 years is not supported by the weight of Wave 1 evidence.

**Caveat 8: Shadow IT AI Expansion Complicates the Category Definition**
[61.3% of all discovered enterprise applications are shadow IT (Torii 2026)](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/24/3243646/0/en/Torii-2026-Benchmark-Report-AI-Isn-t-Consolidating-SaaS-It-s-Expanding-Shadow-IT.html), with AI-native tools being the fastest-growing unmanaged category. Whether AI-native shadow apps are classified as "SaaS" (if they are commercial products) or "custom build" (if agent-generated) will significantly affect how market share shifts are measured. The taxonomy of what counts as SaaS vs. build is itself evolving.

## 6. Wave Verdict

Wave 1 (Foundation) points toward a base case as the probability-weighted outcome — SaaS maintains majority but declining market share in the 45–60% range within 5 years — with the bull case significantly supported by historical precedent and current spending trajectories, and the bear case anchored to real but as-yet-unvalidated technical assumptions. The bull case is strongest in the segments with highest data gravity, network effects, and regulatory moats (ERP, financial systems, HR of record, healthcare vertical SaaS), where every prior disruption wave produced absorption rather than displacement. The bear case is most credible in the long tail of horizontal, point-solution, seat-based SaaS with narrowly-scoped workflows and low switching costs — a segment that Forrester, Deloitte, and Gartner each independently identify as structurally exposed. The critical swing variable is the pace at which agentic coding tools cross the enterprise-grade quality threshold (security, compliance, context reliability) that would make custom building the rational default for a broader class of enterprise use cases. As of early 2026, that threshold has not been crossed — [62% of AI-generated code contains security vulnerabilities](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2025/07/09/understanding-security-risks-in-ai-generated-code), [only 21% of enterprises have a mature governance model for autonomous agents](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/about/press-room/state-of-ai-report-2026.html), and [developer trust in AI accuracy has fallen from 40% to 29% year-over-year](https://stackoverflow.blog/2025/12/29/developers-remain-willing-but-reluctant-to-use-ai-the-2025-developer-survey-results-are-here/). The probability distribution from Wave 1 evidence suggests: Bull (60%+ share) ~30%, Base (45–60%) ~50%, Bear (<45%) ~20% — with the bear case upside risk substantially higher than consensus market pricing reflected pre-SaaSpocalypse.
