---

**Synthesis note:** The table reveals a consistent bifurcation: displacement risk maps almost perfectly onto the absence or presence of three structural moats — regulatory certification requirements, proprietary data gravity, and cross-enterprise network effects. Categories lacking all three (standalone BI, workflow automation, mid-market CRM, customer support point-tools) are already experiencing documented replacement; categories with even one strong moat (ERP's implementation complexity, healthcare vertical's HIPAA certification cycle, security SaaS's expanding threat surface) remain structurally protected within the 5-year window. The aggregate "SaaS is durable" verdict holds because the high-spend, low-displacement categories (platform SaaS, regulated vertical SaaS, financial systems, security) collectively represent the majority of enterprise SaaS expenditure — but the long tail of point-solution SaaS, which constitutes the largest number of *products* in any enterprise portfolio, faces genuine and accelerating erosion.
