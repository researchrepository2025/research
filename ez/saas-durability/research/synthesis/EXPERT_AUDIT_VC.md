# VC and Investor Expert Audit — Camp Assignment Review
## SaaS Durability Research Project
**Auditor:** Claude Code (claude-sonnet-4-6) | **Date:** 2026-03-06
**Research Question:** Will SaaS remain the majority enterprise procurement method (vs. custom build) for AI-driven applications in 2–5 years?

**Strict criterion applied:** An expert belongs in Bull/Base/Bear ONLY if they made a direct, explicit statement about whether enterprises will predominantly *buy SaaS vs. build custom software* for AI-driven applications. Statements about AI capability trajectories, coding productivity, market sizing, pricing model evolution, or general software industry disruption do NOT qualify unless the expert explicitly connected those statements to the buy-vs-build enterprise procurement question.

## Audit Summary and Key Findings

### Scale of Over-Attribution
Of 39 VC/investor experts audited, **21 (54%) have recommended camp changes** under the strict criterion. The majority of changes are from assigned camps to UNCLEAR.

### Pattern of Errors

**1. Pricing model arguments misattributed as procurement arguments (most common error)**
Multiple experts (Casado, Tavel, Grady, Rampell) are placed in camps based on statements about *how* enterprises will pay for software (per-seat vs. outcome-based), not *whether* they will buy or build it. Pricing model evolution is entirely compatible with continued vendor purchasing; it is not a buy-vs-build statement.

**2. TAM expansion arguments misattributed as displacement arguments**
Rampell's "software eats labor" and Smith's "software eats services" are TAM expansion theses — AI software capturing the labor/services market. This is not an argument that enterprises will build rather than buy; SaaS vendors can themselves capture labor TAM. These are in the same category as the Dario Amodei coding example flagged in the brief.

**3. Investment thesis conflated with procurement thesis**
Bravo (PE acquisitions), Levy (fund launch), Huang (portfolio companies), Vishria (portfolio growth metrics), Murphy/Tully/Ranum (VC sector allocation) are commenting on what investors should bet on — not on what enterprises will decide in the buy-vs-build choice.

**4. AI capability statements without procurement connection**
Sinofsky ("more software than ever"), Amble (organizational restructuring), Buhler (agent economy infrastructure), Sonya Huang (AGI timelines) are making capability or industry structure predictions that are orthogonal to the procurement modality question.
