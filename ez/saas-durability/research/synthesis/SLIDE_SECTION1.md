**CATEGORY-DEPENDENT** *(firms whose position is: SaaS outcome varies by software category)*

> These are the most analytically rigorous frameworks in the corpus. All converge on the BASE scenario's structural architecture: SaaS survives where compliance, data gravity, or mission-criticality creates moats; build/displacement gains where point-solution, workflow-adjacent, or commodity software is in scope.

- McKinsey (Jeremy Schneider, Senior Partner) — Make-or-Buy Matrix: build where genuine competitive differentiation exists; buy where speed, cost control, or proven reliability matter more; principled conditional that defaults toward buy for most enterprise capabilities [[https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-ai-centric-imperative-navigating-the-next-software-frontier](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-ai-centric-imperative-navigating-the-next-software-frontier)]
- Gartner (Denis Torii, VP Analyst) — "Buy, Build, Blend" framework: explicitly rejects binary outcome; enterprises will compose heterogeneous portfolios rather than making wholesale procurement-modality shifts [[https://www.agilepoint.com/blog-posts/future-of-enterprise-application](https://www.agilepoint.com/blog-posts/future-of-enterprise-application)]
- Bain (Chuck Whitten, Partner) — "Heterogeneous landscape" thesis: compliance/platform SaaS durable; horizontal point-solutions (HubSpot list-building, Monday.com task boards) explicitly named as AI-feasible substitution targets [[https://www.bain.com/insights/the-great-debate-will-agentic-ai-kill-saas/](https://www.bain.com/insights/the-great-debate-will-agentic-ai-kill-saas/)]
- Forrester (Kate Leggett + Charles Betz, both VP Principal Analysts) — System-of-record SaaS durable; buy wins where regulatory complexity is high; horizontal point-solutions face structural exposure — the body text of Leggett's "SaaS As We Know It Is Dead" projects $576B in SaaS spending by 2029 despite its headline [[https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/saas-as-we-know-it-is-dead-how-to-survive-the-saas-pocalypse/)]
- Insight Partners (Ryan Hinkle, Managing Director) — "Filing cabinet" vs. "systems of action": low-workflow-dependency SaaS (data storage, standalone BI) vulnerable to replacement; mission-critical, workflow-embedded SaaS retains buyers [[https://www.insightpartners.com/ideas/2026-investor-predictions/](https://www.insightpartners.com/ideas/2026-investor-predictions/)]
- a16z (Santiago Rodriguez) — "Frontend tool" SaaS loses (UI-first, low workflow integration); "value-delivering" SaaS wins (genuine outcome delivery, not wrapper UX) [[https://a16z.com/leaders-gainers-and-unexpected-winners-in-the-enterprise-ai-arms-race/](https://a16z.com/leaders-gainers-and-unexpected-winners-in-the-enterprise-ai-arms-race/)]
- Deloitte (Ayo Odusote, Software and Platforms Leader) — "Many enterprises will ultimately balance both approaches rather than choosing one exclusively"; market is "becoming more hybrid"; vendors must "earn renewal, not extract it" [[https://www.cio.com/article/4131904/saas-isnt-dead-the-market-is-just-becoming-more-hybrid.html](https://www.cio.com/article/4131904/saas-isnt-dead-the-market-is-just-becoming-more-hybrid.html)]
