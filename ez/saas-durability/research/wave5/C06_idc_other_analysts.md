# C06: IDC & Other Analyst Firms — Enterprise SaaS Durability and AI's Impact on Software Spending

**Research Wave:** 5
**Scope:** IDC, VC-analyst hybrid firms (Redpoint, Battery), PitchBook, CB Insights, Bloomberg Intelligence, S&P Capital IQ
**Excluded per scope:** Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey, Bain, Deloitte, BCG, Accenture
**Sources:** 2025–2026 only
**Audience:** C-suite

---

## Executive Summary

The analyst consensus through early 2026 is not that enterprise SaaS is dying — it is that SaaS is being re-priced and restructured. IDC projects worldwide AI solution spending to reach $632 billion by 2028, with software spending growing 14% in 2025 alone, the fastest rate since 1996. Yet simultaneously, median SaaS revenue multiples have compressed below 5x as seat-based pricing erodes under AI-driven headcount reduction. The divergence among analysts is sharpest on durability: IDC sees aggregate software spend accelerating; VC-adjacent analysts at Redpoint and Battery see a bifurcation where differentiated, data-moated SaaS platforms survive while undifferentiated point solutions face displacement. PitchBook data shows AI capturing 65% of all VC deal value in 2025, signaling where capital has already cast its durability vote.

## 7. Consensus and Divergence Among Analyst Firms

### Points of Consensus

[FACT]
All surveyed sources — IDC, Redpoint, Battery, PitchBook — agree that 2026 is the year AI moves from experimentation to ROI justification in enterprise software.

[FACT]
IDC, PitchBook, and VC-adjacent analysts all document that AI infrastructure (compute, data center, model training) is absorbing a disproportionate share of new enterprise technology budget relative to application-layer SaaS.

[FACT]
IDC and Battery Ventures both identify agentic AI as the mechanism by which back-office SaaS functions (ERP, finance, procurement) face the most immediate structural disruption in 2026.

[FACT]
IDC (SaaS CAGR 16.5% through 2027) and Fortune Business Insights ($1.25 trillion by 2034) both project long-term aggregate SaaS market growth — in direct tension with near-term multiple compression data from public markets.

### Points of Divergence

[FACT]
IDC aggregate forecasts show software spending accelerating (14% growth in 2025, 10% in 2026). Public-market analyst data shows SaaS equity multiples collapsing below 5x in early 2026. These two data sets are not mutually exclusive but represent spending volume vs. valuation divergence.

[FACT]
Battery Ventures argues "durable companies will look more like SaaS companies of old with AI superpowers" — asserting SaaS model persistence. AI2.work analysis of Wall Street positioning argues seat-based SaaS revenue faces a "90% reduction" in AI-agent-heavy deployments — asserting model disruption. These represent the sharpest analyst divergence in the dataset.

[FACT]
PitchBook documents 65% of VC deal value flowing to AI (2025), implying private capital has largely redirected away from pure SaaS. IDC enterprise software forecasts do not yet reflect this reallocation in aggregate spending figures, creating a leading-indicator / lagging-indicator gap between private market signals and public enterprise budget data.
