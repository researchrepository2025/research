# C02: Bain & Company — Enterprise SaaS Durability and the Build-vs-Buy Equation in the AI Era

**Research Wave:** 5
**Scope:** Bain & Company published positions, 2025–2026
**Audience:** C-suite
**Compiled:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Bain & Company's 2025–2026 published research presents a nuanced, non-binary position on SaaS durability: enterprise software is under structural pressure from agentic AI, but extinction is not the base case. Bain's named partners argue that SaaS vendors retaining proprietary data moats, industry-specific logic, and deep workflow integrations will survive — and in some cases expand their total addressable market. On the build-vs-buy question, Bain's Technology Practice has published a decision framework with seven factors, recommending in-house builds only where cross-system, high-impact differentiation is at stake. The firm's annual Technology Report 2025 (its sixth edition) projects that up to 50% of enterprise technology spending will eventually flow toward AI agents, creating a fundamental reordering of the software stack.

## Section 4: The Build-vs-Buy Equation — Bain's Published Framework

[FACT]
Bain's "Is Agentic AI the Inflection Point for Scaling ERP Transformations?" (2025) identifies seven decision factors for the build-vs-buy-vs-partner choice: (1) Strategic relevance and differentiation potential, (2) Vendor application fit, (3) System integration ease, (4) Total cost of ownership, (5) Internal talent availability, (6) Regulatory risk, (7) Vendor lock-in implications.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025
— Florian Braun, Cyrille Vincey, Erwan Vezin, Juan Francisco (Paco) Jimenez (Partners, Bain & Company); Radhika Vyas (Practice Senior Manager)

[FACT]
Bain's published delivery model recommendations by use case: Partner with platform publishers for "standard, noncritical workflows confined within a single system"; Buy from specialists when the capability represents "a commodity and not overly complex"; Build in-house for "cross-system, high-impact processes that enable competitive differentiation."
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025

[STATISTIC]
"More than 80% of ERP transformations continue to miss budget, timeline, and value goals" — Bain Technology Maturity Assessment Benchmarking Survey, 2025, n=480
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025

[STATISTIC]
"78% of IT leaders expect at least some ERP functionality to be replaced or augmented by agentic AI over the next three years" — Bain Technology Maturity Assessment Benchmarking Survey, 2025, n=480
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain identifies five main roadblocks to scaling agentic AI in enterprise settings: (1) Organizational challenges (unclear operating models, limited skills); (2) Immature technical tooling; (3) Data quality and governance issues; (4) Strategic concerns (vendor lock-in fears, executive sponsorship gaps); (5) ROI quantification difficulty
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain's survey respondents identified three ERP process areas for earliest agentic AI adoption: (1) Procure to pay, (2) Record to report, (3) Forecast to plan
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/is-agentic-ai-the-inflection-point-for-scaling-ERP-transformations/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain's "Building the Foundation for Agentic AI" (Technology Report 2025) projects "5% to 10% of technology spending could be directed toward building foundational [agentic AI] capabilities" over 3–5 years, with "up to half of technology spending could be on agents running across the enterprise" long-term.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025
— Pascal Gautheron (Partner, Paris), Chris Bell (Partner, Sydney)

Date: 2025

## Section 5: Advisory Patterns — What Bain Is Telling Enterprise Clients

[FACT]
Bain's "Building the Foundation for Agentic AI" specifies governance architecture: "central platform teams will control the core agentic platforms" with "distributed accountability" as agents take on more decision-making authority.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain's "Will Agentic AI Disrupt SaaS?" recommends SaaS buyers conduct a buy-vs-build evaluation as one of four or more "best practices," with companies following four or more best practices realizing 12% cost efficiency versus 5% for companies following none of the practices.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain's advisory position on timing: "many companies are still building their generative AI solutions because the off-the-shelf ones are either not ready or not specific enough" but "buying may become more practical and popular as existing SaaS solutions incorporate generative AI."
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain advises enterprise software buyers to make "clear choices about whether and where to buy, build, or partner in the design of their future agent workforces" and warns that "the tech stack will need to support multiple agents to fully scale."
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[FACT]
Bain's advisory directive to incumbent SaaS vendors, published 2026: stay close to customers, understand labor efficiency problems, build AI capabilities while maintaining core business health, and implement better cost control and operational execution.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/
