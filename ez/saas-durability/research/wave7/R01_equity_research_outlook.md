# R01: Wall Street Equity Research — SaaS Sector 2–5 Year Outlook

**Research Wave:** Wave 7 — Equity Research Consensus
**Date Compiled:** March 6, 2026
**Scope:** Major bank SaaS coverage, bull/bear analyst camps, agentic AI modeling, key watched metrics, and notable analyst calls. Valuation analysis and company-specific financials are excluded (see R02 and R04).

---

## Executive Summary

Wall Street equity research on the SaaS sector entered 2026 in a state of acute divergence — a condition without clear precedent in the software analyst community. Between February 2 and February 5, 2026, an estimated $1 trillion in software market capitalization was erased as Anthropic released its Claude Cowork product, triggering a localized bear market the trading desk at Jefferies immediately termed the "SaaSpocalypse." ([FinancialContent, Feb 2026](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-11-saaspocalypse-2026-why-wall-street-is-slashing-software-valuations-and-turning-cautious-on-debt)) The S&P North American Software Index posted its worst monthly contraction since the 2008 financial crisis, while the iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF (IGV) fell more than 23% year-to-date by late February 2026. ([buildy.ai, Feb 2026](https://www.buildy.ai/news/wall-street-says-the-saas-software-business-model-is-dead-agentic-ai-killed-it)) However, the analyst community did not reach consensus: Goldman Sachs strategist Ben Snider compared the potential trajectory to the multi-decade newspaper stock collapse, while JPMorgan simultaneously argued the selloff reflected "broken logic" and represented an overshoot. ([The Motley Fool, Feb 2026](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/02/17/jp-morgan-research-says-broken-logic-is-driving-th/)) Beneath the market turbulence, foundational metrics — Gartner forecasting 14.7% software spending growth in 2026, NRR holding at a sector median of 101%, and 72% of SaaS M&A deals referencing AI positioning — indicate that the structural demand story remains intact even as the seat-based business model faces its most serious challenge in two decades.

## Section 3: Bull vs. Bear Research Camps

### Bear Camp

**Ben Snider**, Strategist, Goldman Sachs — Drew an explicit parallel to the newspaper industry's 95% stock decline between 2002 and 2009, warning the selloff may be "the end of the beginning" of a structural decline, not its end. Argued near-term earnings beats are "in many cases insufficient to disprove the long-term downside risk." ([TheStreet, February 2026](https://www.thestreet.com/investing/stocks/goldman-sachs-signals-grim-shift-as-software-stocks-bounce))

**Jim Covello**, Head of Global Equity Research, Goldman Sachs — Consistently skeptical of AI's monetization trajectory, warning the $1 trillion investment in GenAI may not pay off. Argued efficiency gains from AI would be "arbitraged away" as competitors adopt the same tools. Report titled "Gen AI: Too Much Spend, Too Little Benefit?" represents one of Wall Street's highest-profile bearish calls on AI's enterprise software impact. ([Goldman Sachs, 2024](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/goldman-sachs-exchanges/a-skeptical-look-at-ai-investment) [PRE-2025])

**Jackson Ader**, Lead Software Analyst, KeyBanc — Issued a rare Underweight on ServiceNow in January 2026, flagging "seat count pressure" as the core structural risk. Cited "worrying trends in IT back-office employment data" as a leading indicator of AI-driven demand compression. ([Yahoo Finance, January 2026](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/servicenow-now-stock-downgraded-structural-090321518.html))

**Brent Thill**, Lead Software Analyst, Jefferies — Published February 23, 2026 reset note calling the software industry in need of a full recalibration. Argued the seat-based licensing model faces structural disruption from task-replacing AI, not just productivity enhancement. Identified a subset of "winners" but maintained that the majority of SaaS firms face sustained earnings pressure. ([FinancialContent, February 2026](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-23-the-saaspocalypse-deepens-jefferies-downgrades-workday-and-docusign-as-ai-disrupts-the-app-software-sector))

**Bain & Company Research Team** (David Crawford, Chris McLaughlin, Purna Doddapaneni, Greg Fiore) — Identified five possible SaaS workflow scenarios ranging from AI enhancement to full AI cannibalization of SaaS. Found gross retention holding at ~90% but net revenue retention "stalled." Concluded AI could automate 30–50% of activity across business functions. ([Bain & Company, 2025](https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/))

## Section 4: How Analysts Are Incorporating Agentic Coding Into Models

[FACT]
The "seat-based subscription" model — where per-user licensing drives ARR — is the primary mechanism through which analysts model agentic AI's revenue impact. Enterprises have begun reporting during contract renewals that deployments of AI agents can perform the work of 500-license configurations with only 50 licenses.

Date: February 23, 2026

[FACT]
Bain & Company's framework for assessing SaaS vulnerability to agentic AI uses six indicators: task structure and repetition, risk of error, contextual knowledge dependency, data availability and structure, process variability and exceptions, and human workflow and user interface dependency.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[QUOTE]
Bain & Company: "Within three years, routine rules-based digital tasks could shift from 'human plus app' to 'AI agent plus API'"
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/
Date: 2025

[STATISTIC]

## Key Takeaways

- **Wall Street is deeply divided, not aligned.** Goldman Sachs strategist Ben Snider's newspaper-decline analogy and JPMorgan's "broken logic" thesis represent genuine, irreconcilable frameworks — the resolution will be decided by 2026–2027 earnings results, not analyst debate.

- **The "SaaSpocalypse" selloff is real in market price terms but has not (yet) shown up in fundamental spending data.** The iShares software ETF (IGV) fell 30% from its September 2025 peak while Gartner simultaneously forecasted 14.7% software spending growth in 2026 to over $1.4 trillion.

- **Seat compression is the specific mechanism analysts are modeling, not SaaS elimination.** Jefferies, KeyBanc, Goldman Sachs, and Bain all identify the per-user licensing model — not SaaS itself — as the primary disruption vector. Companies with consumption-based, outcome-based, or proprietary-data moats are systematically rated as "winners" across firms.

- **NRR is the single most-watched metric, and it is declining.** Benchmarkit data shows the sector median fell from 105% (2021) to 101% (2024), with Bain noting NRR has "stalled" even while gross retention holds at ~90%. Analysts across firms treat NRR as the leading indicator of whether AI displacement is showing up in the financials.

- **Agentic coding's enterprise penetration is still nascent.** Morgan Stanley's Sanjit Singh found that when asked about agentic AI familiarity among 150 CIOs, only four raised their hands — a data point bullish analysts use to argue the bear thesis is running well ahead of actual enterprise adoption curves.
