# R02: SaaS Valuation Trends — What Markets Signal About SaaS Durability

**Research Wave:** 7
**Date Compiled:** 2026-03-06
**Scope:** SaaS revenue multiple trends (2023–2026), AI-native vs. traditional SaaS valuation differential, public SaaS market cap trends, how markets price SaaS disruption risk, IPO and de-listing patterns, and SaaS multiples vs. historical enterprise software averages.
**Audience:** C-suite
**Source Tier Priority:** Tier 1 (named analysts), Tier 2 (Bessemer, Sapphire Ventures, Bain, PitchBook), Tier 3 (tech press)

---

## Executive Summary

Public market EV/NTM revenue multiples for SaaS companies have compressed from a peak of 18–19x in 2021 to a median of 5.1x as of December 2025, as tracked by Aventis Advisors across their longitudinal dataset of public SaaS companies. A bifurcation has opened between AI-native software (commanding 10x+ multiples in public markets) and traditional seat-based SaaS (clustering at 2–5x), with Sales and Marketing Automation hitting a floor of 1.9x EV/NTM as of October 2025. The IPO market showed selective revival in 2025 — Figma debuted at 20x forward revenue and tripled on day one — while a parallel wave of take-privates (Dayforce at $12.3B, Smartsheet at $8.4B) signals that private equity is buying SaaS assets at compressed multiples rather than exiting them. Bain & Company identified Anthropic's Claude as a specific trigger for a software stock selloff of approximately 15% from recent peaks, citing AI's capacity to automate 30–50% of activity across business functions. Markets are not pricing SaaS as dead; they are pricing a structural bifurcation between AI-leveraged and AI-threatened SaaS subsectors.

## Key Takeaways

- **Multiple compression is structural, not cyclical.** Public SaaS EV/NTM revenue has declined from an 18–19x peak in 2021 to 5.1x in December 2025 (Aventis Advisors), and the 10-year Sapphire Ventures average of 5.9x suggests the current level is close to a long-run equilibrium — not a temporary trough.

- **Markets have split SaaS into two valuation regimes.** AI-native and infrastructure-adjacent software (Data Infrastructure: 6.2x, DevOps: 5.7x, Vertical AI Apps: 5.4x) commands multiples at a 2–3x premium to AI-threatened horizontal SaaS (Sales & Marketing Automation: 1.9x, AdTech: 1.1x), per the October 2025 Multiples.vc sector data.

- **The IPO and take-private patterns point in the same direction.** Profitable, AI-differentiated SaaS can access public markets at premium valuations (Figma: 20x forward revenue, 40x oversubscribed). Meanwhile, PE firms are acquiring traditional SaaS at compressed multiples (405 enterprise SaaS PE deals in H1 2025 alone), consistent with a view that many SaaS assets need operational restructuring before they are investable at public-market prices.

- **Disruption risk is priced in leading indicators, not yet in revenue.** Gross retention remains approximately 90%+, but NRR has stalled and seat growth is decelerating (Bain). The 12.2% median revenue growth rate as of Q4 2025 (Aventis) is less than half the COVID-era 31% peak, and Bain estimates AI can automate 30–50% of activity across business functions — the market is pricing in anticipation of this transition, not its completion.

- **Budget reallocation toward AI is measurable and accelerating.** 73% of enterprise leaders funded AI/GenAI capabilities in the past 12 months; 39% funded agentic AI specifically; and Deloitte projects 75% of organizations will invest in agentic AI by end of 2026 (Deloitte Tech Value Survey). Gartner projects 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will shift to usage-, agent-, or outcome-based models by 2030 — a structural change that affects the revenue architecture of SaaS, not just its growth rate.
