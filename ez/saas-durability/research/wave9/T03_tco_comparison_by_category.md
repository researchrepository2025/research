# TCO Comparison by Enterprise Application Category: SaaS vs. Build

**Wave 9 | Research File T03 | Prepared March 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

The build-vs-buy calculus in enterprise software has shifted materially since 2024. Agentic coding tools have compressed custom development timelines from months to days, directly attacking one of SaaS's strongest historical advantages: speed to value. A February 2026 survey of 817 enterprise builders found that 35% of organizations have already replaced at least one SaaS product with a custom-built alternative, and 78% plan to accelerate that trend through 2026 ([Retool, 2026](https://retool.com/blog/ai-build-vs-buy-report-2026)). Yet the verdict is not uniform: TCO comparisons reveal sharp category-level divergence, with ERP and security/compliance remaining firmly in SaaS territory while internal tools and productivity software have crossed a meaningful break-even threshold. CRM and HCM sit in contested middle ground where scale, workflow uniqueness, and agentic development capability determine the outcome.

### CRM Category Verdict

At 500+ users, Salesforce SaaS TCO compounds to $3–5M over five years inclusive of add-ons, admin overhead, and hidden costs. A purpose-built CRM at equivalent scale carries higher initial engineering cost but eliminates per-seat fees. The break-even point shifts to build's favor at large user counts and highly differentiated workflow requirements. At sub-200 users, SaaS retains TCO advantage due to implementation speed and maintenance burden.

## Key Takeaways

- **ERP and security/compliance are structurally SaaS categories.** The regulatory update obligation, failure rate (55–75% for ERP), and cost-overrun history ($175K–$400K+ for on-prem SIEM) make build economically irrational for most enterprises. Agentic coding does not change this; it lowers code-generation cost but cannot substitute for vendor investment in compliance content and threat intelligence.

- **Internal tools have passed the TCO break-even threshold.** The Retool 2026 data (35% replacement already underway, 78% planning more) is corroborated by the AI agent build cost data ($50K–$180K for task and RAG agents) vs. accumulating per-user SaaS subscriptions across dozens of point tools. The build case is no longer theoretical.

- **CRM is the most contested category.** At 500+ users, Salesforce's 2.5–4x TCO multiplier over initial implementation cost and the 40–80% license-cost overage for hidden costs create a credible financial argument for custom CRM at enterprise scale. At under 200 users, SaaS still wins on speed and maintenance burden.

- **HCM sits in a hybrid pattern, not a binary verdict.** The $15–22M five-year Workday TCO for 10,000 employees is largely non-negotiable for payroll and tax compliance, but the workflow and productivity layer on top is increasingly being built, not bought — aligning with the internal-tools shift.

- **The TCO calculus is now moving, not static.** Agentic coding tools that compress build timelines from months to days directly attack SaaS's speed-to-value moat. The 78% of enterprises planning more custom builds in 2026 represents a forward indicator of SaaS market compression in the internal-tools and CRM segments specifically.
