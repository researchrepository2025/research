# V06: VC Investment Flow — SaaS vs. AI-Native vs. Build Tools
## Enterprise SaaS Durability in the Agentic Coding Era | Wave 2 Research

**Research Date:** March 6, 2026
**Scope:** VC investment patterns 2024–2026 as revealed by funding data, valuation multiples, and named investor statements.
**Scope Exclusion:** Public market analysis (Wave 7) and individual named VC positions (V01–V05).

---

## Executive Summary

The money flow from 2024 through early 2026 tells a story with unusual clarity: capital is concentrating in AI-native companies at a speed and scale that has no modern precedent. AI captured approximately 50% of all global venture funding in 2025, up from 34% in 2024, while traditional SaaS valuation multiples compressed to decade-low levels. Within AI, agentic coding tools attracted some of the largest single-company bets in venture history — Anysphere (Cursor) alone closed a $2.3 billion Series D at a $29.3 billion valuation in November 2025. The divergence between what VCs say publicly ("SaaS still matters") and what they fund ("AI or nothing") has never been wider. The data below catalogs the verifiable facts underlying that divergence.

## Key Takeaways

1. AI captured ~50% of all global venture capital in 2025 ($211–238B depending on methodology), up from 34% in 2024. SaaS captured ~33% of funding in Q3 2025 but is structurally losing deal count.

2. Agentic coding is the most capitalized sub-sector within AI: Cursor ($2.3B Series D at $29.3B), Poolside ($2B at $12B), Reflection AI ($2B at $8B), Lovable ($330M at $6.6B), and Cognition ($400M at $10.2B) all closed mega-rounds in a five-month window (July–November 2025).

3. The valuation multiple gap is structurally wide: AI-native companies trade at 25–38x revenue at the median; traditional SaaS trades at 5–7x. This 4–5x spread has not narrowed.

4. Named VC investors at Menlo Ventures, Sapphire Ventures, Insight Partners, Grove Ventures, DTCP, and Team8 are on record predicting that horizontal SaaS without AI differentiation will lose VC share in 2026.

5. What VCs fund contradicts the "SaaS is fine" narrative: deal count hit a 10-year low, capital is concentrating in top-decile AI bets, and the categories named as losing investment (workflow management, CRM, HR, marketing, analytics) overlap directly with the horizontal SaaS core.

6. Both Bessemer and a16z have published frameworks (Shooting Stars, systems of action) that redefine the investable SaaS company as one that executes work autonomously — not one that gives workers tools to execute work manually.
