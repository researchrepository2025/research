# V02: Top-Tier VC Perspectives on Enterprise SaaS Durability
## Research Wave 2 — Build-vs-Buy Shift in the Agentic Coding Era

**Scope:** Sequoia Capital, Benchmark Capital, Accel, Lightspeed Venture Partners
**Exclusions:** a16z (V01), growth equity (V03), public market investors (V05)
**Date Range:** 2025–2026 sources only; older sources marked [PRE-2025]
**Compiled:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Top-tier VC firms are not converging on a single thesis about enterprise SaaS durability. A clear fault line has emerged: firms with deep legacy SaaS portfolios (Sequoia's Alfred Lin, Benchmark's Chetan Puttagunta) are making affirmative bets that vertical SaaS survives and evolves into vertical AI, while partners from those same firms who focus on new AI-native investments (Benchmark's Sarah Tavel, Eric Vishria) are arguing that the fundamental SaaS business model—seat-based, productivity-improving software—is being structurally displaced by outcome-based "selling the work" models. Accel and Lightspeed are positioned closer to the disruption camp in their public communications while continuing to back both AI-native and traditional enterprise software companies.

Investment patterns tell the clearest story: all four firms sharply increased deal velocity in AI-native enterprise applications in 2024–2025, while traditional SaaS deal counts have stagnated. The divergence between stated positions and portfolio construction is narrow—these firms are largely walking their talk.
