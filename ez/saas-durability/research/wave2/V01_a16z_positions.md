# Andreessen Horowitz (a16z): Positions on SaaS Durability in the Agentic Coding Era

**Research Wave:** 2 | **File:** V01_a16z_positions.md | **Date Compiled:** 2026-03-06
**Audience:** C-Suite | **Scope:** a16z positions only, 2024–2026

---

## Executive Summary

Andreessen Horowitz has executed a coordinated, multi-voice thesis shift between 2024 and early 2026: the firm's partners no longer defend traditional per-seat SaaS as the dominant paradigm and instead argue that "software is eating labor"—a deliberate successor framing to Marc Andreessen's 2011 "software is eating the world." The firm is not predicting the death of software, but the death of a specific business model within software. The central argument is that the $300 billion global SaaS market is being superseded by a $13 trillion labor market now addressable by AI-native software, and that incumbents anchored to per-seat pricing will lose pricing power to outcome-based AI challengers. Simultaneously, at least two named a16z partners have issued direct pushback against overreach: one calling the "vibe code everything" thesis "flat wrong," another arguing that "the reported death of third party apps is greatly exaggerated." The firm's investment behavior confirms the thesis—$1.7 billion of its January 2026 $15 billion raise is earmarked specifically for AI applications, with portfolio companies including AI-native SaaS replacements in ERP, procurement, and financial services.

---

## Key Takeaways

- [FACT] a16z's official thesis as of 2025–2026: "Software is eating labor," not merely software categories. The labor market ($13T U.S. alone) is the new TAM, versus the $300B global SaaS market.
- [FACT] a16z explicitly argues the per-seat pricing model is obsolete and is being replaced by outcome-based pricing.
- [FACT] a16z partner Anish Acharya stated directly in February 2026 that "the theory that we'll vibe code everything is 'flat wrong.'"
- [FACT] a16z partner Steven Sinofsky published a February 2026 rebuttal to "death of software" narratives, predicting more software will be built than ever before.
- [FACT] a16z partner Sarah Wang stated in December 2025 that "the system of record will finally start to lose primacy" in 2026.
- [FACT] a16z's January 2026 enterprise survey (100 CIOs) found enterprises expect ~75% LLM budget growth over the next year.
- [FACT] a16z raised $15B across six funds in January 2026, allocating $1.7B to AI apps—the investment thesis made capital.

**Martin Casado**, General Partner (Infrastructure Practice Lead), Andreessen Horowitz — On a podcast with Metronome CEO Scott Woody, Casado argued that "in the cloud era, value scaled with the number of users accessing a shared system" but "in the AI era, value shifts to the work the software performs on your behalf." ## Section 8: Internal Tensions in the a16z Position

[FACT]
a16z does not hold a single monolithic view. As of early 2026, named partners hold materially different emphasis positions:

- **Disruption camp** (Rampell, George/Rodriguez, Andrusko, Wang): SaaS per-seat model is dying; labor market is the new TAM; systems of record will lose primacy.
- **Durability camp** (Sinofsky, Acharya, Wang/Kahl/Xu jointly): Software demand will increase; vibe-coding replacements of ERP/CRM are not economically rational; third-party apps are not dead.
- **Infrastructure/neutral camp** (Jennifer Li, Appenzeller/Y. Li, Zhou et al.): General agents cannot yet navigate complex enterprise environments out-of-the-box; specialized agents will win; 6–18 months to materially better capability.

[FACT]
Sarah Wang appears in both camps: co-authoring the "system of record will lose primacy" statement (Big Ideas 2026) while also co-authoring "the reported death of third party apps is greatly exaggerated" (January 2026 survey).
