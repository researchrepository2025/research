**Source Priority:** Tier 1 (named investors, consulting firms, equity research) > Tier 2 (analyst firms, surveys) > Tier 3 (tech press). 2025–2026 sources only unless tagged [PRE-2025].

## Executive Summary

A cohort of prominent investors — spanning Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz, Bessemer Venture Partners, and Insight Partners — has mounted a structured counter-narrative to the "SaaSocalypse" thesis that erased roughly $1 trillion in software market capitalization between mid-January and mid-February 2026. Their argument is not that nothing changes: it is that the competitive moats that made SaaS dominant (data, workflow lock-in, ecosystem, brand, and network effects) are durable or actively widening, that the overall enterprise software spending pool continues to expand, and that agentic AI accelerates the transition toward outcome-based models rather than destroying the category. Venture funding data corroborates continued conviction: SaaS startups on Carta raised $9.3 billion in Q3 2025 alone, up 13% year-over-year.

The bullish investors cluster into three camps:

1. **"Moats Persist or Evolve"** — Sequoia (Alfred Lin), a16z (Alex Immerman, Santiago Rodriguez), Bessemer (Byron Deeter)
2. **"Agentic SaaS Replaces Static SaaS"** — Insight Partners (Ryan Hinkle, Praveen Akkiraju, John Wolff)
3. **"Market Expands Despite Share Shift"** — Accel (Philippe Botteri), Sequoia (Pat Grady, Sonya Huang)

## Section 5: How Bullish Investors Address the Agentic Coding Challenge

[FACT]
Bain & Company acknowledges that AI could automate between 30–50% of activity across enterprise functions, identifying high-volume, digitized, lower-stakes activities (customer support, code writing, inquiry handling) as most vulnerable. However, Bain frames this as "seat compression" — a margin headwind — rather than category destruction.
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/
Date: 2025–2026

[FACT]
Bain identifies the specific threat mode: "Rather than 500 seats, customers might purchase 450 and deploy agents for marginal usage, fundamentally altering per-seat economics without immediately destroying business viability."
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/why-saas-stocks-have-dropped-and-what-it-signals-for-softwares-next-chapter/
Date: 2025–2026

[FACT]
