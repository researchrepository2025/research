# V07: Nuanced Investor Positions on SaaS Durability in the Agentic Coding Era

**Research Wave:** 2 — Expert Opinion Mapping
**Focus:** Conditional and "it depends on X" investor views — the middle ground between strong bull (V05) and strong bear (V04) camps
**Scope:** 2025–2026 sources only; older sources marked [PRE-2025]
**Word Count:** ~2,100

---

## Executive Summary

A distinct cluster of investors, equity analysts, and consulting firms occupies the middle ground between SaaS obituarists and unconditional bulls. Their shared structure: SaaS durability is not a binary question but a function of at least four variables — software category (system of record vs. point solution), enterprise complexity (deterministic vs. probabilistic workflows), customer segment (large enterprise vs. SMB), and the pace at which incumbent vendors can shift from seat-based to outcome-based pricing. This camp does not predict SaaS's death; it predicts differentiation so severe that the category label becomes analytically useless.

## Section 1: The Core "It Depends" Framework

The most cited organizing principle across nuanced positions is a two-dimensional assessment of any given SaaS workflow: how much of it can AI automate, and how deeply has AI already penetrated the incumbent's architecture. [FACT]
Five workflow scenarios for SaaS in an agentic AI era: (1) No AI, (2) AI enhances SaaS, (3) Spending compresses, (4) AI outshines SaaS, (5) AI cannibalizes SaaS.
— David Crawford, Chris McLaughlin, Purna Doddapaneni, Greg Fiore (Partners, Bain & Company)
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/
Date: September 23, 2025

[QUOTE]
"Disruption is mandatory. Obsolescence is optional."
— Bain & Company (Crawford, McLaughlin, Doddapaneni, Fiore)
URL: https://www.bain.com/insights/will-agentic-ai-disrupt-saas-technology-report-2025/
Date: September 23, 2025

[QUOTE]

## Section 5: The "Selling Work, Not Software" Condition

**Sarah Tavel**, General Partner, Benchmark Capital — introduced a structural condition for SaaS survival that is neither bull nor bear: companies that shift from charging for software access to charging for delivered work outcomes occupy a defensible middle position.

[QUOTE]
"It's not about selling 'virtual employees'. It's about unbundling the specific tasks or outcomes that employees have traditionally performed."
— Sarah Tavel, General Partner, Benchmark Capital
URL: https://www.sarahtavel.com/p/a-few-sell-work-not-software-updated
Date: January 19, 2024 [PRE-2025]

[STATISTIC]
Tavel's market sizing conditional: "When you sell a 95% productivity improvement, you are able to charge substantially more for your service than you would have with software on a per seat basis, and the same end markets could be 10–50x larger than traditional software-model expectations."
— Sarah Tavel, Benchmark Capital
URL: https://www.sarahtavel.com/p/a-few-sell-work-not-software-updated
Date: January 19, 2024 [PRE-2025]

[FACT]
Tavel's condition for viability of "selling work" model: the market must already have established, focused outsourced service providers operating internationally — indicating the work type is economically viable at scale and AI can displace human operators.
URL: https://www.sarahtavel.com/p/a-few-sell-work-not-software-updated
Date: January 19, 2024 [PRE-2025]

[FACT]
Tavel identifies a structural risk to the "sell work" model: customers push for 30% discounts upon learning AI has automated the work. "Pretty much every big customer was showing up to their renewal conversations saying basically the same thing: 'I know you're using AI to automate your work. So I'll need a 30% discount.'"
— Sarah Tavel, Benchmark Capital (via search result synthesis)
URL: https://www.flexcapital.com/post/benchmark-capital-gp-sarah-tavel-how-ai-will-transform-saas-business-models

## Section 8: Key Conditions Aggregated — The "It Depends On" Matrix

The following conditions appear across two or more named sources as the variables that determine SaaS durability:

[FACT]
Condition 1 — Software Category: Systems of record (ERP, CRM, HRIS, compliance, billing) are identified as durable by Bain, Jamin Ball, Jared Sleeper, Javier Pérez, Pancras Beekenkamp, and Deloitte. Point-product and UI-first tools identified as vulnerable by the same group.

[FACT]
Condition 2 — Workflow Determinism: Deterministic, rules-based, regulated workflows (healthcare, finance, legal) are protected; probabilistic, judgment-light workflows (customer support, task boards, content) are at risk. Sources: UncoverAlpha (February 2, 2026), Bain (September 23, 2025), Edelweiss Capital (January 25, 2026).

[FACT]
Condition 3 — Customer Segment: Large enterprise SaaS with deep workflow integration and procurement lock-in shows higher durability than SMB-focused seat-based tools. Sources: a16z (January 30, 2026), Edelweiss Capital (January 25, 2026).

[FACT]
Condition 4 — Pricing Model Transition: SaaS vendors that successfully shift from seat-based to outcome-based or usage-based pricing are viewed as conditionally durable. Vendors that cannot execute this transition face structural compression. Sources: Bain (September 23, 2025), IDC (December 2, 2025), Deloitte (November 18, 2025), Brad Sills / BofA (February 2026).

[FACT]
Condition 5 — Pace of AI Agent Maturation vs. Incumbent Adaptation: The race between how quickly third-party apps can build domain-specific depth vs. how quickly foundation model capabilities improve determines which incumbents survive in any given category. Source: a16z (January 30, 2026).

[FACT]
Condition 6 — Timeline: Short-term (2025–2026): seat compression is real but implementation is nascent (6% production deployment). Long-term (2030+): 35% of point-product SaaS replaced; 40% of spend shifts to outcome-based pricing. Sources: Deloitte (November 18, 2025), Gartner (August 2025, June 2025), BofA (February 2026).

## Key Takeaways

1. No named nuanced investor predicts uniform SaaS survival or uniform SaaS death. Every position in this camp is explicitly conditional.

2. The single most agreed-upon variable is software category: system of record vs. point solution. This distinction appears in every major nuanced source reviewed.

3. The sell-off of February 2026 (approximately $1–2 trillion in SaaS market cap erased) has been characterized by BofA (Arya, Sills) and Bain as indiscriminate — pricing in uniform disruption where selective disruption is the more defensible thesis.

4. Timeline matters: the nuanced camp generally holds that full structural disruption is a 5-year-plus process, not a 2026 event, creating a conditional window during which incumbents can adapt.

5. The "sell work, not software" pricing transition (Tavel's framework, now widely cited) is itself a conditional: it creates new upside for SaaS vendors who own the data and the workflow, but introduces margin compression risk when enterprise buyers realize AI has automated the work.

6. Gartner's dual-sided finding is representative of the nuanced camp's posture: 40% of enterprise apps will embed agents by 2026 (integration, not replacement) AND 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 (hype exceeds execution capacity).
