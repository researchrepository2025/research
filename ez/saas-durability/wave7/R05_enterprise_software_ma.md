# R05: Enterprise Software M&A Patterns — Signals for SaaS Durability

**Research Question:** What do enterprise software M&A patterns reveal about industry expectations for SaaS durability?

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Scope:** 2024–2026 | Excludes: VC investment (V06), individual vendor strategies (Wave 4)

---

## Executive Summary

Enterprise software M&A reached unprecedented scale in 2025, with 2,698 SaaS transactions recorded — a 28% year-over-year increase and the highest annual volume on record — signaling sustained industry confidence in the SaaS model rather than retreat from it. Private equity firms, led by Thoma Bravo's $12.3 billion take-private of Dayforce and a $34.4 billion fund haul, are committing historically large pools of capital to software buyouts, betting on recurring-revenue durability under private ownership. Strategic SaaS vendors simultaneously executed high-value AI acquisitions — ServiceNow's $2.85 billion purchase of Moveworks, Salesforce's $8 billion Informatica deal, and Workday's $1.1 billion Sana acquisition — embedding AI capabilities directly into incumbent platforms rather than ceding ground to new entrants. The valuation structure has bifurcated sharply: AI-positioned, high-NRR companies command 8x–22x revenue multiples while sub-scale peers trade at 3x–5x, reflecting selective but not wholesale devaluation of SaaS assets. Taken together, M&A patterns indicate SaaS is consolidating and AI-integrating rather than declining, with acquirers treating enterprise SaaS as durable infrastructure worth large capital commitments.

## Key Takeaways

- [STATISTIC] SaaS M&A volume hit an all-time record in 2025 (2,698 transactions, up 28% YoY), while total software deal value rose 66% to $420 billion, directly contradicting a narrative of SaaS category decline at the M&A activity level. (Source: Software Equity Group 2026 Annual Report, https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/software-equity-groups-2026-saas-report-highlights-record-deal-volume-and)

- [FACT] Private equity capital commitment to SaaS is at a historical apex: Thoma Bravo alone closed a $34.4 billion fund haul (including a $24.3 billion fund described as the largest tech-focused fund in history) and executed its largest ever deal ($12.3 billion for Dayforce), with $1.2 trillion in global PE dry powder actively targeting software assets. (Source: CorpDev.Org, https://www.corpdev.org/2025/06/05/thoma-bravo-secures-34-4-billion-buyout-fund-haul-decoding-the-software-sectors-new-power-play/)

- [FACT] AI capability acquisition by incumbent SaaS vendors — exemplified by ServiceNow's $2.85 billion Moveworks deal, Salesforce's $8 billion Informatica purchase, and Workday's $1.1 billion Sana acquisition — shows established SaaS platforms are absorbing rather than being displaced by AI-native competitors. (Sources: Fortune https://fortune.com/2025/03/10/servicenow-buys-ai-agent-company-moveworks-for-2-85-billion/; Salesforce https://www.salesforce.com/news/press-releases/2025/05/27/salesforce-signs-definitive-agreement-to-acquire-informatica/; Workday https://newsroom.workday.com/2025-11-04-Workday-Completes-Acquisition-of-Sana)

- [FACT] Valuation bifurcation is accelerating: AI-positioned, high-NRR SaaS companies command 8x–22x revenue multiples while sub-scale peers trade at 3x–5x, indicating the market is differentiating between durable-platform and commoditized-point-solution SaaS rather than marking the entire category down. (Source: Software Equity Group Q3 2025, https://softwareequity.com/blog/saas-ma-deal-volume-and-valuations/)

- [FACT] The acqui-hire wave — with Big Tech spending over $40 billion in 2024–2025 on talent-focused deals structured as licensing transactions — demonstrates that even the most sophisticated buyers treat AI engineering talent as scarce enough to acquire through SaaS and AI startup M&A, a pattern that attracted FTC and DOJ scrutiny for potential anticompetitive effects by mid-2025. (Source: Clera Insights https://www.getclera.com/blog/acqui-hires-big-tech-talent-acquisition; Financial Content https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-1-23-the-great-talent-heist-how-2024s-ai-acqui-hires-rewrote-the-rules-of-big-tech-m-and-a)
