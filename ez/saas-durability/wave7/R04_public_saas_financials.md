# R04: Public SaaS Company Financial Metrics — Sector Health and Vulnerability

**Research Question:** What do public SaaS company financial metrics reveal about the sector's health and vulnerability?

---

## Executive Summary

Public SaaS company financial metrics from 2024–2025 reveal a sector in simultaneous structural maturation and growth deceleration. Median revenue growth for public SaaS companies has declined from a pandemic peak of 36% (Q1 2022) to approximately 15% by Q1 2025, while gross margins and free cash flow metrics have improved materially as companies exited the growth-at-all-costs era. Net revenue retention rates have softened across the cohort — even among marquee names — signaling contraction pressure and slower expansion from existing customers, though headline rates remain above 100% for most publicly traded companies. On the demand side, Gartner projects worldwide SaaS end-user spending approaching $300 billion in 2025, up from approximately $247 billion in 2024, indicating absolute dollar growth continues even as per-company growth rates compress. The most significant vulnerability signal is a 29% year-over-year decline in aggregate net new ARR added across the cloud software universe in Q1 2025, which may reflect the earliest measurable pressure from AI-era budget reallocation, tightened enterprise procurement, or both.

## Key Takeaways

- **Revenue growth has durably decelerated.** Public SaaS median revenue growth fell from 36% (Q1 2022) to 15% (Q1 2025). The BVP Cloud Index average sits at 18.8%, masking a bifurcated cohort where large incumbents (Salesforce at ~9%) drag the median toward single digits while usage-based platforms (Snowflake at 29%, Datadog at 26%) sustain higher rates.

- **Profitability has structurally improved.** Median public SaaS EBITDA margins reached 9.3% in Q3 2025 after years below zero, and FCF margins are rising across flagship names. This represents a genuine regime shift from growth-at-all-costs to efficient growth, not merely a cyclical adjustment.

- **NRR is compressing but has not collapsed.** Even marquee SaaS companies (HubSpot from 115% peak to ~102%; Okta declining sequentially) show NRR softening. ChartMogul data from 2,500+ companies confirms achieving ≥100% NRR is harder across all ARR segments than it was in 2021–2022. The mechanism is slowing expansion, not accelerating churn.

- **CAC efficiency is worsening at the median.** The median new CAC ratio rose 14% in 2024 to $2.00, and the median payback period extended from 14 to 18 months year-over-year. The gap between top-quartile performers ($1.00 ratio) and bottom-quartile ($2.82) is widening, suggesting the efficiency crisis is concentrated, not universal.

- **No measurable evidence of AI-driven SaaS displacement in reported financials as of March 2026.** The 29% year-over-year decline in aggregate net new ARR in Q1 2025 is the strongest bearish signal, but it precedes widespread agentic AI deployment and may reflect macro budget tightening, pricing model changes, or competitive saturation rather than structural replacement. Seat compression effects, if they materialize at scale, have not yet appeared in enterprise churn or NRR data for any publicly traded SaaS company reviewed.
