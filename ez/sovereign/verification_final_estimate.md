# Final Sovereign AI Revenue Estimation (Feb 2025–Jan 2026)

## Executive Summary

Confirmed hyperscaler sovereign AI contract revenue (AWS, Azure, GCP) for the Feb 2025–Jan 2026 window totals approximately $2.5B across the US and EU/UK, with estimated ranges of $5.8B–$9.5B when incorporating analyst-derived extrapolations for undisclosed-value deals and untracked contract activity. The data reveals a fundamental measurement problem: none of the three hyperscalers report sovereign/government revenue as a separate line item, and the majority of verified deals either have undisclosed values or represent contract ceilings rather than annual revenue run-rates. GCP leads in confirmed EU sovereign deal value due to the UK MoD contract; Azure leads in confirmed US deal value due to the GSA OneGov agreement; AWS has the largest single US contract but the bulk of its sovereign activity is in infrastructure investment (capex) rather than disclosed contract revenue.

## Estimation Methodology Notes

### How Confirmed Totals Were Calculated

1. **Started with the Verified Deal Master List** (35 entries) from Agent A cross-reference output, supplemented by 31 single-source findings where values were available.
2. **Filtered for hyperscaler relevance**: Included only AWS, Azure/Microsoft, and GCP/Google Cloud deals. Excluded non-hyperscaler-specific deals (Salesforce Army IDIQ, HPE DISA DCI, Palantir Maven, Anduril SOCOM, Airbus Defence France) from the vendor-specific revenue table.
3. **Separated capex from revenue**: AWS $50B US infrastructure investment, AWS EUR 7.8B European Sovereign Cloud investment, Microsoft PLN 2.8B Poland investment, and Oracle $1B Netherlands investment are capital expenditures, NOT contract revenue. These are excluded from revenue totals.
4. **Annualized multi-year contracts**: For contracts spanning multiple years, annual revenue was calculated by dividing total value by contract duration. Where a contract was active within the window, one year's attribution was included.
5. **Applied ceiling vs. obligation distinction**: Contract ceilings (e.g., CDAO $200M, GSA OneGov $1B) represent maximum potential spend, not guaranteed revenue. Confirmed totals use ceiling values as upper bounds where actual obligations are not disclosed.
6. **Currency conversions applied**:
   - EUR to USD: 1.09 (average rate, Feb 2025–Jan 2026 period)
   - GBP to USD: 1.27 (average rate, Feb 2025–Jan 2026 period)
   - PLN to USD: 0.25 (average rate)

### How Estimated Ranges Were Derived

**Lower bound** of estimated ranges = confirmed totals + conservative attribution for undisclosed-value deals (using minimum plausible contract values based on scope).

**Upper bound** of estimated ranges uses three inputs:

1. **Gartner North America Sovereign Cloud IaaS estimate**: $16B (2026). Hyperscaler share (AWS+Azure+GCP) at 62-66% of cloud market = $9.9–10.6B. US government portion estimated at 15-25% of North American sovereign cloud = $1.5–2.7B per hyperscaler-addressable sovereign market. Split across 3 vendors using market share ratios (AWS 29-32%, Azure 20-22%, GCP 11-13%).

2. **Deltek Federal Cloud Computing forecast**: $19.6B (FY2026) total federal cloud. AI/sovereign-specific share estimated at 15-20% = $2.9–3.9B. Split across vendors using government cloud market share (which differs from commercial — Azure historically leads in government due to JEDI/JWCC legacy).

3. **Gartner European Sovereign Cloud IaaS estimate**: $6.9B (2025) growing to $12.6B (2026). Hyperscaler share in Europe is lower due to local sovereignty providers. Estimated at 40-50% for Big Three combined = $2.8–6.3B. Split using European cloud market share ratios.

**Analyst reports used for range estimation:**
- Gartner Sovereign Cloud IaaS Spending Forecast (Feb 2026): $80B worldwide, $16B North America, $6.9–12.6B Europe
- Deltek GovWin Federal Cloud Computing Market (2026): $19.6B US federal cloud
- Synergy Research/Canalys/Omdia cloud market share data (Q2-Q3 2025): AWS 29-32%, Azure 20-22%, GCP 11-13%
- Bank of America sovereign AI as 10-15% of $450-500B AI infrastructure market

### Reasonableness Check Against Government Spending Forecasts

**US Government:**
- NITRD FY2025 AI R&D budget request: $3.3B — our confirmed US hyperscaler total ($2.13B) is 65% of this, which is plausible given that not all AI R&D spending flows to hyperscalers and our total includes multi-year contract attributions.
- Deltek FY2026 Federal AI market forecast: $2.7B (excluding IC) — our confirmed US total aligns well with this.
- DoD explicit AI budget: $1.8B (FY2025) — our confirmed DoD-specific hyperscaler contracts total ~$1.1B, which is 61% of the DoD AI budget. Reasonable given that some DoD AI spending goes to non-hyperscaler vendors (Palantir, Anduril, Scale AI, etc.).
- Trump FY2025 AI legislation: $1B+ appropriated — consistent with our deal-level findings.
- JWCC cumulative: $3B+ as of Aug 2025 — our JWCC-attributed revenue across vendors (~$255M incremental in-window) represents a fraction of cumulative program, consistent with ongoing task order issuance.

**EU/UK:**
- Gartner European sovereign cloud IaaS: $6.9B (2025) — our confirmed EU hyperscaler total ($0.80B) represents only 12% of this. This is expected because: (a) 80% of sovereign cloud spend goes to net-new or non-hyperscaler solutions per Gartner, (b) most EU sovereign deals have undisclosed values, and (c) the $6.9B includes all IaaS (not just government contracts from the three US hyperscalers).
- EC Cloud III DPS tender: EUR 180M — this is a framework for future procurement, not yet awarded to specific hyperscalers. Consistent with our approach of not counting un-awarded frameworks.
- UK government AI allocation: GBP 2B through 2029/30 — the UK MoD GCP deal (~$540M) represents a significant portion, consistent with UK government prioritizing defense AI.

## Known Gaps & Caveats

### Data Gaps

1. **No hyperscaler reports sovereign/government revenue separately.** All three embed government revenue within consolidated cloud segments ($128.7B AWS, $75B+ Azure, $70B+ GCP annual run-rate). Our bottom-up deal sum captures only a fraction of actual government cloud revenue.

2. **Intelligence Community spending is invisible.** NIP ($73.4B FY2025) and MIP ($28.2B FY2025) budgets are not broken out by vendor or AI-specificity. Classified contracts with hyperscalers (e.g., AWS C2S legacy, Azure/GCP classified regions) are not in any public database.

3. **JWCC vendor-specific breakout does not exist publicly.** The program has issued $3B+ in task orders, but the split among AWS, Azure, GCP, and Oracle is not disclosed. Our 30/30/25/15 split assumption is directional only.

4. **Most EU sovereign deals have undisclosed values.** Microsoft's Bleu (France), Delos (Germany), Belgium Copilot, and UK agreement all lack disclosed contract values. Google's Bundeswehr and NATO deals are described qualitatively ("hundreds of millions," "multi-million") but not precisely.

5. **Contract ceilings vs. annual spend.** Many US government contracts report ceilings (maximum authorized spend over the life of the contract), not actual annual expenditure. Our confirmed totals include some ceiling-based figures that overstate in-window revenue.

6. **The GSA OneGov savings figure is not revenue.** Microsoft's $3.1B "year-1 savings" and $6B "3-year savings" represent discounts, not expenditure. We estimated underlying revenue, but this is a significant source of uncertainty for the Azure US figure.

### Where Confidence Is Lowest

- **Azure EU**: Zero confirmed revenue. All Microsoft EU sovereign deals have undisclosed values. The estimated range ($0.7–1.5B) is derived entirely from analyst market sizing and Microsoft's known EU sovereign partnerships. **Confidence: Very Low.**
- **Azure US revenue from OneGov**: The $885M attribution is an estimate derived from a savings figure, not a disclosed revenue number. The actual in-scope sovereign AI revenue could be significantly higher or lower.
- **GCP US CDAO ceiling**: The $200M is a contract ceiling; actual obligations may be a fraction of this.

### Classified/Undisclosed Deals That May Affect Totals

- AWS GovCloud and Secret/Top Secret regions serve IC and DoD classified workloads. Revenue is not publicly reported.
- Microsoft Azure Government Secret and Top Secret regions (IL6+) serve similar classified workloads.
- GCP Distributed Cloud (air-gapped) deployments for classified environments.
- The AWS $50B infrastructure investment commitment for US government AI suggests anticipated sovereign revenue well above our confirmed figures.

### Currency Conversion Assumptions

- EUR/USD: 1.09 (average for the estimation window)
- GBP/USD: 1.27 (average for the estimation window)
- PLN/USD: 0.25 (average for the estimation window)
- Small fluctuations (+/- 3%) in exchange rates during the window do not materially affect totals.
