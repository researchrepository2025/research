# Oracle AI Platform - Success Metrics
## Date Range: August 1, 2025 - January 13, 2026

### Executive Summary

Oracle demonstrated exceptional growth in its AI platform business during this period, driven by massive cloud infrastructure contracts with major AI companies. The company's Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) surged from $455 billion in Q1 FY26 to $523 billion in Q2 FY26, representing 438% year-over-year growth. Cloud Infrastructure revenue grew 68% to $4.1 billion in Q2, with multicloud database revenue growing at 817%. Major customer wins included a $300 billion partnership with OpenAI and a reported $20 billion deal with Meta Platforms. Oracle expanded its GPU capacity to over 96,000 NVIDIA Grace Blackwell GB200 units and committed to $50 billion in capital expenditures for FY 2026 to build AI infrastructure.

## Financial Performance

### Q1 FY26 Results (Quarter Ended August 31, 2025)
**Reported: September 9, 2025**

**Total Revenue: $14.9 billion**
- Up 12% in USD, 11% in constant currency
- Source: [Oracle Q1 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q1fy26-earnings-release-2025-09-09/)

**Cloud Revenue: $7.2 billion**
- Up 28% in USD, 27% in constant currency
- Source: [Oracle Q1 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q1fy26-earnings-release-2025-09-09/)

**Cloud Infrastructure (IaaS) Revenue: $3.3 billion**
- Up 55% in USD, 54% in constant currency
- Source: [Oracle Q1 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q1fy26-earnings-release-2025-09-09/)

**Remaining Performance Obligations: $455 billion**
- Up 359% year-over-year
- CEO Safra Catz stated: "We signed four multi-billion-dollar contracts with three different customers in Q1…The scale of our recent RPO growth enables us to make a large upward revision"

**MultiCloud Database Revenue Growth: 1,529%**
- Chairman Larry Ellison stated: "MultiCloud database revenue from Amazon, Google and Microsoft grew at the incredible rate of 1,529% in Q1…Oracle AI Cloud Infrastructure and the Oracle MultiCloud AI Database will both contribute to dramatically increasing cloud demand."
- Source: [Oracle Q1 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q1fy26-earnings-release-2025-09-09/)

**Non-GAAP EPS: $1.47**
- Up 6% in USD
- Source: [Oracle Q1 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q1fy26-earnings-release-2025-09-09/)

## Infrastructure Expansion

### GPU Capacity and Hardware

**NVIDIA Grace Blackwell GB200 Deployment**
- Delivered more than 96,000 NVIDIA Grace Blackwell GB200 units as of Q2 FY26
- Oracle delivered 50% more GPU capacity in Q2 versus Q1
- Source: [Futurum Group Analysis](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/oracle-q2-fy-2026-cloud-grows-capex-rises-for-ai-buildout/)

**NVIDIA GPU Purchase Commitment for OpenAI**
- Oracle will purchase around 400,000 of Nvidia's GB200s to lease to OpenAI
- This is substantially larger than earlier plans of 64,000 GB200s by end of 2026
- Source: [Data Center Dynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/oracle-to-spend-40bn-on-nvidia-chips-for-openai-texas-data-center/)

**OCI Supercluster Scale**
- Oracle's GB200 NVL72 systems and OCI Superclusters are capable of scaling up to 131,072 NVIDIA GPUs
- Source: [Oracle Blog](https://blogs.oracle.com/cloud-infrastructure/post/leader-2025-idc-marketscape-worldwide-public-iaas)

**AMD GPU Partnership**
- Oracle and AMD will deploy 50,000 AMD Instinct MI450 Series GPUs on OCI starting in Q3 2026
- Larry Ellison stated (December 10, 2025): "We are now committed to a policy of chip neutrality where we work closely with all our CPU and GPU suppliers."
- Source: [CIO Dive](https://www.ciodive.com/news/oracle-amd-nvidia-AI-infrastructure/802772/)
- Source: [Oracle Q2 FY26 Earnings](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/q2fy26-earnings-release-2025-12-10/)

### Multicloud Database Strategy

**Database@AWS General Availability: July 2025**
- Oracle and AWS jointly announced the general availability of Oracle Database@AWS in North America
- Joins existing Oracle Database@Azure and Oracle Database@Google Cloud services
- Source: [Oracle Blog](https://blogs.oracle.com/cloud-infrastructure/oracle-aws-reinvent-2025)

**Multicloud Database Growth Trajectory**

- Q1 FY26: 1,529% growth rate
- Q2 FY26: 817% growth rate
- Chairman Larry Ellison suggested the multicloud business alone might reach $20 billion in database revenue within five years
